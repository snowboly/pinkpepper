# Great Britain Food Import Guide Design

## Goal

Publish a practical, search-focused article for first-time commercial importers bringing food into England, Scotland, or Wales from countries outside the EU.

## Content

The article will preserve the submitted step-by-step structure but correct unsupported or inaccurate claims. It will separate the import routes for products of animal origin, animal by-products, composite products, high-risk food and feed of non-animal origin, plants and plant products, fishery products, organic food, and standard food not subject to SPS controls.

Dynamic requirements such as risk categories, check frequencies, approved countries, establishments, safeguards, certificate models, and BCP designations will point readers to the live official service rather than state a value that can become stale.

## Visuals

The hero will be a relevant port, container, or food-logistics photograph selected through the Pexels API. The body will contain two local SVG diagrams in PinkPepper colours:

1. A seven-stage import workflow.
2. A product-route decision tree.

The SVGs will include concise labels, meaningful alternative text, and explanatory captions. They will not encode changing legal thresholds.

## Publication

The article will use the existing HTML-in-Markdown format and article manifest. It will be English-only initially, categorised as `Import & Export`, dated 15 June 2026, and use the slug `how-to-import-food-into-great-britain-from-non-eu-countries`.

## Verification

A focused test will verify that the manifest and article exist, that the article is limited to Great Britain, that it includes the Pexels hero and both diagrams, and that known incorrect claims from the submitted draft are absent.
