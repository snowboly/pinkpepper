# Food Safety Opening and Closing Checklist Template

## Recommended Template Title

`Food Safety Opening and Closing Checklist Template`

## Format

- Use `xlsx`
- Create two tabs:
- `Opening`
- `Closing`

This should be a repeat-use operational checklist, not a row-by-row incident log.

## Layout

Use:

- Y axis = `Check item`
- X axis = `Day of month`

Recommended sheet structure:

`Check item | Standard | 1 | 2 | 3 | ... | 31 | Notes / corrective action`

## Cell Values

Keep entries simple:

- `OK`
- `Not OK`
- `N/A`

Or use initials plus status if stronger accountability is needed:

- `JS OK`
- `JS NOK`
- `JS N/A`

## Opening Tab

Suggested rows:

- Fridge temperatures checked
- Freezer temperatures checked
- Handwash stations stocked
- Sanitiser / cleaning chemicals available
- Food-contact surfaces clean
- Equipment clean and ready
- Probe thermometer available and clean
- Allergen information available
- Deliveries area clean
- No signs of pests

## Closing Tab

Suggested rows:

- Food stored covered and labelled
- Chilled food returned to correct storage
- Waste removed
- Bins cleaned / closed
- Cleaning completed
- High-risk areas sanitised
- Temperatures recorded
- Equipment switched off if required
- Premises secured
- Issues handed over

## Useful Left-Side Columns

- `Check item`
- `Standard`

## Useful Right-Side Column

- `Notes / corrective action`

Use this for anything that failed, exceptions, or handover notes.

## Recommendation

This template should be practical first:

- prefill the checklist items
- let staff complete only the daily result cells
- keep corrective action notes short and operational

That will make it much more likely to be used consistently by cafes, restaurants, catering teams, and small food producers.
