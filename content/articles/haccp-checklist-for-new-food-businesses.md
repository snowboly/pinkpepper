<h2>HACCP Checklist for New Food Businesses</h2>
<p>New businesses usually fail inspections for basic execution gaps: missing records, weak allergen control, or unclear responsibility during busy shifts. This checklist focuses on getting the essentials right before opening.</p>
<blockquote><strong>Audit Tip:</strong> Assign one named owner for each control area: temperatures, cleaning, allergens, and supplier checks.</blockquote>

<h3>Fast answer</h3>
<p>Before opening, complete registration, process flow mapping, hazard analysis, CCP setup, monitoring forms, training, supplier approval, and allergen controls. Then run a mock audit before service starts.</p>

<h3>UK vs EU practical differences</h3>
<ul><li>Registration and inspection workflows differ by local authority but HACCP basis is shared.</li><li>Allergen communication expectations are strict in both frameworks and need explicit procedures.</li><li>Very small low-risk setups may use simpler documentation, but controls must still be effective.</li></ul>
<h3>Compliance references</h3>
<ul><li>Regulation (EC) 852/2004 food hygiene</li><li>Regulation (EU) 1169/2011 allergen information</li></ul>
<h3>Common mistakes and fixes</h3>
<ul><li>Mistake: Opening before staff training is signed off. Fix: No food handling until induction completed.</li><li>Mistake: No approved supplier records. Fix: Create supplier list and receiving checks before first delivery.</li><li>Mistake: Allergen matrix not updated for specials. Fix: Daily menu sign-off before service.</li></ul>
<h3>What auditors ask for as evidence</h3>
<ul><li>Business registration and opening checks</li><li>Hazard analysis and CCP summary</li><li>Daily temperature, cleaning, and allergen records</li><li>Supplier approval and delivery acceptance logs</li></ul>
<h3>Enforcement-style examples</h3>
<ul><li>New operators often receive findings for missing monitoring records within the first weeks.</li><li>Inadequate allergen information can escalate quickly to formal action.</li><li>No documented corrective action is treated as loss of process control.</li></ul>
<h3>Action plan</h3>
<ol><li>Complete pre-opening HACCP file and staff induction pack.</li><li>Run a 1-day mock service with full logging.</li><li>Fix any missing controls before public launch.</li><li>Hold weekly review meetings for first 8 weeks.</li><li>Transition to monthly verification once stable.</li></ol>
<h3>Final checklist</h3>
<ul><li>Pre-opening file complete and signed</li><li>Staff training records complete</li><li>Allergen controls live and visible</li><li>Mock audit completed with corrective actions closed</li></ul>
<h3>Frequently asked questions</h3>
<h4>What fails most first inspections?</h4>
<p>Missing records, weak allergen controls, and staff unable to explain control actions.</p>
<h4>Can one checklist work for all sectors?</h4>
<p>No. Core checks are shared, but hazards differ by product, process, and service model.</p>
<h4>Should I run a mock audit?</h4>
<p>Yes. A pre-opening mock audit catches execution gaps before formal inspection.</p>
<h4>How many logs should I use?</h4>
<p>Use the minimum needed for control and traceability. Too many logs usually reduce completion quality.</p>
