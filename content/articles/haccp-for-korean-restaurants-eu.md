<p><em>Regulation (EC) No 852/2004 • Commission Notice 2022/C 355/01 • SFBB Adaptation for Korean Cuisine</em></p>

<h2>1. Regulatory Framework</h2>

<p>Article 5 of Regulation (EC) No 852/2004 requires food business operators to implement procedures based on HACCP principles. The 2022 Commission Notice (2022/C 355/01) formalises <strong>Operational Prerequisite Programmes (OPRPs)</strong>—essential controls not suited to binary critical limits—and incorporates requirements on allergen management and food safety culture introduced by Regulation (EU) 2021/382.</p>

<p>The FSA's Safer Food Better Business (SFBB) framework organises compliance around the "Four Cs": Cross-contamination, Cleaning, Chilling, and Cooking. While no dedicated Korean cuisine SFBB pack exists, operators may adapt the general catering pack supplemented with cuisine-specific hazard awareness. The flexibility provisions in EU law recognise that the nature of the activity and size of the establishment determine appropriate control measures.</p>

<h2>2. Cuisine-Specific Hazards</h2>

<h3>2.1 Marinated Meats: Bulgogi and Galbi</h3>

<p>Korean barbecue relies on pre-marinated meats (beef, pork, chicken) held in soy-based sauces with garlic, sesame oil, and sugar. Research on <em>cheyuk-pokkum</em> (spicy pan-fried pork) demonstrates that marinated meat dishes present significant microbiological hazards requiring systematic control.</p>

<p>Raw pork and marinade ingredients introduce pathogens including <em>Salmonella</em> and <em>E. coli</em>. Extended marination at ambient temperature enables pathogen proliferation. Cross-contamination risk arises from shared tongs and grill plates during tabletop cooking.</p>

<p><strong>Control measures:</strong></p>

<ul>
  <li>Marination conducted under refrigeration at ≤5°C</li>
  <li>Defrosting frozen meats at ≤5°C for maximum 12 hours</li>
  <li>Dedicated containers for raw marinated meats; never reuse marinade as sauce without boiling</li>
  <li>Separate tongs and serving utensils for raw and cooked meat handling</li>
</ul>

<h3>2.2 Banchan: Multiple Small Dishes at Ambient Temperature</h3>

<p>Banchan—the array of small side dishes served with every Korean meal—presents a distinctive food safety challenge. These items (kimchi, namul, jeon, jorim) are prepared in advance and held at ambient or variable temperatures throughout service. Research on <em>Naeng-myeun</em> and <em>Bibimbab</em> preparation identified <strong>holding on display</strong> and <strong>food handling after cooking</strong> as Critical Control Points due to significant microbial proliferation during extended ambient holding.</p>

<p>Cooked vegetables (namul) handled after cooking show elevated total plate counts. Multiple small dishes prepared in advance increase cumulative risk exposure, and frequent staff handling during replenishment introduces contamination.</p>

<p><strong>Control measures:</strong></p>

<ul>
  <li>Banchan preparation in small batches; replenish from refrigerated storage</li>
  <li>Time control: maximum 4 hours cumulative ambient exposure</li>
  <li>Dedicated tongs per banchan type; no bare-hand contact with ready-to-eat items</li>
  <li>Acidified items (kimchi, pickled vegetables) present lower risk but still require hygienic handling</li>
</ul>

<h3>2.3 Kimchi: Fermentation Safety and Cross-Contamination</h3>

<p>Kimchi safety relies on lactic acid fermentation achieving pH reduction to ≤4.6. Korean HACCP guidelines for kimchi manufacturers identify <strong>ginger as the primary source of <em>Yersinia enterocolitica</em></strong> contamination and specify disinfection protocols. While restaurant kitchens prepare smaller batches than manufacturers, the principles apply.</p>

<p><strong>Control measures:</strong></p>

<ul>
  <li>Thorough washing of cabbage and vegetables in potable water</li>
  <li>Ginger and garlic disinfection: chlorine-based sanitizer (100 ppm free residual chlorine, 10 minutes) or boiling water blanching (60 seconds)</li>
  <li>Salt concentration adequate to support lactic acid fermentation while inhibiting pathogens</li>
  <li>Fermentation containers cleaned and sanitised between batches</li>
  <li>Refrigerated storage post-fermentation; use clean utensils for service</li>
</ul>

<h3>2.4 Rice and Grain Bowls: Bibimbap and Cooling</h3>

<p>Bibimbap (mixed rice with vegetables and meat) and other rice-based dishes present <em>Bacillus cereus</em> hazards from cooked rice cooling. Research identified pre-preparation, preparation, and holding on display as CCPs requiring monitoring.</p>

<p>Spores survive cooking; slow cooling enables germination and toxin production.</p>

<h3>2.5 Cold Noodles: Naeng-myeun Temperature Control</h3>

<p>Cold noodle dishes present risk due to post-cooking handling and ambient holding. Research on <em>Naeng-myeun</em> preparation found elevated microbial counts after cooking and handling, with display holding identified as a CCP.</p>

<p><strong>Control measures:</strong></p>

<ul>
  <li>Cooked noodles rapidly chilled in ice bath</li>
  <li>Preparation using chilled equipment and refrigerated broth</li>
  <li>Assembly immediately before service; minimal holding time</li>
  <li>Strict hand hygiene and glove use during noodle handling</li>
</ul>

<h3>2.6 Raw Seafood: Hoe and Fermented Seafood</h3>

<p>Hoe (raw fish) and fermented seafood products (jeotgal, salted squid/oysters) present pathogen and parasite risks. There is no kill step.</p>

<p><strong>Control measures:</strong></p>

<ul>
  <li>Source from approved suppliers with documented HACCP systems</li>
  <li>Frozen storage for parasite destruction (species-dependent; -20°C for minimum periods)</li>
  <li>Dedicated preparation area and utensils</li>
  <li>Consumer advisory notice for raw seafood items</li>
  <li>Fermented seafood stored refrigerated; use clean utensils; prevent cross-contamination</li>
</ul>

<h2>4. Documentation and Verification</h2>

<h3>4.1 Records That Attract Scrutiny</h3>

<ul>
  <li><strong>Defrosting logs:</strong> Time and temperature ≤5°C for frozen meat thawing</li>
  <li><strong>Cooking temperature verification:</strong> Core temperature ≥75°C for meats and reheated items</li>
  <li><strong>Rice cooling logs:</strong> Time/temperature from cooking to ≤5°C within 4 hours</li>
  <li><strong>Refrigerator temperature records:</strong> Daily min/max ≤5°C for all units</li>
  <li><strong>Banchan holding records:</strong> Time tracking for ambient-held items; 4-hour discard</li>
  <li><strong>Allergen matrix:</strong> Current and verified; particular attention to soy, gluten, sesame</li>
  <li><strong>Raw seafood advisory:</strong> Documented consumer communication for hoe</li>
</ul>

<h3>4.2 Internal Verification</h3>

<p>Verification (HACCP Principle 6) requires periodic evaluation:</p>

<ul>
  <li>Weekly management walk-through observing defrosting, marination, and banchan holding practices</li>
  <li>Monthly thermometer calibration (ice-point and boiling-point methods)</li>
  <li>Quarterly documentation review</li>
  <li>Annual HACCP plan review triggered by menu changes</li>
</ul>

<p>Records should be completed contemporaneously, show actual values, and identify the responsible person.</p>

<h2>5. Food Safety Culture</h2>

<p>Regulation (EU) 2021/382 requires evidence of food safety culture: management commitment, employee awareness, open communication, and sufficient resources. For Korean restaurants:</p>

<ul>
  <li>Documented training on marination safety, defrosting protocols, and banchan time control</li>
  <li>Front-of-house training on allergen communication (soy, gluten, sesame)</li>
  <li>Clear procedures for tabletop barbecue safety (raw/cooked utensil separation)</li>
  <li>A process for employees to raise concerns without fear of reprisal</li>
</ul>

<h3>6.1 Tabletop Barbecue: A Distinctive Challenge</h3>

<p>Korean barbecue restaurants where customers cook meat at the table present unique cross-contamination risks: customers using the same tongs for raw and cooked meat, grill plates not changed between raw and ready-to-eat items, and banchan and dipping sauces contaminated by raw meat drips.</p>

<p><strong>Control measures:</strong></p>

<ul>
  <li>Provide separate colour-coded tongs for raw and cooked meat handling</li>
  <li>Staff training to monitor and replace grill plates proactively</li>
  <li>Position banchan and sauces away from grill zone</li>
  <li>Clear consumer guidance (verbal or signage)</li>
</ul>
