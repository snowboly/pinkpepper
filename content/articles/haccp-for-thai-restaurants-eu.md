<p><em>Regulation (EC) No 852/2004 • SFBB Adaptation for Thai Cuisine</em></p>

<h2>1. Regulatory Framework</h2>

<p>Article 5 of Regulation (EC) No 852/2004 requires HACCP-based procedures. The 2022 Commission Notice (2022/C 355/01) formalises <strong>Operational Prerequisite Programmes (OPRPs)</strong>—essential controls not suited to binary critical limits. For Thai kitchens, bulk rice cooling and curry paste holding are more appropriately OPRPs than CCPs.</p>

<p>The FSA's SFBB framework organises compliance around the "Four Cs": Cross-contamination, Cleaning, Chilling, and Cooking. While no dedicated Thai cuisine SFBB pack exists, operators may adapt Asian-cuisine frameworks or the general catering pack.</p>

<h2>2. Cuisine-Specific Hazards</h2>

<h3>2.1 Rice and Bacillus cereus</h3>

<p>Thai cuisine is built on rice—jasmine, sticky, noodles. Research in Thailand demonstrated <em>B. cereus</em> widely present on rice in all forms. A 2022 Pattaya outbreak investigation confirmed <em>B. cereus</em> transmission via kitchen surfaces and utensils.</p>

<p><strong>Cooling requirement:</strong> From ≥93°C to ≤5°C within 4 hours, using shallow containers (≤5 cm depth).</p>

<h3>2.2 Fermented Ingredients</h3>

<p>Nam pla (fish sauce) and kapi (shrimp paste) achieve safety through high salt and low water activity. The hazard is <strong>cross-contamination</strong>: spores transferred via utensils to ready-to-eat foods. Control requires designated spoons, refrigeration post-opening, and handwashing protocols.</p>

<h3>2.3 Som Tam and Raw Components</h3>

<p>Green papaya salad contains raw, unheated ingredients. No kill step exists. Control requires thorough washing, dedicated sanitised mortar and pestle, and refrigerated holding of prepped components.</p>

<h3>2.4 Coconut Milk</h3>

<p>Fresh and canned coconut milk support rapid bacterial growth. Curries held in bains-marie must maintain ≥63°C, verified at 2-hour intervals.</p>

<h2>3. Documentation Requirements</h2>

<p>Most scrutinised records in Thai restaurant inspections:</p>

<ul>
  <li><strong>Rice cooling logs</strong>: time/temperature from cooking to ≤5°C</li>
  <li><strong>Refrigerator records</strong>: daily min/max ≤5°C</li>
  <li><strong>Hot-hold checks</strong>: curries and soups ≥63°C at 2-hour intervals</li>
  <li><strong>Allergen matrix</strong>: current and verified against supplier specifications</li>
</ul>

<h2>4. Food Safety Culture</h2>

<p>Regulation (EU) 2021/382 amended Annex II of 852/2004 to require evidence of food safety culture: management commitment, employee awareness, open communication, and sufficient resources. For Thai restaurants, this means documented training, team briefings, and a process for raising concerns.</p>

<h2>5. Post-Brexit Context</h2>

<p>The proposed UK-EU SPS Agreement (targeted mid-2027) would create a shared SPS zone with dynamic alignment. Current hygiene requirements remain aligned. Compliance with SFBB satisfies both UK and EU expectations. Northern Ireland continues to apply EU legislation directly.</p>
