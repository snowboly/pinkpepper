<h3>Introduction</h3>

<p>Running a brunch and breakfast cafe often leads to common pitfalls, like keeping hollandaise sauce tepid in squeeze bottles or bain-maries below 63°C. These oversights can put your customers at risk and lead to significant audit failures. The consequences of improper temperature control can be severe, including foodborne illnesses that can tarnish your reputation.</p>

<blockquote><strong>Audit Tip:</strong> Always check the temperature of hollandaise sauce at the start of service and every 30 minutes thereafter. Discard any that has not been held at ≥63°C.</blockquote>

<h4>What you'll learn</h4>
<ul>
    <li>How to manage specific Critical Control Points (CCPs) for brunch menu items.</li>
    <li>Best practices for staff training on allergen communication and temperature control.</li>
    <li>Common supplier checks to ensure food safety and compliance with regulations.</li>
</ul>

<h3>What Auditors Check First</h3>
<ul>
    <li>Temperature records for hollandaise sauce and hot-held breakfast meats.</li>
    <li>Communication of runny egg policies to vulnerable customers.</li>
    <li>Storage practices for raw batter and the separation from ready-to-eat items.</li>
</ul>
<h3>Hazards Specific to Brunch and Breakfast Cafes</h3>

<p><strong>Biological Hazards:</strong></p>
<ul>
    <li><strong>Salmonella Enteritidis:</strong> Found in undercooked poached or fried eggs.</li>
    <li><strong>Listeria monocytogenes:</strong> Associated with smoked salmon and chilled ready-to-eat garnishes.</li>
    <li><strong>Staphylococcus aureus:</strong> Risk from high-touch areas during breakfast assembly and garnishing.</li>
</ul>
<p>On a busy Saturday when multiple orders for runny eggs come in, ensure you use Lion-mark or equivalent assured eggs. Lightly cooked eggs must be served immediately after cooking, while fully cooked eggs for vulnerable customers must have both yolk and white firm.</p>
<p>Maintain hollandaise sauce at ≥63°C or ≤5°C; discard if held lukewarm on the pass. Verify temperature with a probe, ensuring it remains at these thresholds.</p>

<p><strong>Chemical Hazards:</strong></p>
<ul>
    <li><strong>Allergens:</strong> Common allergens include egg, milk, gluten, and nuts.</li>
</ul>
<p>On a busy Sunday brunch when staff are assembling multiple dishes, ensure front-of-house (FOH) staff communicate allergen information clearly. Allergen declarations must be captured and displayed prominently for dishes containing eggs, milk, and gluten.</p>
<p>Ensure that all suppliers provide allergen specifications, especially for pancake mixes and syrups. Regular updates to the allergen matrix must be documented.</p>

<p><strong>Physical Hazards:</strong></p>
<ul>
    <li><strong>Foreign objects:</strong> Items like shell fragments from eggs or debris from the kitchen.</li>
</ul>
<p>On a hectic holiday weekend, when multiple cooks are cracking eggs rapidly, implement a strict protocol to inspect egg shells before use. Regularly check work surfaces for any foreign objects during service.</p>
<p>Train staff to identify and eliminate any physical hazards during food preparation and assembly. Conduct periodic checks to maintain a clean and safe workspace.</p>

<h3>Seasonal Considerations</h3>
<p>During bank holiday and weekend surges, prolonged service creates opportunities for repeated temperature abuse. Ensure hot-held breakfast meats, such as bacon and sausages, remain above 63°C from 10:00 to 15:00 with frequent probing and small-batch replenishment.</p>
<p>In summer, outdoor dining increases the risk of temperature control failure for smoked salmon and egg dishes. Keep these items at ≤5°C during service and monitor ambient conditions closely.</p>
<h3>Critical Control Points</h3>

<ul>
    <li>
        <strong>Egg Safety CCP:</strong>
        <p>Use Lion-mark or equivalent assured eggs for lightly cooked service. For vulnerable consumers, offer fully cooked eggs (yolk and white firm).</p>
        <p>Monitor egg supplier status and shell integrity during deliveries. Check every delivery and maintain a daily log.</p>
        <p>If unassured eggs are found, discard immediately and replace with compliant eggs. Inform your supplier of the breach.</p>
    </li>
    <li>
        <strong>Hollandaise Control:</strong>
        <p>Hold hollandaise sauce at ≥63°C or ≤5°C. Discard any hollandaise held between these temperatures for more than 2 hours.</p>
        <p>Use a calibrated probe thermometer to check temperatures every 30 minutes. Document findings in a temperature log.</p>
        <p>If the sauce is found tepid, discard it immediately and prepare a fresh batch, ensuring proper holding temperatures are maintained.</p>
    </li>
    <li>
        <strong>Extended Brunch Hot-Hold CCP:</strong>
        <p>Bacon and sausages must remain >63°C from 10:00-15:00. Check temperatures every 30 minutes.</p>
        <p>Use a probe thermometer for accurate readings. If the temperature drops, reheat the food to above 75°C before serving.</p>
        <p>If the hot-hold drops below 63°C for more than 15 minutes, discard the food and prepare fresh items.</p>
    </li>
    <li>
        <strong>Smoked Salmon CCP:</strong>
        <p>Maintain smoked salmon at ≤5°C. Follow strict use-by/opened-pack controls to limit Listeria growth.</p>
        <p>Use a thermometer to check the temperature upon delivery and every 2 hours during service.</p>
        <p>If salmon exceeds 5°C, discard it immediately and verify storage conditions with staff.</p>
    </li>
    <li>
        <strong>Raw Batter CCP:</strong>
        <p>Refrigerate egg-based pancake batter at ≤5°C between service bursts. Discard after 2 hours at room temperature.</p>
        <p>Use a thermometer to check batter temperature every hour. Keep a log of times and temperatures.</p>
        <p>If the batter exceeds 5°C for more than 15 minutes, discard it and prepare a new batch.</p>
    </li>
</ul>

<h3>Common Mistakes</h3>
<ul>
    <li>Mistake: Hollandaise kept tepid in squeeze bottles or bain-marie below 63°C → Fix: Always monitor temperature every 30 minutes and discard if not within range.</li>
    <li>Mistake: Runny egg policy not communicated to vulnerable customers → Fix: Clearly mark menus and inform staff to verbally communicate this to customers.</li>
    <li>Mistake: Hot breakfast meats topped up repeatedly without full container change and temperature verification → Fix: Change containers and verify temperatures every 30 minutes; do not top up.</li>
</ul>
<p>Supplier management and receiving checks play a vital role in maintaining food safety in brunch and breakfast cafes. Implementing stringent checks upon delivery helps prevent the introduction of hazards into your kitchen. This section outlines key checks for your suppliers and delivery processes.</p>

<h3>Delivery Acceptance Checklist</h3>
<ul>
    <li>Egg suppliers: Verify Lion-mark or equivalent assured scheme status and check shell integrity.</li>
    <li>Smoked salmon suppliers: Ensure delivery temperature ≤5°C and confirm robust Listeria controls.</li>
    <li>Breakfast meats: Check for proper cooking/reheating instructions and allergen specifications for sausages (gluten, sulphites).</li>
    <li>Pancake mix/syrups/toppings: Capture allergen declarations (egg, milk, gluten, nuts) and update your allergen matrix.</li>
</ul>

<h3>When to Reject a Delivery</h3>
<ul>
    <li>Eggs with cracked shells or no assured scheme certification.</li>
    <li>Smoked salmon delivered above 5°C or with no clear shelf-life guidance.</li>
    <li>Breakfast meats lacking proper cooking instructions or with visible spoilage signs.</li>
    <li>Pancake mix with missing allergen declarations or out-of-date shelf life.</li>
</ul>

<blockquote>Check the temperature of delivered products immediately. Use a calibrated probe thermometer and document findings to identify trends and potential issues.</blockquote>
<h3>Daily Monitoring Checklist</h3>
<ul>
    <li>Check and record the temperature of hollandaise: ≥63°C or ≤5°C.</li>
    <li>Verify egg safety: use Lion-mark or equivalent assured eggs for runny eggs; ensure fully cooked for vulnerable consumers.</li>
    <li>Monitor hot-held breakfast meats (bacon/sausages): maintain >63°C with frequent temperature checks.</li>
    <li>Inspect smoked salmon storage: keep at ≤5°C and check use-by dates.</li>
    <li>Refrigerate raw pancake batter: ensure ≤5°C between service bursts and discard after validated holding time.</li>
</ul>

<h3>What Records Auditors Expect</h3>
<ul>
    <li>Temperature logs for hollandaise, hot-held meats, and smoked salmon.</li>
    <li>Supplier verification records for eggs and smoked salmon, including assured scheme status and delivery temperatures.</li>
    <li>Records of staff training sessions, including topics covered and staff signatures.</li>
    <li>Allergen declarations for all pancake mix, syrups, and toppings, updated regularly.</li>
    <li>Daily monitoring checklists with signatures from responsible staff members.</li>
</ul>

<h3>Staff Training Requirements</h3>
<p>Brunch line cooks must master correct probe use for checking sausage and bacon temperatures. They also need to understand hollandaise time-temperature management to prevent it from dropping below 63°C.</p>
<p>Front-of-house staff should communicate allergen information clearly, especially regarding dishes containing egg, milk, and gluten. Ensure they can identify potential cross-contamination risks.</p>
<p>Prep staff must separate raw egg batter workflows from ready-to-eat fruit and toppings to avoid contamination. Shift leads should have a decision tree for reheating or discarding food when hot-hold temperatures drop below 63°C.</p>
<h3>Conclusion</h3>

<p>Brunch and breakfast cafes must comply with EC 852/2004 Annex II Ch. IX(5) for temperature control and hot holding, EC 853/2004 for hygiene requirements on eggs and products of animal origin, and Regulation (EU) 1169/2011 Annex II + Art. 44 for allergen information to consumers. Adhering to these regulations reduces food safety risks and protects vulnerable consumers. Implementing a robust HACCP plan will help manage key pathogens like Salmonella, Listeria, and Staphylococcus.</p>

<h3>Quick-Start Action Plan</h3>
<ol>
    <li>Review supplier documentation for eggs and smoked salmon to verify compliance with assured schemes and delivery temperatures.</li>
    <li>Train all staff on the correct holding temperatures for hot foods (≥63°C) and cold foods (≤5°C) this week.</li>
    <li>Implement a daily check for hollandaise sauce, ensuring it is held at ≥63°C or ≤5°C and not kept tepid on the pass.</li>
    <li>Set up a schedule for discarding raw egg batter after validated holding times and ensure it remains ≤5°C between service bursts.</li>
    <li>Conduct a staff meeting to reinforce allergen communication protocols, focusing on dishes that contain egg, milk, and gluten.</li>
</ol>
