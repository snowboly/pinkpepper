<h3>Introduction</h3>

<p>Serving doner kebabs directly from the spit without checking the core temperature of the slices is a common pitfall. This negligence can lead to foodborne illnesses, particularly from pathogens like Salmonella and E. coli. If you want your takeaway to thrive, address these issues head-on.</p>

<blockquote><strong>Audit Tip:</strong> Always verify that shaved doner slices reach a minimum core temperature of 75°C before serving, especially when they are pre-sliced and finished on a hotplate.</blockquote>

<h4>What You'll Learn</h4>
<ul>
  <li>How to implement critical control points (CCPs) specific to kebab and doner preparation.</li>
  <li>Best practices for managing raw-to-ready-to-eat (RTE) segregation in your kitchen.</li>
  <li>Effective staff training protocols to minimize cross-contamination risks during peak service times.</li>
</ul>

<h3>What Auditors Check First</h3>
<p>When an Environmental Health Officer (EHO) audits your kebab and doner takeaway, they focus on the following:</p>
<ul>
  <li>Core temperatures of shaved doner slices to ensure they reach at least 75°C before service.</li>
  <li>Separation practices between raw chicken skewers and ready-to-eat components to prevent cross-contamination.</li>
  <li>Documented cleaning resets between raw preparation periods and late-night service to ensure hygiene standards are met.</li>
</ul>
<h3>Hazards Specific to Kebab and Doner Takeaways</h3>

<p><strong>Biological Hazards:</strong> Key pathogens include Salmonella spp., Shiga toxin-producing E. coli (including O157), Campylobacter jejuni, and Staphylococcus aureus.</p>

<p>On a busy Saturday when the kitchen is under pressure, Salmonella spp. can thrive in raw minced poultry and lamb doner cones if not handled properly. Ensure that doner slices reach a core temperature of ≥75°C before serving, especially when shaved ahead and finished on the hotplate.</p>

<p>Shiga toxin-producing E. coli can contaminate minced meat doner mixes. Maintain strict segregation between raw and ready-to-eat (RTE) areas to prevent cross-contamination.</p>

<p><strong>Chemical Hazards:</strong> Common chemical hazards include allergens such as gluten, sesame, and mustard in sauces.</p>

<p>When preparing sauces on a busy night, staff may inadvertently cross-contaminate allergen-free ingredients. Confirm allergen declarations for all sauces and ensure staff are trained on allergen handling protocols.</p>

<p>Use separate utensils and equipment for allergenic and non-allergenic food items to minimize risks. Maintain a cleaning schedule that includes cleaning resets between raw prep periods and late-night service.</p>

<p><strong>Physical Hazards:</strong> Physical hazards can arise from foreign objects like glass or plastic from packaging.</p>

<p>On a late-night shift, if a container breaks, shards may contaminate the food. Conduct a thorough inspection of all food preparation areas to ensure no foreign objects are present before serving.</p>

<p>Implement a system for regular checks of storage containers and packaging to prevent such incidents. Train staff to recognize and report any potential physical hazards immediately.</p>

<h3>Seasonal Considerations</h3>

<p>Weekend late-night peaks increase fatigue and queue pressure, raising the risk of cross-contamination. Staff must remain vigilant and adhere to strict hygiene practices during these busy periods.</p>

<p>During summer festivals, higher throughput may lead to shortcuts, such as partial-cook or rapid serve of doner on spits. Monitor cooking times closely and ensure doner slices reach ≥75°C before service to avoid foodborne illness.</p>
<h3>Critical Control Points</h3>

<ul>
    <li>
        <strong>Doner Lethality CCP</strong>
        <p>Measure the core temperature of shaved doner slices. They must reach ≥75°C before service.</p>
        <p>Monitor using a calibrated probe thermometer every time doner is served.</p>
        <p>If the temperature is below 75°C, reheat the slices to at least 75°C or discard them.</p>
    </li>
    <li>
        <strong>Vertical Spit Control</strong>
        <p>Ensure the outer cooked surface of the doner is shaved thinly and frequently. No serving from the inner layers unless they are fully cooked.</p>
        <p>Monitor by visually inspecting the doner every 15 minutes during service hours.</p>
        <p>If the inner layers are served, stop service and re-educate staff on shaving procedures.</p>
    </li>
    <li>
        <strong>Raw-to-RTE Segregation</strong>
        <p>Verify that raw chicken shish skewers are prepared in a physically separated zone from salad and pitta assembly.</p>
        <p>Monitor with a checklist to confirm dedicated utensils are used during each shift.</p>
        <p>If raw and ready-to-eat items are cross-contaminated, halt service and conduct a thorough cleaning and reset.</p>
    </li>
    <li>
        <strong>Late-Night Supervision CCP</strong>
        <p>Conduct fixed probe checks on hot-held doner trays every hour after 22:00 to ensure they stay above 63°C.</p>
        <p>Use a calibrated probe thermometer for checks.</p>
        <p>If a tray drops below 63°C, reheat to ≥75°C or discard the contents immediately.</p>
    </li>
</ul>

<h3>Common Mistakes</h3>
<ul>
    <li>
        <strong>Mistake:</strong> Serving doner directly from the spit without verifying slice core temperature. 
        <br><strong>Fix:</strong> Always check that shaved slices reach ≥75°C before service.
    </li>
    <li>
        <strong>Mistake:</strong> Using the same gloved hands to handle raw chicken skewers and then touching pitta/salad.
        <br><strong>Fix:</strong> Implement strict handwash/glove-change protocols between handling raw and ready-to-eat items.
    </li>
    <li>
        <strong>Mistake:</strong> Not resetting cleaning protocols between raw prep and late-night service.
        <br><strong>Fix:</strong> Document and conduct a thorough cleaning reset before late-night operations commence.
    </li>
</ul>
<p>Supplier management is a critical component of food safety in kebab and doner takeaways. Proper checks upon receiving deliveries help prevent foodborne illnesses and maintain product quality. Follow these guidelines to ensure your ingredients meet safety standards.</p>

<h3>Delivery Acceptance Checklist</h3>
<ul>
    <li>Doner cone supplier must provide microbiological assurance and species integrity documentation.</li>
    <li>Accept raw poultry/lamb only when delivered at ≤4°C with intact packaging.</li>
    <li>Ensure full batch traceability for all meat products.</li>
    <li>Salad ingredients (lettuce, onions, tomatoes) must come from approved produce suppliers and be handled as ready-to-eat (RTE) after washing.</li>
    <li>Confirm allergen declarations (gluten, sesame, mustard, egg in mayo sauces) for pitta/bread and sauces.</li>
</ul>

<h3>When to Reject a Delivery</h3>
<ul>
    <li>Any raw meat delivered above 4°C or with compromised packaging.</li>
    <li>Missing or incomplete microbiological assurance documentation from suppliers.</li>
    <li>Salad ingredients showing signs of spoilage or contamination.</li>
    <li>Inconsistent allergen declarations on pitta/bread or sauces.</li>
</ul>

<blockquote>Always document any rejected deliveries with specific reasons. This creates a record to address any recurring supplier issues.</blockquote>
<h3>Daily Monitoring Checklist</h3>
<ul>
    <li>Check doner slice core temperature ≥75°C before service.</li>
    <li>Verify outer cooked surface of doner is shaved thinly and frequently.</li>
    <li>Ensure raw chicken shish prep area is physically separated from salad/pitta assembly.</li>
    <li>Confirm hot-held doner trays are maintained at ≥63°C during service.</li>
    <li>Document hourly probe checks post-22:00 to monitor temperature compliance.</li>
    <li>Inspect that handwashing and glove-change protocols are followed after handling raw poultry.</li>
</ul>

<h3>What Records Auditors Expect</h3>
<ul>
    <li>Temperature logs for doner slices (recorded at service times).</li>
    <li>Cleaning schedules showing resets between raw prep and late-night service.</li>
    <li>Delivery checks for raw poultry/lamb with temperature and traceability documentation.</li>
    <li>Competency training records for doner carvers and raw chicken staff.</li>
    <li>Allergen declaration records for pitta/bread and sauces, including supplier details.</li>
</ul>

<h3>Staff Training Requirements</h3>
<ul>
    <li>Doner carvers: Training on safe shave depth and mandatory ≥75°C checks on served portions.</li>
    <li>Raw chicken staff: Training on strict handwashing and glove-change triggers before touching RTE components.</li>
    <li>Night manager: Protocol for corrective actions when hot hold drops below 63°C (rapid reheat to ≥75°C or discard).</li>
    <li>Counter team: Training on allergen scripts for sesame, gluten, milk/egg sauces and limits on cross-contact.</li>
</ul>
<p>Your kebab and doner takeaway must comply with key regulations such as ensuring doner slices reach ≥75°C before service (EC 852/2004 Annex II Ch. IX(5)), maintaining raw-to-ready-to-eat segregation (EC 852/2004 Annex II Ch. V(2)), and adhering to allergen labeling requirements. Regular checks and documentation are required to verify compliance with these standards. Failure to meet these regulations can result in significant health risks and potential enforcement actions from local authorities.</p>

<h3>Quick-Start Action Plan</h3>
<ol>
  <li>Review your current temperature logs and ensure doner slices are consistently reaching ≥75°C.</li>
  <li>Physically separate raw chicken prep areas from salad and pitta assembly zones with dedicated utensils.</li>
  <li>Train staff on handwashing protocols and glove-change triggers before handling RTE components.</li>
  <li>Implement hourly temperature checks on hot-held doner trays during late-night service.</li>
  <li>Audit suppliers for microbiological assurance and confirm allergen declarations on sauces and breads.</li>
</ol>
