Regulation (EC) No 1069/2009 is the core EU framework for animal by-products and derived products not intended for human consumption.

Why it matters:
- Covers animal-origin material that falls out of the human food chain
- Important for catering waste, condemned products, animal-origin waste, and disposal routes
- Relevant to meat, fish, dairy, eggs, and mixed food operations

Core practical themes:
- Risk-based categorisation of animal by-products
- Rules for collection, transport, storage, use, processing, and disposal
- Need to distinguish edible food, waste, and ABP material
- Strong overlap with hygiene, traceability, and recall/withdrawal workflows

Use this source when the question concerns:
- catering waste
- disposal of food of animal origin
- handling returned or rejected animal-origin goods
- whether certain material may remain in the human food chain
- segregation of edible stock and waste streams
