REGULATION (EU) 2017/625 OF THE EUROPEAN PARLIAMENT AND OF THE COUNCIL
of 15 March 2017
on official controls and other official activities performed to ensure the application of food and feed law, and related rules

OFFICIAL SOURCE
- EUR-Lex ELI: https://eur-lex.europa.eu/eli/reg/2017/625/oj/eng
- CELEX: 32017R0625
- OJ L 95, 7.4.2017, pp. 1-142
- EUR-Lex consolidated versions include a current version dated 5 January 2025

ROLE IN THE LEGAL STACK
- This is the main EU official-controls framework
- It is primarily about how competent authorities inspect, verify, sample, and enforce
- It complements substantive obligations in laws such as:
  - Regulation (EC) No 178/2002
  - Regulation (EC) No 852/2004
  - Regulation (EC) No 853/2004
  - Regulation (EC) No 2073/2005

PRACTICAL EFFECT

1. RISK-BASED CONTROLS
- Official controls are to be organised on a risk basis
- Higher-risk businesses, foods, and supply chains can expect more frequent or more intensive controls

2. COMPETENT AUTHORITY POWERS
- Authorities may carry out inspections, audits, sampling, documentary checks, identity checks, and physical checks
- Operators must cooperate and provide access to premises, records, and relevant information

3. OFFICIAL METHODS AND LABORATORIES
- Official controls rely on documented procedures, validated methods, and designated laboratory systems
- Important for microbiology, contaminants, imports, and dispute situations

4. NON-COMPLIANCE RESPONSE
- Authorities may require operators to correct deficiencies
- Measures can include:
  - intensified controls
  - restrictions on placing food on the market
  - withdrawal or recall
  - treatment or destruction
  - other proportionate enforcement measures

5. IMPORT AND BORDER CONTROLS
- The regulation underpins the EU framework for official checks on certain imported foods
- Relevant when discussing high-risk imports, documentary checks, identity checks, and physical checks

WHY THIS MATTERS FOR RETRIEVAL

- Useful for import-control questions
