The Food Safety Act 1990 is a main UK domestic foundation for food safety enforcement.

Why it matters:
- Creates core offences around unsafe food, unfit food, and misleading food description
- Important for due diligence and enforcement reasoning
- Often sits behind local-authority and prosecution decisions

Use this source when the question concerns:
- due diligence
- unsafe or unfit food
- misleading description or presentation
- UK enforcement and prosecution risk
