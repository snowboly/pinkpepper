COMMISSION IMPLEMENTING REGULATION (EU) No 931/2011
of 19 September 2011
on the traceability requirements set by Regulation (EC) No 178/2002 for food of animal origin

OFFICIAL SOURCE
- EUR-Lex: https://eur-lex.europa.eu/legal-content/EN/TXT/?uri=CELEX%3A32011R0931
- CELEX: 32011R0931
- OJ L 242, 20.9.2011, pp. 2-3
- Applies from 1 July 2012

LEGAL PURPOSE
- Implements Article 18 of Regulation (EC) No 178/2002 for food of animal origin
- Makes traceability requirements more specific for higher-risk animal-origin foods

OFFICIAL CONTEXT FROM THE REGULATION
- Article 18 of Regulation (EC) No 178/2002 requires traceability at all stages of production, processing and distribution
- Operators must be able to identify from whom they received food and to whom they supplied it
- The regulation states that this information must be made available to competent authorities upon demand

SCOPE
- Applies to food defined as unprocessed and processed products under the hygiene framework
- Does not operate as a generic traceability law for all foods
- Particularly relevant for meat, fish, dairy, eggs, and similar animal-origin categories

TRACEABILITY INFORMATION EXPECTED
- accurate description of the food
- volume or quantity
- name and address of the supplier / dispatcher
- name and address of the consignee
- reference identifying the lot, batch or consignment, as appropriate
- date of dispatch

AVAILABILITY OF RECORDS
- The information must be provided to the competent authority without undue delay when requested
- Operators may choose the practical format, provided the information is clear and retrievable

WHY THIS MATTERS FOR RETRIEVAL
- Useful for questions about animal-origin traceability fields
- Useful for recall and withdrawal scope questions
- Useful where general Article 18 advice needs a more specific sector rule

USE WITH
- Regulation (EC) No 178/2002, Article 18
- Regulation (EC) No 852/2004
- Regulation (EC) No 853/2004
