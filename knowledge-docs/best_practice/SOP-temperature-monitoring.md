## 1. Purpose

To establish procedures for monitoring and controlling food temperatures throughout receipt, storage, preparation, cooking, cooling, hot holding, and service, ensuring compliance with The Food Hygiene (England) Regulations 2006 and EC 852/2004.

## 2. Scope

Applies to all temperature-controlled food products and processes including chilled storage, frozen storage, cooking, cooling, reheating, hot holding, and transport.

## 3. Responsible Persons

| Role | Responsibility |
|---|---|
| All food handlers | Record temperatures as per schedule; report deviations |
| Supervisors | Review temperature records daily; investigate failures; implement corrective actions |
| Manager | Ensure calibrated thermometers available; review trends; update HACCP plan |

## 4. Legal Temperature Requirements

### Cold Food Storage

| Food Type | Legal Requirement | Business Target |
|---|---|---|
| **Chilled food (general)** | ≤8°C | ≤5°C |
| **High-risk ready-to-eat food** | ≤8°C | ≤4°C |
| **Raw meat, fish, dairy** | ≤8°C | 0–4°C |
| **Frozen food** | No specific temperature (FSA guidance: -18°C or colder) | ≤-18°C |

> **Note:** The legal requirement is ≤8°C for most chilled food, but **industry best practice and food safety certification schemes (BRC, SALSA) require ≤5°C or lower**.

### Hot Holding

| Requirement | Temperature |
|---|---|
| **Minimum temperature for hot holding** | ≥63°C |
| **Business target** | ≥70°C |

Food held below 63°C for >2 hours must be discarded or chilled rapidly and used within appropriate shelf life.

### Cooking

- **Core temperature:** Food must reach ≥75°C for 30 seconds (or equivalent time/temperature combination per HACCP plan)
- **Alternative validated process:** 70°C for 2 minutes, 80°C for 6 seconds (for specific products with validated process)

### Cooling

Cooked food must be cooled rapidly:
- From **75°C to 8°C within 90 minutes** (best practice)
- Or from **60°C to 21°C within 2 hours, then to 8°C or below within a further 4 hours** (legal maximum)

### Reheating

Previously cooked food must be reheated to **≥75°C** core temperature.

## 5. Temperature Monitoring Equipment

### Probe Thermometers

**Types:**
- Digital probe thermometer (preferred for accuracy and speed)
- Infrared thermometer (surface temperature only; use for initial checks, verify with probe)

**Calibration:**
- Calibrate all thermometers **monthly** or when dropped/damaged
- Use ice-water method (0°C ±1°C) or boiling water method (100°C ±1°C at sea level)
- Record calibration checks on **thermometer calibration log**
- Label thermometer with calibration date
- Thermometers reading >±2°C outside target replaced or repaired

**Probe Hygiene:**
- Sanitise probe before and after each use (antibacterial wipe or sanitiser spray)
- Store in protective case
- Do not cross-contaminate (clean between raw and cooked food checks)

### Fridge and Freezer Thermometers

- Each fridge and freezer fitted with visible internal thermometer
- Digital display preferred (easier to read and record)
- Positioned in warmest part of unit (usually top front or door area)

### Data Loggers (if used)

- Continuous temperature monitoring with alarm alerts
- Data downloaded and reviewed weekly
- Alarms investigated immediately

## 6. Fridge and Freezer Monitoring

### Frequency

- **Minimum:** Check and record fridge and freezer temperatures **twice daily** (start of day and mid-afternoon)
- **Best practice:** Additional check at end of day (especially in warm weather)

### Procedure

1. Open fridge/freezer door briefly
2. Read display thermometer
3. Record temperature on **temperature monitoring log**
4. Check all temperatures within safe limits
5. Sign log

**Tolerance:**
- **Fridge:** 0–5°C (target); acceptable range 0–8°C
- **Freezer:** ≤-18°C

**Out of Range Action:**
- If temperature >8°C (fridge) or >-15°C (freezer): investigate immediately (see Section 10)

## 7. Delivery Temperature Checks

### Chilled Deliveries

- Check temperature of at least **3 random items** per chilled delivery using calibrated probe thermometer
- Insert probe into centre of product (between packs if probe cannot penetrate packaging)
- Record temperatures on **goods inward temperature log**

**Acceptance Criteria:**
- **Chilled food:** ≤8°C (preferably ≤5°C)
- **Frozen food:** ≤-12°C on delivery (must be solid frozen; no thawing or ice crystal loss)

**Rejection:** Deliveries arriving above acceptance temperature rejected and returned to supplier; supplier notified.

### Ambient Deliveries

- Check packaging integrity and absence of temperature abuse (condensation, ice crystals on ambient product)

## 8. Cooking Temperature Checks

### Core Temperature Monitoring

For all cooked food (meat, poultry, fish, reheated meals):
1. Insert calibrated probe thermometer into thickest part of food (centre of largest piece)
2. Ensure probe does not touch bone or cooking vessel (false reading)
3. Wait for temperature reading to stabilise
4. Record core temperature on **cooking temperature log**
5. Ensure temperature reaches **≥75°C for 30 seconds** (or validated alternative)

**Corrective Action:** If core temperature <75°C, continue cooking and recheck.

### Frequency

- **High-risk products (poultry, minced meat, rolled joints, burgers, sausages):** Check **every batch**
- **Whole joints, large roasts:** Check **each piece**
- **Other cooked products:** Check at least **one item per batch** or as per HACCP plan

---

## 9. Cooling Temperature Checks

### Rapid Cooling Procedure

1. Transfer cooked food to shallow containers (depth ≤50mm for rapid cooling)
2. Cover loosely (allow steam to escape)
3. Place in blast chiller or refrigerator (do not stack containers; allow air circulation)
4. Monitor cooling using probe thermometer inserted into thickest portion

### Cooling Checks

Record temperatures at intervals:
- **T0 (Start):** Temperature when cooling begins (typically ~75°C)
- **T30 (30 minutes):** Temperature after 30 minutes
- **T60 (60 minutes):** Temperature after 60 minutes
- **T90 (90 minutes):** Temperature after 90 minutes (target: ≤8°C)

**Target:** Achieve **≤8°C within 90 minutes**.

**Corrective Action:** If cooling too slowly (>8°C after 90 mins):
- Transfer to smaller/shallower containers
- Place in blast chiller
- Use ice bath around container
- If >4 hours elapsed and food >8°C: discard product

---

## 10. Hot Holding Temperature Checks

### Frequency

Check hot-held food temperature **every 2 hours** (or more frequently if risk-assessed).

### Procedure

1. Insert probe into centre of food (thickest part; avoid edges near heat source)
2. Record temperature on **hot holding temperature log**
3. Ensure temperature ≥63°C (target ≥70°C)

**Corrective Action:** If temperature <63°C:
- Check how long food has been <63°C
- If <2 hours: reheat to ≥75°C and return to hot hold
- If >2 hours: discard food

---

## 11. Reheating Temperature Checks

### Procedure

1. Reheat food to **core temperature ≥75°C**
2. Check with probe thermometer (thickest part)
3. Record temperature
4. Serve immediately or transfer to hot holding ≥63°C

**Important:** Reheat only once. Do not reheat food that has already been reheated previously.

## 12. Corrective Actions for Temperature Failures

### Fridge or Freezer Breakdown

| Temperature | Action |
|---|---|---|
| **Fridge 8–10°C** | Assess duration; if <4 hours and food appears safe, continue monitoring; if >4 hours, discard high-risk ready-to-eat food |
| **Fridge >10°C** | Discard high-risk food; transfer remaining stock to working fridge; arrange engineer callout |
| **Freezer -12 to -15°C** | Check product condition (solid frozen, no thawing); if solid, use product first or transfer to working freezer |
| **Freezer >-12°C or thawed** | Discard all food showing signs of thawing (soft, ice crystals melted); do not refreeze |

**Engineer Callout:**
- Contact refrigeration engineer immediately
- Do not reload fridge/freezer until repaired and temperature verified stable for 2 hours

**Stock Transfer:**
- Transfer stock to alternative fridge/freezer if available
- Record batch codes of affected stock
- Label and use first, or discard if safety in doubt

## 13. Temperature Monitoring Logs

### Daily Temperature Monitoring Log

Record:
- Date and time
- Fridge/freezer identification (unit number or location)
- Temperature reading
- Corrective action taken (if applicable)
- Staff signature

### Cooking / Cooling / Hot Holding Logs

Record:
- Date and time
- Product name or batch code
- Temperature reading
- Corrective action (if applicable)
- Staff signature

**Template:** See `knowledge-docs/templates/temperature-log-template.md`

---

## 14. Record Review

### Daily Review

Supervisor reviews temperature logs **daily**:
- Verify all required checks completed
- Identify missing records or unsigned logs (follow up with staff)
- Check corrective actions appropriate
- Identify trends (fridges running warm, frequent failures)

### Monthly Review

Manager reviews temperature logs **monthly**:
- Analyse trends (temperature fluctuations, seasonal variations)
- Identify repeat failures (equipment requiring maintenance or replacement)
- Verify staff compliance with monitoring schedule
- Review corrective actions and effectiveness

---

## 15. Equipment Maintenance

### Planned Preventive Maintenance (PPM)

- Fridges and freezers serviced **annually** by qualified refrigeration engineer
- Service includes: gas pressure check, thermostat calibration, condenser cleaning, door seal inspection
- Obtain service certificate; retain for equipment lifetime

### Cleaning and Defrosting

- Fridges and freezers cleaned **monthly** (or more frequently if required)
- Defrost manual-defrost freezers when ice build-up >5mm
- During cleaning: transfer stock to alternative cold storage; monitor temperature

---

## 16. Training

All food handlers must receive training on:
- Legal temperature requirements
- How to use and sanitise probe thermometers
- Recording temperatures accurately
- Corrective actions for temperature failures
- Importance of rapid cooling

**Frequency:** Induction + refresher annually.
