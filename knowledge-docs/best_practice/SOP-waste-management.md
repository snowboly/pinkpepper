## 1. Purpose

To ensure that all food waste, non-food waste, animal by-products (ABP), and recyclable materials are managed in a safe, hygienic, and legally compliant manner, preventing contamination of food and minimising pest risk.

## 2. Scope

Applies to all waste generated within the food production, preparation, and storage areas, including:
- Food waste (non-ABP)
- Animal by-products (Category 3)
- Used cooking oil
- Packaging and recyclables
- General non-recyclable waste

## 3. Responsible Persons

| Role | Responsibility |
|---|---|
| All food handlers | Deposit waste in correct bins promptly; report full bins |
| Shift supervisor | Monitor waste segregation; ensure bins emptied at correct frequency |
| Designated waste operative | Remove waste from food areas; clean waste stations; maintain records |
| Site manager / owner | Ensure licensed contractors are in place; review waste records |

---

## 4. Equipment Required

- Colour-coded, foot-operated bins with lids (correctly labelled by waste type)
- External lidded wheelie bins or skips (hard, cleanable surface storage)
- Sealed drums for used cooking oil
- Red-labelled, sealed bins for animal by-products (ABP)
- Bin liners of appropriate size and strength
- PPE: gloves, apron (when handling waste)
- Cleaning materials for waste area

---

## 5. Procedure — During Production

**5.1 — Immediately remove waste from food handling areas**
- Do not allow waste to accumulate on work surfaces or floors
- Place all food waste in the designated pedal-operated bin immediately
- Do not overfill bins — remove and replace bin liner when bin is approximately ¾ full

**5.2 — Segregate at source**
- Food waste (non-ABP): brown/green bin
- Raw meat/fish trimmings, bones (ABP Category 3): red ABP bin
- Cardboard/paper: blue recycling bin
- Plastic packaging: blue recycling bin (where accepted) or general waste
- Used cooking oil: separate sealed drum — never pour down drain
- General non-recyclable: black/grey bin

**5.3 — Handling used cooking oil**
- Allow oil to cool in fryer/vessel before handling
- Filter if possible to extend life
- Pour cooled oil into the designated sealed oil drum using a funnel
- Seal drum immediately
- Label drum with date oil was deposited

---

## 6. Procedure — End of Each Shift

1. Remove all bin liners from internal bins
2. Tie liners securely and transport to the appropriate external bin
3. Re-line all internal bins with fresh liners
4. Clean internal bins: wipe interior and exterior with appropriate disinfectant
5. Wipe and disinfect bin lids, handles, and surrounding area
6. Replace bin in designated location
7. Record on the cleaning schedule

---

## 7. External Waste Storage Area

1. Keep external waste storage area clean at all times
2. Clean external bins and storage area at minimum weekly (more often if soiled)
3. Ensure external bins have secure lids — closed at all times except when depositing waste
4. Check for signs of pests around bins weekly; report sightings immediately
5. Do not allow waste to be stored outside bins (bagged waste left on floor attracts pests)
6. Ensure adequate drainage around waste area

---

## 8. Animal By-Products (ABP Category 3) Procedure

1. Place raw meat/fish trimmings, bones, and offal from healthy animals into the designated **red ABP bin only**
2. ABP bin must be **clearly labelled** "Animal By-Products — Category 3 — Not for Human Consumption"
3. Bin must be **sealed** and **leak-proof**
4. Never mix ABP with general food waste or recycling
5. Transfer ABP to external ABP storage container at end of each shift
6. External ABP container kept locked or sealed to prevent pest access
7. Arrange collection with **APHA-registered ABP collector** (retain copy of their approval number)
8. Complete a **commercial document (consignment note)** for each collection:
   - Date
   - Type of material (Category 3 ABP)
   - Estimated quantity (kg)
   - Producer name and address
   - Carrier name, registration number, and vehicle reg
   - Destination processing facility
9. Retain consignment notes for **minimum 2 years**

---

## 9. Used Cooking Oil Procedure

1. Never pour used cooking oil down drains — this is an environmental offence
2. Store in designated sealed drums in the external waste area
3. Arrange collection with a **licensed waste carrier** registered to collect used cooking oil
4. Obtain a **waste transfer note** at each collection
5. Retain waste transfer notes for **minimum 2 years**
6. Record volume collected on waste log

---

## 10. Duty of Care Records

All waste collections from the premises must be accompanied by a **waste transfer note** (or equivalent) confirming the carrier is licensed.

Before using any waste carrier, verify their registration:
- England: Environment Agency Public Register (environment.data.gov.uk)
- Scotland: SEPA register
- Wales: NRW register
- Northern Ireland: NIEA register

Retain a copy of the carrier's registration/licence.

---

## 12. Non-Conformance

| Issue | Action |
|---|---|
| Wrong waste in wrong bin | Remove and place in correct bin; retrain operative |
| Bin not emptied; overflow | Empty immediately; retrain operative; review bin capacity/frequency |
| ABP bin lid left open | Reseal; retrain; increase supervision |
| Oil poured down drain | Report; unblock drain if affected; retrain; consider disciplinary action |
| Unlicensed waste carrier used | Do not allow removal without transfer note; obtain correct carrier |
| Pest activity at waste area | Report to pest control contractor immediately; increase cleaning frequency |

## 13. Review

This SOP must be reviewed:
