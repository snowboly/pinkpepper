## 1. Purpose

To establish systematic cleaning and sanitation procedures for all food contact surfaces, equipment, and food handling areas, ensuring compliance with EC 852/2004 Annex II Chapter IX and maintaining effective microbiological control.

## 2. Scope

Applies to all cleaning activities within food preparation, processing, and storage areas, including equipment cleaning, environmental cleaning, and sanitation verification.

## 3. Responsible Persons

| Role | Responsibility |
|---|---|
| Cleaning staff | Execute cleaning schedules; complete cleaning records |
| Supervisors | Verify cleaning effectiveness; conduct visual inspections; manage cleaning chemical safety |
| Manager | Approve cleaning schedules; arrange environmental swab testing; review validation data |

---

## 4. Cleaning vs. Sanitising

### Definitions

**Cleaning:** Removal of visible soil, food residue, grease, and dirt using detergent and water.

**Sanitising (Disinfection):** Reduction of microorganisms to safe levels using approved sanitisers after cleaning.

> **Critical:** Sanitisers only work on clean surfaces. Always clean first, then sanitise.

### Two-Stage Clean

1. **Stage 1 — Clean:** Remove visible contamination using detergent
2. **Stage 2 — Sanitise:** Apply approved sanitiser to reduce microbial load

## 5. Cleaning Chemicals

### Approved Chemicals Register

Maintain a register of all cleaning chemicals containing:
- Product name and supplier
- Safety Data Sheet (SDS)
- Approved use (detergent, sanitiser, descaler, etc.)
- Dilution rate and contact time
- Date approved for use

### Chemical Storage

- Store in dedicated chemical storage area (separate from food and food packaging)
- Keep in original labelled containers
- If decanted: use clearly labelled secondary containers (product name, dilution, hazard warnings)
- Lock away from unauthorised access
- Store incompatible chemicals separately (acids away from chlorine products)

### Chemical Safety

- COSHH risk assessments completed for all cleaning chemicals
- Training provided on safe use, dilution, and emergency procedures
- PPE worn as specified on SDS (gloves, eye protection, apron)
- Chemical spillage kit available and staff trained

## 6. Cleaning Schedule

### Schedule Requirements

A documented cleaning schedule must be in place covering:
- **What:** Item or area to be cleaned
- **How:** Method and materials (detergent, sanitiser, equipment)
- **When:** Frequency (after each use, daily, weekly, monthly)
- **Who:** Responsible person
- **Verification:** Sign-off and monitoring method

### Frequency Categories

| Frequency | Examples |
|---|---|
| **After each use** | Food contact surfaces, slicers, mixers, chopping boards, knives |
| **Daily** | Floors, walls, door handles, light switches, hand contact surfaces, waste bins |
| **Weekly** | High-level surfaces, walls behind equipment, drains, extraction filters |
| **Monthly** | Deep clean (move equipment), ceilings, light fittings, ventilation grilles |
| **Quarterly** | Refrigeration coils, extraction ductwork (professional service) |

## 7. Cleaning Procedures

### Food Contact Surface Cleaning (After Each Use)

**Applies to:** Worktops, chopping boards, slicers, mixers, cutting equipment, food containers

**Procedure:**
1. Remove all food debris and loose soil
2. Apply detergent solution at correct dilution
3. Scrub all surfaces using clean cloth or brush
4. Rinse thoroughly with clean potable water
5. Apply food-safe sanitiser at correct dilution and contact time
6. Allow to air-dry or use single-use paper towel
7. Equipment reassembled only when completely dry
8. Record cleaning completion on daily cleaning checklist

**Contact Time:** Minimum as per sanitiser manufacturer instructions (typically 30–60 seconds)

### Equipment Dismantling Procedure

1. Switch off and isolate from power
2. Dismantle removable parts (blades, guards, hoppers)
3. Wash all parts in detergent solution; sanitise
4. Clean fixed parts in situ
5. Rinse and sanitise
6. Reassemble when dry
7. Test equipment operation before use

### Floor Cleaning (Daily)

**Procedure:**
1. Remove debris and sweep or vacuum
2. Apply detergent solution; scrub with mop or mechanical scrubber
3. Rinse with clean water
4. Apply floor sanitiser if required (high-risk areas)
5. Allow to dry; use wet floor warning signs
6. Clean drains and gullies
7. Record completion

**High-Risk Areas:** Floors in raw preparation areas, near open food, and around ready-to-eat production require sanitising after cleaning.

### Wall and High-Level Cleaning (Weekly/Monthly)

**Weekly:**
- Wipe down walls behind equipment and in splash zones
- Clean skirting boards and corners
- Clean window sills and ledges

**Monthly:**
- Ceiling cleaning (remove cobwebs, dust)
- Light fittings (ensure shatterproof covers in place)
- Ventilation grilles and extraction units (deep clean or professional service quarterly)

## 8. Colour-Coded Cleaning System

### Colour Code

| Colour | Use |
|---|---|
| **Red** | Raw meat and poultry areas |
| **Blue** | Raw fish areas |
| **Yellow** | Cooked and ready-to-eat food areas |
| **Green** | Fruit and vegetable preparation areas |
| **White** | Bakery and dairy areas |
| **Black** | Non-food areas (bins, floors, toilets) |

### Items to Colour-Code

- Cleaning cloths
- Mop heads and handles
- Buckets
- Scrubbing brushes
- Chopping boards
- Waste bins (additional labelling required)

### Cross-Contamination Prevention

- Never use cloths or equipment from one colour zone in another
- Store colour-coded items separately by zone
- Launder cloths separately or in designated wash cycle order (least contaminated first)

## 9. Cleaning Tools and Cloths

### Single-Use vs. Reusable

**Single-Use (Disposable):**
- Use disposable paper towel or single-use wipes for final drying of food contact surfaces
- Use once and discard
- Reduces risk of cross-contamination

**Reusable Cloths:**
- Must be laundered at ≥60°C after each shift
- Stored dry and clean in designated container
- Discard when worn, frayed, or odorous
- Change cloths regularly during cleaning task

### Mops and Buckets

- Mop heads laundered daily at ≥60°C
- Buckets cleaned, sanitised, and stored inverted to dry
- Replace mop heads when worn

### Scrubbing Brushes and Equipment

- Clean after each use; sanitise; air-dry
- Check for wear and replace as required
- Store in dedicated cleaning equipment area (not in food areas)

## 10. Waste and Refuse Area Cleaning

### Bin Cleaning

- Internal food waste bins: emptied and cleaned **daily**
- External bins: cleaned **weekly** or more frequently if required
- Bin lids cleaned; bins kept closed when not in use
- Bin areas swept and washed down

### Cleaning Procedure

1. Empty bin contents into external refuse collection point
2. Rinse bin with hose or pressure washer
3. Apply detergent; scrub
4. Rinse thoroughly
5. Apply sanitiser
6. Allow to dry inverted
7. Return bin to designated location with lid

## 11. Sanitiser Contact Time and Dilution

### Types of Sanitisers

| Sanitiser Type | Typical Dilution | Contact Time | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Quaternary Ammonium Compounds (Quats)** | 200–400 ppm | 30–60 seconds | Effective against bacteria; less effective on viruses |
| **Chlorine-based (Sodium Hypochlorite)** | 150–200 ppm available chlorine | 30 seconds | Broad-spectrum; rinse if used on food contact surfaces; corrosive |
| **Alcohol-based (70% ethanol)** | Ready-to-use | 30 seconds | Small surfaces; evaporates quickly; flammable |
| **Peracetic Acid** | As per manufacturer | 1–2 minutes | Effective on biofilms; rinse not always required |

### Correct Dilution

- Use manufacturer's instructions or dilution charts
- Use measuring equipment (dosing pumps, measuring cups)
- Test concentration using test strips where available (e.g. chlorine test strips for hypochlorite)
- Record dilution checks on cleaning records

### Contact Time

Sanitiser must remain in contact with the surface for the **full contact time** specified by the manufacturer. Do not wipe off immediately.

## 12. Clean-In-Place (CIP) Systems

### Automated Cleaning

For large equipment (tanks, pipework, heat exchangers):
1. Follow manufacturer's CIP cycle instructions
2. Record cycle parameters (temperature, chemical concentration, time)
3. Verify rinse cycle completion (check for chemical residues)
4. Conduct ATP or microbiological swabbing to verify effectiveness

---

## 13. Verification and Monitoring

### Visual Inspection

Supervisors conduct daily visual inspections:
- Surfaces free of visible soil, residue, and moisture
- No grease build-up
- No unpleasant odours
- Equipment correctly reassembled and dry

### ATP (Adenosine Triphosphate) Testing

- Rapid surface hygiene testing
- Use swabs on random high-risk surfaces weekly
- Record results; investigate failures (re-clean and re-test)
- Trend analysis to identify problem areas

### Microbiological Swabbing

- Environmental swabbing programme for high-risk areas
- Monthly or quarterly (frequency risk-based)
- Send to accredited laboratory; review results with EHO or consultant
- Corrective action for positive results (deep clean, review procedure, re-train staff)

---

## 14. Deep Cleaning Programme

### Frequency

At minimum **quarterly** or more frequently as risk-assessed.

### Procedure

1. Schedule deep clean during non-production period
2. Move all portable equipment
3. Clean behind and underneath fixed equipment
4. Pressure wash floors and walls if suitable
5. Descale drains and gullies
6. Clean extraction ductwork (professional contractor if required)
7. Verify completion with ATP or swab testing

---

## 15. Allergen Cleaning

When switching from allergen-containing production to allergen-free:
1. Complete normal cleaning procedure
2. Use dedicated or freshly laundered cloths
3. Clean equipment in reverse order of assembly (dismantle fully)
4. Rinse thoroughly to remove all allergenic residue
5. Verify allergen removal using allergen swab test kits (if available) or visual inspection + ATP
6. Record allergen cleaning completion and verification

## 16. Training

All cleaning staff and supervisors must receive training on:
- This SOP and the master cleaning schedule
- Chemical safety (COSHH) and correct dilution
- Colour-coded cleaning system
- Sanitiser contact time and effectiveness
- Use of ATP meters and swab testing (where applicable)

**Frequency:** Induction + refresher annually or when procedures change.
