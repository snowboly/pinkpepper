## 1. Purpose

To establish effective pest prevention and control measures to protect food from contamination, ensuring compliance with EC 852/2004 Annex II Chapter IX and maintaining a pest-free food production environment.

## 2. Scope

Applies to all areas of the food business including production areas, storage areas, waste areas, external perimeter, and incoming deliveries. Covers rodents, insects, birds, and other pests.

## 3. Responsible Persons

| Role | Responsibility |
|---|---|
| Manager | Contract pest control company; review pest control reports; implement corrective actions |
| Supervisors | Conduct daily pest inspections; monitor bait stations and traps; report sightings |
| All staff | Report any pest sightings immediately; maintain proofing standards |
| Pest Control Contractor | Conduct surveys; maintain bait stations and traps; provide treatment; submit reports |

---

## 4. Pest Control Strategy

### Integrated Pest Management (IPM)

The food business operates an Integrated Pest Management approach:

1. **Proofing:** Prevent pest entry through building maintenance and good design
2. **Monitoring:** Early detection through inspections, traps, and bait stations
3. **Treatment:** Targeted control measures when pests detected
4. **Documentation:** Record all inspections, sightings, and treatments

> **Hierarchy:** Prevention is always preferred over treatment. Eliminate pest harbourage and entry points before relying on pesticides.

## 5. Proofing and Prevention

### Building Proofing

**External:**
- All gaps >3mm sealed (mice can enter through 6mm gaps; rats through 12mm)
- Door seals intact; self-closing mechanisms on all external doors
- Windows fitted with insect-proof mesh (if openable)
- External drains fitted with rodent-proof covers
- Air vents fitted with mesh screens
- Loading bay doors kept closed when not in use; fitted with strip curtains or rapid-roll doors
- No vegetation or stored items within 1 metre of external walls

**Internal:**
- Wall and floor junctions sealed
- Service entry points (pipes, cables) sealed with wire wool and sealant
- Holes in walls, ceilings, and floors sealed
- No false ceilings accessible to pests (or sealed and monitored)

### Waste Management

- Internal bins: lidded; emptied daily; cleaned regularly
- External bins: lidded; located away from building entrances; cleaned weekly
- Bin areas: hard-standing surface; kept clean; no spillages left overnight
- Refuse collection regular and frequent

### Goods Receiving

- Inspect all deliveries for signs of pest activity before acceptance
- Check packaging for gnawing, droppings, webbing, or live pests
- Reject infested deliveries; quarantine and report to supplier
- Record pest-related rejections

### Housekeeping

- No food or food waste left exposed overnight
- Spillages cleaned immediately
- Floors swept and cleaned daily
- No accumulation of cardboard or packaging in production areas (pest harbourage)
- Equipment and stock stored off the floor (minimum 150mm clearance)
- Storage areas tidy; no clutter or unused items

## 6. Pest Monitoring

### Pest Control Contractor Visits

- Contracted pest control company visits at minimum **monthly** (or more frequently if required by risk assessment or infestation history)
- Contractor holds recognised qualifications (BPCA, RSPH Level 2 or equivalent)
- Contractor operates to British Pest Control Association (BPCA) Code of Practice

### Monitoring Equipment

| Equipment | Purpose | Location |
|---|---|---|
| **Rodent bait stations** | Detect and control rodents | External perimeter (every 5–10m); internal high-risk areas |
| **Glue boards (non-toxic)** | Detect rodent activity | Internal areas where bait not permitted (near open food) |
| **Insect Light Traps (ILT / EFK)** | Attract and capture flying insects | Internal areas away from open food; near entry points |
| **Pheromone traps** | Monitor stored product insects | Dry goods storage areas |
| **Crawling insect monitors** | Detect cockroaches, ants, beetles | Warm areas; near equipment motors; storage areas |

### Site Plan and Numbering

- Up-to-date site plan showing locations of all monitoring devices
- Each device numbered sequentially
- Map reviewed and updated when devices added, removed, or relocated

## 7. Internal Pest Inspections

### Daily Staff Inspections

All staff must remain vigilant and report immediately:
- Live pests (rodents, insects, birds)
- Pest droppings or smear marks
- Gnawed packaging or structural damage
- Unusual odours (e.g. mouse urine)
- Dead pests

**Reporting:** Report sightings to supervisor immediately; supervisor records in pest sighting log.

### Weekly Supervisor Inspections

Supervisors conduct systematic weekly inspections:
- Check insect light traps (empty and record catch)
- Inspect all accessible bait stations and glue boards (external stations inspected if safe to access)
- Check building proofing (doors, seals, gaps)
- Inspect waste areas and drains
- Record findings on **weekly pest inspection checklist**

**Action:** Any pest activity triggers immediate investigation and corrective action.

## 8. Contractor Pest Control Visits

### Monthly Visit Procedure

1. Contractor arrives; signs in; receives hygiene and site safety briefing
2. Contractor inspects all monitoring devices; records findings
3. Contractor inspects building proofing and identifies risks
4. Contractor treats any infestations using approved methods
5. Contractor completes written report and discusses findings with manager
6. Contractor recommendations implemented by agreed deadline

### Pest Control Report

Each visit report must include:
- Date, time, and technician name
- Bait station/trap inspection results (numbered devices; activity recorded)
- Species identified and levels of infestation
- Treatments applied (pesticide name, location, quantity)
- Recommendations for proofing or corrective action
- Trend analysis (increasing/stable/decreasing activity)
- Signature of technician and food business representative

### Report Review

Manager reviews each pest control report within **48 hours**:
- Verify no pest activity or declining trend
- Action any contractor recommendations (proofing repairs, additional devices, treatment)
- Escalate persistent or increasing activity (request additional visit; deep clean; investigate root cause)
- File report (retain for minimum 3 years)

## 9. Bait Stations and Rodenticides

### External Bait Stations

- Tamper-proof lockable stations
- Positioned every 5–10 metres around external perimeter
- Securely fixed to ground or wall
- Contain rodenticide bait blocks or non-toxic monitoring blocks (if no activity)
- Inspected monthly by contractor; weekly external inspection by supervisor (where safe)

### Internal Bait Stations

- **Preference:** Non-toxic monitoring devices (glue boards) in internal areas
- If rodenticide bait required internally (e.g. during active infestation): risk assess; use tamper-proof stations; remove as soon as infestation cleared
- Never place bait near open food or food contact surfaces

### Rodenticide Safety

- All rodenticides stored securely by contractor or in locked chemical store
- Safety Data Sheets available
- Only applied by trained, competent contractor
- Bait consumption recorded; replenished as required
- Dead rodents removed and disposed of safely (wear gloves; double-bag; incinerate or dispose as clinical waste)

## 10. Insect Control

### Flying Insects

**Insect Light Traps (ILT / Electric Fly Killers):**
- Positioned away from open food (minimum 3 metres) to avoid attracting insects to food
- Located near entry points (doors, windows, loading bays)
- Positioned out of direct sunlight (UV tubes lose effectiveness in daylight)
- Height: 1–2 metres above floor
- Cleaned and bulbs replaced annually or per manufacturer schedule
- Catch trays emptied and recorded weekly

**Action Levels:**
- Low catch (<10 flies per week per trap): normal monitoring
- Moderate catch (10–50 flies per week): investigate; increase cleaning; check proofing
- High catch (>50 flies per week): contractor visit; identify source; increase treatment frequency

### Stored Product Insects

Common pests: flour beetles, weevils, moths, mites

**Prevention:**
- Rotate stock (FIFO — First In, First Out)
- Inspect incoming goods for infestation
- Store in sealed containers
- Maintain cool, dry storage conditions
- Clean spillages immediately

**Monitoring:**
- Pheromone traps in dry goods storage
- Weekly visual inspections for webbing, larvae, or adult insects

**Treatment:**
- Quarantine infested stock
- Dispose of infested product
- Deep clean storage area
- Contractor treatment if widespread

### Crawling Insects

Common pests: cockroaches, ants, beetles

**Monitoring:**
- Sticky monitors in warm, dark areas (behind equipment, near motors, in storage areas)
- Inspect weekly; replace when full or every 3 months

**Treatment:**
- Identify species and harbourage
- Contractor applies residual insecticide to harbourage areas (not near food)
- Improve housekeeping and proofing
- Follow-up treatment as required

## 11. Birds

### Prevention

- Proof all roof voids and eaves
- Fit bird netting or spikes on ledges and roosting areas
- Keep loading bay doors closed; use rapid-roll doors or strip curtains
- Remove external water sources and food waste

### Control

If birds present internally:
1. Evacuate food from area
2. Contractor to remove birds humanely
3. Clean and disinfect area (bird droppings carry pathogens)
4. Identify and seal entry point
5. Deep clean before food production resumes

## 12. Pest Infestation — Emergency Response

### Immediate Actions

1. **Stop production** in affected area if contamination risk
2. **Quarantine product** that may be contaminated
3. **Contact pest control contractor** for emergency visit
4. **Investigate source** and extent of infestation
5. **Implement treatment** (contractor applies approved pesticides)
6. **Clean and sanitise** affected areas after treatment
7. **Dispose of contaminated product** (record as waste)
8. **Review and improve** proofing and prevention measures

### Product Withdrawal

If pest contamination detected in finished product:
- Quarantine affected batch
- Conduct traceability check (identify all affected product)
- Assess risk (physical contamination, pathogen risk from droppings)
- If distributed: initiate **product recall** procedure (see SOP-Traceability)
- Notify relevant authorities if required

## 13. Pesticide Use and Safety

### Approved Pesticides

- Only pesticides approved for use in food premises are used
- Contractor maintains list of approved products and Safety Data Sheets
- Application records maintained (product name, active ingredient, location, quantity, date)

### Safety Precautions

- Food and food contact surfaces covered or removed before treatment
- Staff excluded from treated areas until safe (per pesticide label)
- Treated areas cleaned after required contact time
- No pesticide application near open food
- Spillages cleaned immediately

## 14. Training

All staff must receive training on:
- Recognising signs of pest activity
- Reporting procedures
- Proofing and prevention measures (doors, waste management)
- Personal responsibility for housekeeping

**Supervisors** additionally trained on:
- Weekly inspection procedure
- Interpreting pest monitoring data
- Emergency response

**Frequency:** Induction + refresher annually.
