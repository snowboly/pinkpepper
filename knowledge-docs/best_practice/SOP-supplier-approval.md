## 1. Purpose

To establish procedures for the approval, monitoring, and review of food and food-contact material suppliers, ensuring that all suppliers meet food safety and quality standards and comply with relevant legislation.

## 2. Scope

Applies to all suppliers of:
- Raw materials and ingredients
- Packaging and food-contact materials
- Finished products for resale
- Catering supplies and ancillary items used in food production

## 3. Responsible Persons

| Role | Responsibility |
|---|---|
| Manager / Owner | Approve new suppliers; review supplier performance; manage supplier database |
| Purchasing / Procurement | Source suppliers; collect approval documentation; place orders only with approved suppliers |
| Quality Assurance | Conduct supplier audits; review test results; manage non-conformances |
| Goods Receiving | Inspect deliveries; reject non-conforming product; record supplier performance |

---

## 4. Supplier Approval Process

### New Supplier Assessment

Before placing orders with a new supplier, the following approval process must be completed:

#### Step 1: Risk Classification

Classify the supplier based on product risk:

| Risk Category | Examples | Approval Requirements |
|---|---|---|
| **High Risk** | Raw meat, fish, dairy, ready-to-eat products | Full approval (audit or third-party certification) |
| **Medium Risk** | Dry ingredients, canned goods, frozen products | Documentary approval + sampling |
| **Low Risk** | Packaging, cleaning chemicals, non-food items | Basic approval (supplier questionnaire) |

#### Step 2: Documentary Approval

Request and review the following documents:

**All Suppliers:**
- Company registration details
- Food business registration or approval number
- Public liability and product liability insurance
- Contact details (including out-of-hours emergency contact)

**Food Suppliers (High and Medium Risk):**
- Current food safety certification (BRC, SALSA, ISO 22000, FSSC 22000, or equivalent)
- HACCP plan or food safety management system documentation
- Allergen management procedures
- Traceability procedures
- Product specifications for each product supplied

**Food Contact Material Suppliers:**
- Declaration of compliance with EC 1935/2004
- Migration test certificates (if applicable)
- Material safety declarations

#### Step 3: Third-Party Certification Verification

- For **high-risk suppliers:** Verify current third-party certification (BRC, SALSA, etc.)
- Check certificate scope covers the products being supplied
- Verify certificate validity (expiry date, accreditation body)
- Obtain copy of latest audit report (if available) or certificate

**If no third-party certification:** Conduct supplier audit (see Section 5).

#### Step 4: Product Sampling and Testing (if applicable)

- For high or medium-risk products: request product samples
- Conduct sensory evaluation (appearance, smell, taste if appropriate)
- Send samples for laboratory testing:
  - Microbiological testing (TVC, Enterobacteriaceae, pathogens as risk-assessed)
  - Chemical testing (allergens, additives, contaminants as applicable)
- Review test results; accept only if results meet specifications

#### Step 5: Trial Order

- Place small trial order
- Assess product quality, packaging integrity, labelling accuracy
- Check delivery conditions (temperature, packaging, vehicle cleanliness)
- Record trial outcome

#### Step 6: Approval Decision

- Manager reviews all documentation and trial results
- Supplier approved, conditionally approved, or rejected
- If approved: add to **Approved Supplier List**
- Assign supplier reference number

## 5. Supplier Audits

### When Audits Are Required

- High-risk supplier with no third-party certification
- Supplier performance concerns (frequent non-conformances, complaints)
- Major product or process change at supplier
- Re-approval following suspension

### Audit Planning

- Schedule audit at least 2 weeks in advance
- Prepare audit checklist covering:
  - HACCP system and prerequisite programmes
  - Traceability and recall procedures
  - Allergen management
  - Supplier approval and raw material controls
  - Pest control
  - Cleaning and sanitation
  - Staff training
  - Complaints and corrective actions

### Audit Conduct

- Conducted by trained auditor or third-party consultant
- Opening meeting with supplier management
- Site tour and document review
- Interviews with staff
- Closing meeting: findings discussed; corrective actions agreed

### Audit Report

- Non-conformances categorised (Critical, Major, Minor)
- Corrective action plan agreed with timescales
- Supplier approval status confirmed or amended
- Report filed; follow-up audit scheduled if required

## 6. Approved Supplier List

### Register Contents

Maintain an **Approved Supplier List** (digital or hard copy) containing:

| Field | Details |
|---|---|
| Supplier name | Trading name and legal entity |
| Supplier reference number | Unique internal reference |
| Address and contact details | Including emergency contact |
| Products supplied | Description and product codes |
| Risk category | High / Medium / Low |
| Approval date | Date approved |
| Approval method | Certification / Audit / Documentary |
| Certification details | Scheme, certificate number, expiry date |
| Review due date | Annual review or as risk-assessed |
| Status | Approved / Conditional / Suspended / Rejected |

### Access and Control

- Only **approved suppliers** on the list may be used for ordering
- Purchasing team accesses list before placing orders
- List updated immediately when suppliers approved, suspended, or removed
- Manager authorises all changes to the list

## 7. Ongoing Supplier Monitoring

### Delivery Inspections

Every delivery inspected on arrival:
- Vehicle cleanliness and temperature (for chilled/frozen goods)
- Product temperature (use calibrated probe thermometer)
- Packaging integrity (no damage, leaks, contamination)
- Labelling accuracy (product name, date marks, allergen information, batch codes)
- Absence of pests or pest damage
- Sensory check (appearance, smell)

**Record:** Goods inward inspection log.

**Rejection:** Non-conforming deliveries rejected; supplier notified; corrective action requested.

### Performance Monitoring

Track supplier performance using key metrics:

| Metric | Target | Monitoring Frequency |
|---|---|---|
| On-time delivery rate | ≥95% | Monthly |
| Product conformance rate (rejections) | ≤2% | Monthly |
| Temperature compliance (chilled/frozen) | 100% | Per delivery |
| Labelling accuracy | 100% | Per delivery |
| Customer complaints linked to supplier | 0 | Per incident |

**Action:** Suppliers failing to meet targets contacted; corrective action requested; performance reviewed.

### Product Testing Programme

- Periodic sampling and testing of high-risk products
- Frequency: quarterly or as risk-assessed
- Tests: microbiological, chemical (allergens, additives), authenticity
- Results reviewed; non-conformances investigated with supplier

## 8. Supplier Review

### Annual Supplier Review

All approved suppliers reviewed annually (or more frequently if high-risk or poor performance):

**Review includes:**
- Performance data (delivery, quality, rejections, complaints)
- Certification status (check expiry dates; request updated certificates)
- Any changes to supplier processes or products
- Test results (if applicable)
- Audit findings (if conducted)

**Outcome:**
- **Remain approved:** Continue supply
- **Conditional approval:** Corrective action required; re-review in 3–6 months
- **Suspend:** Stop ordering until issues resolved; re-audit or request evidence
- **Remove approval:** Cease supply; source alternative supplier

**Record:** Supplier review record (date, reviewer, outcome, actions).

## 9. Supplier Non-Conformance Management

### Reporting Non-Conformances

When a supplier delivers non-conforming product or fails to meet requirements:
1. **Reject delivery** or **quarantine product**
2. **Raise supplier non-conformance report** (date, product, issue, evidence)
3. **Notify supplier** immediately (phone + written confirmation)
4. **Request corrective action** (root cause investigation and preventive measures)
5. **Supplier responds** with corrective action plan and timescale
6. **Verify effectiveness** (check subsequent deliveries; request evidence)

### Escalation

**Minor non-conformance (isolated incident):** Supplier corrects; monitor next delivery.

**Major non-conformance or repeated issues:**
- Suspend supplier
- Request urgent corrective action and evidence
- Conduct supplier audit or request third-party audit
- Source alternative supplier if corrective action inadequate

**Critical non-conformance (immediate food safety risk):**
- Reject all stock from supplier
- Suspend supplier immediately
- Notify enforcement authority if required
- Conduct product recall if affected product distributed (see SOP-Traceability)

## 10. Changes to Supplier Products or Processes

Suppliers must notify the food business **in advance** of any changes to:
- Ingredients or formulation
- Allergen status
- Manufacturing process or location
- Packaging materials
- Specifications

**Food Business Action:**
- Review change impact (food safety, allergen, quality)
- Request updated documentation (spec, certificate, test results)
- Re-approve supplier if significant change
- Update product risk assessment and labelling if required

**Unapproved changes:** Supplier deliveries rejected until approval obtained.

---

## 11. Supplier Contracts and Specifications

### Written Agreements

For high and medium-risk suppliers, maintain written agreements covering:
- Product specifications (ingredients, quality standards, shelf life)
- Allergen declarations and cross-contamination controls
- Delivery requirements (temperature, packaging, lead time)
- Traceability and batch coding
- Notification of changes
- Right to audit
- Product recall cooperation

### Product Specifications

Each product supplied must have a documented specification including:
- Product name and description
- Ingredient list (including allergens)
- Microbiological and chemical standards
- Pack size and packaging type
- Storage and shelf-life requirements
- Country of origin (if relevant)
- Specification version and date

**Review:** Specifications reviewed annually or when changes occur.

---

## 12. Traceability from Suppliers

### Supplier Traceability Requirements

Suppliers must:
- Provide batch codes or lot numbers on all deliveries
- Maintain traceability one-step-back to their suppliers
- Provide traceability information within **4 hours** on request
- Participate in product recall or withdrawal if required

**Verification:** Conduct traceability exercises with suppliers annually (request batch information for random delivery; verify supplier can trace within 4 hours).

---

## 13. Emergency Supplier Approval

In exceptional circumstances (e.g. regular supplier fails; urgent production requirement):

**Temporary approval may be granted for up to 3 months** subject to:
1. Minimum documentary checks (registration, insurance, allergen declaration)
2. Manager authorisation
3. Enhanced goods-inward inspection
4. Full approval process completed within 3 months

**Record:** Justification for emergency approval; expiry date of temporary approval.

---

## 14. Training

Staff involved in supplier approval and monitoring must be trained on:
- This SOP
- Risk classification and approval requirements
- Goods-inward inspection procedure
- Non-conformance reporting
- Traceability requirements

**Frequency:** Induction + refresher every 2 years.
