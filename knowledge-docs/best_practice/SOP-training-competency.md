## 1. Purpose

To ensure all food handlers receive appropriate food safety training and are competent to perform their duties safely, in compliance with EC 852/2004 Article 4 and Regulation 852/2004 Chapter XII.

## 2. Scope

Applies to all employees, agency staff, contractors, and volunteers involved in food handling or food safety management.

## 3. Training Requirements by Role

| Role | Minimum Training | Frequency |
|---|---|---|
| **Food handlers** | Level 2 Food Safety in Catering | Every 3 years |
| **Supervisors** | Level 3 Food Safety Supervision | Every 3 years |
| **Managers / Owners** | Level 4 HACCP or equivalent | Every 5 years |
| **All staff** | Induction training (site-specific) | On joining + annual refresher |

### Induction Training

Conducted **before unsupervised food handling work**. Covers:
- Personal hygiene (handwashing, protective clothing, fitness to work)
- Cross-contamination prevention
- Temperature control
- Allergen management
- Cleaning and sanitisation
- Pest awareness
- Reporting incidents and illness

**Delivery:** Face-to-face session with supervisor; written materials provided.

**Assessment:** Competency checked through observation and verbal questioning.

**Record:** Induction training record signed by trainer and trainee.

---

## 4. On-the-Job Training

### Task-Specific Training

Provided when staff assigned to new tasks:
- Operating specific equipment (slicers, mixers, ovens)
- HACCP monitoring tasks (temperature recording, CCP checks)
- Allergen handling procedures
- Cleaning chemical use (COSHH training)

**Delivery:** Demonstration by competent supervisor; supervised practice; competency assessment.

**Record:** Training log (task, date, trainer, outcome).

### Competency Assessment

- Supervisor observes staff performing tasks
- Checks adherence to procedures
- Provides feedback and retraining if required
- Records competency check outcome

**Frequency:** New starters assessed within first 2 weeks; ongoing observation.

---

## 5. Refresher Training

### Annual Refresher

All staff receive **annual refresher training** covering:
- Key food safety hazards
- Personal hygiene standards
- Allergen awareness update
- Changes to procedures or legislation
- Lessons learned from incidents

**Delivery:** Team meeting, toolbox talk, or online module.

**Duration:** Minimum 1 hour.

**Record:** Attendance register and training content summary.

### Formal Qualification Renewal

- Level 2/3/4 qualifications renewed before expiry
- Certificates retained on staff files

---

## 6. Training Records

### Individual Training File

Maintain for each employee:
- Name and job role
- Date of joining
- Induction training record (signed and dated)
- Formal qualification certificates (Level 2/3/4)
- On-the-job training log
- Annual refresher training attendance
- Competency assessment records

**Retention:** Duration of employment + 3 years.

### Training Matrix

Maintain a training matrix showing:
- Staff name and role
- Qualifications held (type, date, expiry)
- Induction completion date
- Refresher training dates
- Competency status (competent / under training / retraining required)

**Review:** Monthly by manager; identify training gaps.

---

## 7. External Training Providers

- Use accredited training providers (Highfield, CIEH, RSPH, or equivalent)
- Verify trainer qualifications
- Obtain certificates within 4 weeks of course completion

---

## 8. Language and Literacy

- Training delivered in language understood by staff
- Use visual aids, demonstrations, and interpreters if required
- Check understanding through practical demonstration (not just written tests)

---

## 9. Contractors and Visitors

- Contractors receive hygiene briefing before entering food areas
- Visitors complete visitor hygiene declaration and briefing
- Record maintained

---

## 10. Training Needs Analysis

Conducted annually:
- Review incident log (identify training gaps linked to incidents)
- Review competency assessments (identify retraining needs)
- Consider new legislation, products, or processes
- Plan training schedule for year ahead

---

## 11. Review

This SOP is reviewed:
- Annually
- Following food safety incidents linked to staff competency
- When new processes or hazards introduced
- When legislation changes
