## 1. Purpose

To establish clear personal hygiene standards for all staff working in food handling and food preparation areas, ensuring compliance with EC 852/2004 Annex II Chapter VIII and the food business's HACCP plan.

## 2. Scope

Applies to all employees, agency workers, contractors, and visitors who enter food handling or food preparation areas.

## 3. Responsible Persons

| Role | Responsibility |
|---|---|
| All food handlers | Comply with this SOP at all times |
| Supervisors | Enforce standards; conduct hygiene checks; manage fitness-to-work decisions |
| HR / Manager | Ensure induction training covers this SOP; maintain training records |

## 4. Handwashing Procedure

### Required Handwashing Facilities
- Dedicated handwashing sinks (separate from food-washing or equipment-washing sinks)
- Hot and cold running water (or blended warm water)
- Liquid antibacterial soap
- Single-use paper towels
- Pedal or sensor-operated taps preferred (to avoid re-contamination after washing)

### When to Wash Hands

Wash hands:
- Before entering any food handling area
- Before starting work and after every break
- After handling raw food (meat, fish, vegetables, eggs)
- After touching face, hair, nose, or mouth
- After coughing or sneezing (even into a tissue)
- After handling waste, refuse, or dirty items
- After using the toilet (handwash in toilet facility AND on re-entry to food area)
- After handling money, keys, or personal items
- After handling cleaning materials
- After removing gloves
- When switching between allergen-containing and allergen-free tasks
- After handling chemicals

### 20-Second Handwashing Method

1. Wet hands under running water
2. Apply liquid soap
3. Lather and scrub:
   - Rub palms together
   - Rub backs of both hands
   - Interlock fingers and scrub between fingers
   - Rub fingertips against palms
   - Rub around thumbs
   - Rub around wrists
   - **Continue scrubbing for at least 20 seconds**
4. Rinse thoroughly under running water
5. Dry completely with a single-use paper towel
6. Use the paper towel to turn off the tap

> Damp or incompletely dried hands transfer bacteria more readily than dry hands. Dry thoroughly.

## 5. Protective Clothing

### Entering the Food Area

Before entering any food production or preparation area, food handlers must:

1. Remove outdoor clothing
2. Change into clean work uniform (whites, apron, or dedicated uniform as required for the area)
3. Fit a clean hairnet covering all hair, including the nape of the neck; a beard snood if required
4. Put on designated footwear
5. Remove all jewellery except a plain wedding band
6. Wash hands

### Leaving the Food Area

When leaving the food area temporarily (e.g. for a break or toilet visit):
- Remove apron (hang in designated area or place in laundry)
- Remove gloves and discard
- Wash hands before re-entering and repeat the entry procedure above

### Protective Clothing Rules
- Uniform is worn only in the food business — not in public (protect from environmental contamination)
- Store uniform separately from outdoor clothing in a dedicated locker or changing area
- Uniform washed at ≥60°C; provided/laundered by the business or by approved commercial laundry
- Uniform changed when visibly soiled; at minimum daily

## 6. Jewellery, Nails, and Personal Items

### Prohibited in Food Areas
- All jewellery except a plain wedding band (no stone settings)
- Watches (including smart watches)
- Nail varnish and false/acrylic/gel nails
- Long nails (must be kept short and clean)
- Piercings (facial, tongue, ear stretchers, visible body piercings)
- False eyelashes
- Strong perfume or aftershave (can taint food)

### Permitted
- Plain wedding band (covered by glove if worn)
- Plain sleeper earrings only if fully covered by hairnet
- Medical alert bracelets (must be covered or risk assessed)

### Mobile Phones and Personal Items
- Mobile phones and personal devices are **not permitted** in food handling areas
- Used in break rooms or designated areas only
- Food and drinks are consumed only in designated break areas — never in food preparation areas
- Smoking in designated outdoor areas only; wash hands on return

## 7. Cuts, Wounds, and Skin Conditions

### Cuts and Wounds
1. Report all cuts, sores, or skin infections on hands or exposed areas to the supervisor before work
2. All cuts on hands must be covered with a **blue waterproof plaster** before working
3. Blue colour makes lost plasters visible; metal-detectable plasters used where metal detection is in place
4. Plasters are recorded on the **plaster log** (count at start and end of each shift by supervisor)
5. If a plaster is missing during a shift: **stop work immediately; quarantine product handled since last check; attempt to locate plaster; inform supervisor**
6. Heavily infected wounds, boils, or sores on exposed skin: supervisor to assess whether exclusion is required

### Skin Conditions
- Eczema, psoriasis, or dermatitis affecting hands: supervisor must risk assess before placing handler on open food work; gloves may be required
- Infected skin conditions: excluded from open food handling until cleared

## 8. Fitness to Work — Illness Exclusion

### Conditions Requiring Immediate Exclusion

| Condition | Exclusion Period |
|---|---|
| Diarrhoea | 48 hours after last episode (both diarrhoea and vomiting resolved) |
| Vomiting | 48 hours after last episode |
| Diarrhoea and vomiting combined (suspected norovirus) | 48 hours after both fully resolved |
| Confirmed Salmonella, E. coli O157, or other notifiable enteric pathogen | Until two clear stool samples 24 hours apart (obtain advice from Health Protection Team) |
| Confirmed Typhoid / Paratyphoid | Until medically cleared |
| Suspected Hepatitis A | Until medically cleared |
| Infected sore throat / tonsillitis | Supervisor risk assessment; may be excluded from high-risk work |

### Reporting Procedure
1. If an employee becomes ill before their shift: they must **phone in before arriving** — do not come to work and then inform the supervisor
2. Supervisor completes an **illness notification record** (date, name, symptoms, last shift worked)
3. Supervisor assesses whether other employees or products may be affected
4. Consider whether product produced by the employee during their symptomatic period requires investigation

### Return to Work
1. Employee contacts supervisor before returning
2. Supervisor confirms the exclusion period has been met:
   - GI illness: 48 hours minimum after resolution of symptoms (confirm no further episodes)
   - Notifiable disease: written medical clearance required
3. **Return-to-work interview** conducted and recorded (date, who conducted it, outcome)
4. Employee washes hands before recommencing food handling duties
5. If employee returns before exclusion period: send home; do not allow food handling

## 9. Visitor Hygiene

All visitors to food production and preparation areas must:

1. Receive a **hygiene briefing** before entry
2. Complete and sign a **visitor hygiene declaration** (confirming no current GI symptoms; no food-borne illness in past 48 hours; no open wounds on hands)
3. Be issued with: hairnet, overshoes or designated footwear, overalls or disposable coat
4. Be accompanied by a designated food business representative at all times
5. Wash hands before entering the food area
6. Not touch food or food-contact surfaces unless specifically required
7. Visitor PPE returned, disposed of, or laundered after the visit

---

## 10. Training

All food handlers must receive personal hygiene training:
- **On induction** — before working unsupervised with food
- **Refresher** — at minimum every 3 years, or when there is an incident, new hazard, or process change
- Training must include: this SOP, handwashing procedure, fitness-to-work rules, allergen awareness, protective clothing requirements

Training records retained for duration of employment + 3 years.
