## 1. Purpose

To prevent cross-contamination between raw and cooked foods, allergens, and other hazards through physical separation, workflow design, and hygiene controls in compliance with EC 852/2004.

## 2. Scope

Applies to all food handling activities including storage, preparation, cooking, and service areas.

## 3. Separation Requirements

### Physical Separation

**High-risk ready-to-eat areas** must be physically separated from raw food preparation:
- Separate rooms or clearly defined zones
- Separate equipment and utensils (colour-coded)
- Separate storage (fridges, freezers, dry stores)
- Separate sinks (handwashing, food prep, equipment washing)

### Workflow Design

- **Linear flow:** Raw → Preparation → Cooking → Cooling → Ready-to-eat
- No crossing of raw and cooked product routes
- Staff assigned to specific zones (do not move between raw and ready-to-eat without changing PPE and handwashing)

---

## 4. Colour-Coded Equipment

| Colour | Use Area |
|---|---|
| **Red** | Raw meat and poultry |
| **Blue** | Raw fish and seafood |
| **Yellow** | Cooked and ready-to-eat food |
| **Green** | Fruit, salad, vegetables (ready-to-eat) |
| **Brown** | Vegetables (to be cooked) |
| **White** | Bakery and dairy |

**Items colour-coded:** Chopping boards, knives, containers, cleaning cloths, tongs, preparation bowls.

**Storage:** Store colour-coded items separately; label storage areas clearly.

---

## 5. Storage Controls

### Fridge Storage Hierarchy

**Top to bottom:**
1. **Top shelf:** Ready-to-eat food, cooked food (covered)
2. **Middle shelves:** Prepared vegetables, dairy, eggs
3. **Bottom shelf:** Raw meat, poultry, fish (in sealed containers; prevent drips)

**Rules:**
- All food covered or in sealed containers
- No overstocking (allow air circulation)
- Clean up spills immediately

### Dry Storage

- Raw ingredients separate from ready-to-eat items
- High-risk allergens (nuts, sesame) stored in sealed containers; clearly labelled
- Stock rotation (FIFO); check for pest damage

---

## 6. Personal Hygiene and PPE

- Wash hands when moving from raw to ready-to-eat tasks
- Change apron or wear colour-coded aprons per zone
- Change gloves between tasks (gloves not a substitute for handwashing)
- Remove jewellery; tie back hair; cover cuts with blue plasters

---

## 7. Cleaning Between Tasks

- Clean and sanitise surfaces after preparing raw food and before ready-to-eat food
- Use separate cloths (colour-coded)
- Sanitise knives, boards, and equipment between uses
- Wash hands

---

## 8. Allergen Cross-Contamination

- Dedicated equipment for allergen-free production (or clean and verify)
- Separate storage for allergenic ingredients
- Clean surfaces thoroughly when switching between allergen-containing and allergen-free batches
- See `SOP-allergen-management.md` for full procedures

---

## 9. Training

All staff trained on:
- Cross-contamination risks
- Colour-coded system
- Workflow and zone restrictions
- Handwashing and cleaning procedures

**Frequency:** Induction + annual refresher.

---

## 10. Review

Reviewed annually or following contamination incidents.
