## 1. Purpose

To ensure all food contact materials (FCMs) used in the food business are legally compliant, suitable for their intended use, correctly maintained, and replaced when necessary — preventing chemical, physical, or microbiological contamination of food.

## 2. Scope

All materials and articles that come into direct or indirect contact with food, including:
- Cutting boards, utensils, trays, containers, pots, pans
- Food packaging materials (bags, film, boxes, wrapping)
- Equipment gaskets, seals, and conveyor belts
- Gloves used during food preparation
- Single-use items (cups, plates, cutlery, food boxes)

## 3. Legal Basis

- EC/UK Regulation (EC) No 1935/2004 — framework regulation for FCMs
- EU/UK Regulation 10/2011 — specific rules for plastic FCMs
- EC 852/2004 — general inertness and hygiene requirements for food-contact equipment

---

## 4. Purchasing FCMs

When procuring new FCMs:

**Step 1: Confirm the item is suitable for food contact**
- Look for the **glass-and-fork symbol** or "for food contact" text on the product or packaging
- If purchasing online or from a non-food specialist supplier, explicitly confirm food-contact suitability before purchasing

**Step 2: Confirm suitability for intended use**
- Check the stated temperature range (e.g. "freezer safe", "microwave safe", "max 120°C")
- Check suitability for the food type (acidic, fatty, aqueous, dry)
- For plastic items: confirm plastic type and whether it is appropriate (PP and HDPE preferred for general food use; avoid PVC with fatty foods; avoid PS for hot/fatty foods)

**Step 3: Obtain a Declaration of Compliance (DoC)**
- For all plastic FCMs: request a DoC from the supplier confirming compliance with Regulation 10/2011
- For other materials: request a written statement confirming compliance with EC 1935/2004
- Retain the DoC on file

**Step 4: Verify colour-coding alignment**
If the item is a cutting board, utensil, or food-contact surface that will be colour-coded:
- Confirm it matches the colour-coding scheme in use at the premises
- Add to the colour-coding chart if a new category

---

## 5. Receiving FCMs

When FCM deliveries arrive:

1. Inspect packaging for damage
2. Check the glass-and-fork symbol or food-contact marking is present on the item or outer packaging
3. Confirm items match the purchase order (correct material type, grade, and size)
4. Check for any cracking, discolouration, or visible damage to the items
5. Reject and return damaged or unidentified items
6. File the DoC with the supplier record

---

## 6. Storing FCMs

- Store FCMs in a clean, dry location, protected from contamination
- Packaging materials: stored above floor level on racking; not near chemicals or waste
- Cutting boards and utensils: stored in designated location, clean and dry, not stacked directly on the floor
- Single-use items: kept in original packaging until needed; not stored near allergens where cross-contact could contaminate the items

---

## 7. Daily Inspection of FCMs Before Use

Before each use, visually inspect each FCM:

| Defect | Action |
|---|---|
| Cracks or chips | Remove from use immediately; dispose of |
| Deep scoring (cutting boards) | Remove from use; replace |
| Flaking or peeling coating (non-stick pans, lacquered surfaces) | Remove from use immediately; dispose of |
| Discolouration or strong unusual odour | Remove from use; investigate; replace if persistent |
| Rust or corrosion (metal items) | Remove from use; clean and inspect; replace if rust cannot be removed |
| Damaged or compressed seals/gaskets | Notify maintenance; do not use equipment until replaced |
| Loss of colour-code clarity | Re-label or replace item |

Items removed from use must be:
- Quarantined and labelled "Not for use — awaiting disposal/repair"
- Disposed of or repaired before being returned to service

---

## 8. Cleaning and Maintenance

- Clean all reusable FCMs immediately after use as per the cleaning schedule (SOP-CL-001)
- Do not use abrasive scourers on non-stick coatings
- Do not use metal utensils in or on non-stick surfaces
- Plastic cutting boards and containers: dishwasher at ≥60°C where rated; or manual hot water + detergent + disinfect
- Stainless steel items: hot water + food-safe detergent; dry to prevent water staining and scale build-up
- Rubber seals and gaskets: disassemble where possible; clean and inspect; reassemble per manufacturer instructions

---

## 9. Using Single-Use FCMs

- Single-use items (disposable cups, plates, cutlery, packaging) must only be used once
- Do not reuse single-use plastic containers (e.g. takeaway trays) for food storage — they are not designed for repeated use and may migrate more on re-heating or repeated contact
- Single-use gloves: inspect before putting on (check for tears); change between tasks and allergen types; never wash and re-use

---

## 10. Colour-Coded Equipment Management

| Colour | Food Type | Storage Location |
|---|---|---|
| Red | Raw meat | Raw meat preparation station |
| Blue | Raw fish and seafood | Fish preparation station |
| Yellow | Cooked meat and poultry | Cooked food area |
| Green | Salads, fruit, vegetables | Salad/veg prep station |
| White | Dairy, bakery, general | Central prep area |
| Brown | Root vegetables, soiled produce | Veg prep sink area |
| Purple (if used) | Allergen-free / free-from | Allergen-free preparation zone |

- Equipment must only leave its designated colour area when being transported to the dishwasher/cleaning area
- Never use red equipment for green tasks or vice versa, even if the item has been cleaned
- Staff must be trained on the colour code system at induction

---

## 11. Non-Conformance

| Situation | Action |
|---|---|
| FCM without food-contact marking found in use | Remove from use; investigate source; do not return to use unless food-contact status confirmed |
| DoC not available for plastic FCM | Obtain DoC from supplier before continued use; if unavailable, replace with compliant FCM |
| Damaged FCM found | Remove from use; quarantine; investigate whether contamination of food has occurred; document |
| Non-food-contact item used with food (e.g. industrial container) | Remove from use; investigate; consider product recall if contamination risk is significant; document |

---

## 12. Records

| Record | Responsible | Frequency | Retention |
|---|---|---|---|
| FCM Declarations of Compliance | Purchasing / technical | Per supplier / product | 5 years or shelf life + 6 months |
| Daily inspection results (damaged items) | Supervisor / food handler | When defect found | 2 years |
| Equipment disposal records | Supervisor | When item disposed | 2 years |
| Cleaning records (reusable FCMs) | Cleaning operative | Per cleaning event | 3 months |
| Equipment maintenance records (seals, gaskets) | Maintenance | Per maintenance event | 5 years |

## 13. Review

This SOP is reviewed:
- Annually
- When new FCM types are introduced
- Following any incident related to FCM contamination or non-compliance
- When legislation changes
