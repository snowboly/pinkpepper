## Introduction

This schedule lists all cleaning and disinfection tasks required to maintain hygiene standards in accordance with EC 852/2004, Annex II and the premises' HACCP plan.

### Cleaning vs. Disinfection

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| **Cleaning** | Removal of dirt, grease, and food residues from a surface |
| **Disinfection** | Reduction of microorganisms to a level that does not pose a public health risk |
| **Sanitising** | Combined cleaning and disinfection in one step (where a combined product is validated for the surface type and soil level) |

Most food-contact surfaces require **both cleaning and disinfection** as a two-step process.

### Cleaning Method Key

| Code | Method |
|---|---|
| **M1** | Manual wash: hot water (≥60°C) + detergent; rinse; apply disinfectant; contact time; rinse if required |
| **M2** | Foam application: apply foam detergent; dwell time; rinse; apply disinfectant; contact time; rinse if required |
| **M3** | Dishwasher: minimum 60°C wash cycle |
| **M4** | CIP (Clean in Place): automated detergent + rinse + disinfectant cycle |
| **M5** | Dry clean only: brush off debris; wipe with damp sanitiser-impregnated cloth; no water immersion |
| **M6** | External surfaces: damp wipe with all-purpose cleaner or disinfectant |

## Verification Methods

| Method | What It Tests | When Used |
|---|---|---|
| **Visual inspection** | Surface cleanliness; no visible debris | After every clean; supervisor checks |
| **ATP bioluminescence (Hygiene Testing)** | Organic residue (not allergen-specific; not micro-specific) | Post-clean verification; periodic monitoring |
| **Allergen lateral flow swab test** | Specific allergen residue (e.g. gluten, peanut, milk) | After allergen cleaning changeover; cleaning validation |
| **Microbiological surface swab (environmental)** | Pathogen / indicator organism levels (Listeria, E. coli, TVC) | Monthly/quarterly in high-care areas; after remediation |
| **Protein swab (biuret / colorimetric)** | Residual protein (non-specific) | Quick check for protein removal in allergen cleaning |

### ATP Cleaning Pass/Fail Targets

| Surface Category | Pass | Borderline | Fail |
|---|---|---|---|
| Food-contact surfaces (high-risk) | <10 RLU | 10–25 RLU | >25 RLU |
| Food-contact surfaces (general) | <50 RLU | 50–100 RLU | >100 RLU |
| Environmental (non-food contact) | <100 RLU | 100–200 RLU | >200 RLU |

_(RLU values may vary by ATP meter brand — calibrate to your own system)_

## Corrective Actions — Cleaning Failures

If a surface fails verification (visual or ATP/swab):
1. **Re-clean** the surface using the full procedure
2. **Re-verify** before food production resumes
3. **Record** the failure and corrective action
4. **Investigate** cause (wrong chemical, wrong dilution, missed step, damaged surface)
5. **Retrain** operative if procedural failure identified
6. **Review** cleaning procedure if systematic failure identified
