## 1. Policy Statement

_[Business Name]_ requires all staff involved in food handling to maintain the highest standards of personal hygiene at all times. This policy ensures compliance with **Regulation (EC) 852/2004, Annex II, Chapter VIII** and protects both consumers and staff from food safety hazards.

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to:
- All employees, agency workers, contractors, and volunteers who handle food or enter food handling areas
- All food preparation, storage, service, and cleaning areas

## 3. Handwashing Procedure

### 3.1 When to Wash Hands

Hands must be washed:
- On entering a food handling area
- After using the toilet
- After handling raw food (especially raw meat, poultry, fish, eggs)
- After handling waste or waste bins
- After cleaning tasks
- After touching face, hair, nose, or mouth
- After sneezing, coughing, or blowing nose
- After smoking, eating, or drinking
- After handling packaging, cardboard, or non-food items
- After handling money
- After any break from food handling

### 3.2 How to Wash Hands

1. Wet hands under warm running water
2. Apply liquid antibacterial soap from a dispenser
3. Rub hands together for at least **20 seconds** — covering all surfaces (palms, backs of hands, between fingers, thumbs, fingertips, wrists)
4. Rinse thoroughly under clean running water
5. Dry hands completely using **single-use paper towels** or a warm-air dryer
6. Use a paper towel to turn off taps (if not sensor-operated)

> Alcohol hand gel/rub may be used as a supplement but **never as a replacement** for handwashing with soap and water.

### 3.3 Handwash Facilities

Handwash basins must be:
- Dedicated for handwashing only (not used for food preparation or equipment washing)
- Supplied with hot and cold (or warm) running water
- Stocked with liquid antibacterial soap and paper towels at all times
- Located at entry to food areas and near toilet facilities

## 4. Protective Clothing

| Item | Requirement |
|---|---|
| **Clean uniform / whites** | Changed daily or when soiled; not worn outside the premises |
| **Apron** | Clean apron worn over uniform during food handling; changed when soiled or between tasks |
| **Hair covering** | Hair net, hat, or snood covering all head hair; beard snoods for facial hair |
| **Footwear** | Clean, enclosed, non-slip safety shoes; not worn outside the premises |
| **Gloves** (if used) | Single-use; changed between tasks and when torn; hands washed before and after use |

> Protective clothing must be stored separately from outdoor/personal clothing.

---

## 5. Jewellery and Personal Items

| Item | Rule |
|---|---|
| **Jewellery** | No jewellery to be worn in food areas (exception: plain wedding band and sleeper earrings, subject to risk assessment) |
| **Watches** | Not permitted in food handling areas |
| **Nail polish / false nails** | Not permitted |
| **Nails** | Short, clean, unvarnished |
| **Perfume / aftershave** | Strong fragrances avoided; risk of taint to food |
| **Mobile phones** | Not permitted in food handling areas |
| **Personal medication** | Stored in locker; not brought into food areas (except epinephrine auto-injectors with manager approval) |

## 6. Illness Reporting and Fitness to Work

### 6.1 Reporting Requirements

Staff must report the following symptoms to their supervisor **before starting work**:

| Symptom / Condition | Action |
|---|---|
| Vomiting | **Exclude** from food handling; do not return until **48 hours** symptom-free |
| Diarrhoea | **Exclude** from food handling; do not return until **48 hours** symptom-free |
| Nausea / stomach cramps | Assess; exclude if vomiting/diarrhoea develops |
| Jaundice | **Exclude**; seek medical advice; notify EHO |
| Sore throat with fever | Assess; exclude from open food handling |
| Infected skin lesion (boils, septic cuts, infected wound) | **Exclude** from food handling or cover with detectable (blue) waterproof dressing and glove |
| Ear, eye, or nose discharge | Assess; exclude if discharge cannot be contained |
| Contact with infectious disease (household member) | Assess; monitor for symptoms; exclude if symptoms develop |
| Recent travel to high-risk area with GI illness | Assess; stool testing may be required |

### 6.2 Return to Work

Staff excluded due to GI illness (particularly Salmonella, E. coli O157, Campylobacter, Shigella, Hepatitis A, Cryptosporidium) must:
- Be symptom-free for **48 hours minimum** before returning to food handling duties
- Obtain GP/medical clearance if required (especially for confirmed notifiable organisms)
- Complete a return-to-work fitness declaration

## 7. Cuts and Wounds Procedure

1. **Report** any cut, wound, or skin abrasion to supervisor immediately
2. **Clean** the wound thoroughly
3. **Cover** with a **blue detectable waterproof dressing** (metal-detectable type preferred)
4. **Wear a disposable glove** over the dressing if the wound is on a hand
5. **Change** the dressing regularly and if it becomes wet, loose, or soiled
6. **Exclude** from food handling if the wound cannot be adequately covered (e.g. large weeping wound, infected cut)

> Blue dressings must be used so they are visible if they fall into food. Metal-detectable dressings provide additional protection in production environments.

## 8. Visitor and Contractor Hygiene Rules

All visitors and contractors entering food handling areas must:
- ☐ Sign in at reception
- ☐ Be briefed on hygiene requirements before entry
- ☐ Wear protective clothing (provided): coat, hair net, overshoes (as required)
- ☐ Remove jewellery and watches
- ☐ Wash hands on entry to food areas
- ☐ Be accompanied by a member of staff at all times
- ☐ Declare any illness symptoms (vomiting, diarrhoea) — entry refused if symptomatic

## 9. Monitoring and Enforcement

| Check | Frequency | Responsible |
|---|---|---|
| Visual hygiene checks (clothing, hair nets, handwashing) | Every shift | Supervisor |
| Handwash station supplies check | Daily | Cleaning operative / supervisor |
| Staff illness log review | Weekly | Manager |
| Hygiene training refresher | Annually (or on concern) | Manager / Trainer |
| Audit of protective clothing condition | Monthly | Supervisor |

Non-compliance will be addressed through:
1. Verbal reminder and retraining (first instance)
2. Written warning (repeat offence)
3. Disciplinary action (persistent non-compliance or serious breach)
