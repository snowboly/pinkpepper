## 1. Purpose

This SOP defines the cleaning and disinfection procedures required to maintain food safety and hygiene standards across all operational areas. It ensures compliance with **Regulation (EC) 852/2004, Annex II, Chapter I** and supports the premises' HACCP plan.

---

## 2. Scope

This procedure applies to:
- All food preparation, storage, and service areas
- All food-contact surfaces, equipment, and utensils
- Non-food-contact surfaces (floors, walls, ceilings, drains)
- Welfare areas (toilets, changing rooms, handwash stations)

## 4. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| **Cleaning** | Removal of dirt, grease, and food residues from a surface |
| **Disinfection** | Reduction of microorganisms to a level that does not compromise food safety |
| **Sanitising** | Combined cleaning and disinfection in one step (where a validated product is used) |

## 6. Step-by-Step Cleaning Procedure

### 6.1 Standard Two-Stage Clean (Food-Contact Surfaces)

1. **Remove** loose debris and food residues (scrape, sweep, or wipe)
2. **Pre-rinse** with warm water to loosen remaining soil
3. **Apply detergent** at correct dilution using a clean cloth or spray
4. **Scrub / agitate** to lift grease and residues; pay attention to joints and edges
5. **Rinse** thoroughly with clean water to remove detergent
6. **Apply disinfectant** at correct dilution; allow full contact time as per product label
7. **Final rinse** (if required by product instructions)
8. **Air-dry** — do not use tea towels on sanitised surfaces

### 6.2 Non-Food-Contact Surfaces (Floors, Walls, External Equipment)

1. Sweep or vacuum to remove debris
2. Mop or wipe with detergent solution
3. Rinse if required
4. Apply disinfectant where specified (e.g. toilet areas, handwash stations)
5. Allow to air-dry

## 7. Cleaning Frequency Schedule

|---|---|---|---|
| Food preparation worktops | Two-stage clean | Between tasks and end of day | Food handlers |

## 8. Verification

### 8.1 Visual Inspection

- Supervisors must visually inspect cleaned surfaces after each shift
- Any visible soil, grease, or residues = fail → re-clean before production

### 8.2 ATP Bioluminescence Testing

| Surface Category | Pass (RLU) | Borderline (RLU) | Fail (RLU) |
|---|---|---|---|
| Food-contact (high-risk) | <10 | 10–25 | >25 |
| Food-contact (general) | <50 | 50–100 | >100 |
| Environmental | <100 | 100–200 | >200 |

Frequency: _[e.g. Weekly spot checks on food-contact surfaces]_

- Environmental swabs (Listeria, E. coli, TVC) in high-care areas: _[e.g. Monthly]_
- Results reviewed by: _[Technical / QA Manager]_

---

## 9. Corrective Actions

If a surface fails visual or ATP/swab verification:

1. **Re-clean** using the full procedure
2. **Re-verify** before food production resumes
3. **Record** the failure and corrective action taken
4. **Investigate** root cause (wrong dilution, missed step, damaged surface, untrained operative)
5. **Retrain** operative if procedural failure identified
6. **Review** SOP if systematic or repeat failures occur
