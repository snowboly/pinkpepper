## 3. Legal Requirements

| Legislation | Key Requirement |
|---|---|
| **Regulation (EC) 852/2004, Annex II, Chapter VI** | Food waste must be removed from rooms containing food as quickly as possible; waste must not be a source of contamination |
| **Environmental Protection Act 1990, Part II** | Duty of care for waste — businesses must store, transfer, and dispose of waste safely and legally |
| **Waste (England and Wales) Regulations 2011** | Waste hierarchy: prevent, reuse, recycle, recover, dispose |
| **Animal By-Products Regulations (EC) 1069/2009** | Category 3 animal by-products (catering waste) must be handled by approved carriers |
| **Hazardous Waste Regulations 2005** | Hazardous waste (oils, chemicals) must be segregated and disposed of via licensed carriers |
| **The Controlled Waste Regulations 2012** | Classification of waste types and disposal routes |

## 6. Internal Waste Handling

### 6.1 During Operations
- Food waste bins must be lined and have self-closing or foot-pedal lids
- Bins must be emptied when ¾ full or at end of each service/shift (whichever is sooner)
- Hands must be washed after handling waste
- Waste must never be placed on or near food preparation surfaces

### 6.2 Transfer to External Storage
- Waste transferred to external bins/containers at end of each day (minimum)
- External bins must have tight-fitting lids to prevent pest access
- Waste storage area must be kept clean; spillages cleaned immediately
- External area must be hard-surfaced, well-drained, and away from food entry points

## 8. Duty of Care

The business must ensure:
- ☐ All waste carriers hold a valid **waste carrier licence** (check via Environment Agency public register)
- ☐ **Waste transfer notes** are obtained for every collection and retained for **2 years** (general waste) or **3 years** (hazardous waste consignment notes)
- ☐ Waste is accurately described on transfer notes
- ☐ Waste is only transferred to authorised persons at licensed facilities
- ☐ Animal by-product waste carriers hold **APHA approval** (Category 3)

## 10. Corrective Actions

| Issue | Action |
|---|---|
| Overflowing bins in food areas | Empty immediately; clean surrounding area; review bin size or emptying frequency |
| Pest activity near waste area | Deep clean; review bin condition and lid closure; notify pest control contractor |
| Missed collection | Contact carrier; store waste securely; increase next collection if needed |
| Missing or expired waste transfer notes | Contact carrier for duplicate; verify licence validity |
| Waste contamination (wrong stream in wrong bin) | Re-sort if possible; retrain staff on segregation |
