# FSSC 22000 Food Safety System Certification — Summary

## Overview

**FSSC 22000 (Food Safety System Certification 22000)** is a GFSI-benchmarked food safety management system certification scheme. It is owned and managed by the **Foundation FSSC 22000**, a not-for-profit organisation based in the Netherlands.

FSSC 22000 builds on **ISO 22000:2018** by adding sector-specific **Prerequisite Programme (PRP) requirements** (ISO/TS 22002-x series) and **FSSC Additional Requirements**. The combination creates a comprehensive, GFSI-recognized scheme accepted by major global retailers and food businesses.

**Current version:** FSSC 22000 Version 6 (v6), published January 2023; mandatory from January 2024.

**GFSI recognition:** Yes — benchmarked against GFSI Guidance Document Seventh Edition. Accepted by Walmart, Tesco, Ahold Delhaize, Metro, and most other major global retailers.

**Scope:** Applicable across the entire food chain:
- Pre-farm gate (primary production)
- Feed production
- Food manufacturing and processing
- Storage and distribution
- Transport
- Retail and foodservice
- Packaging and packaging material manufacturing
- Biochemical sector

## Scheme Structure

FSSC 22000 v6 comprises three components:

### Component 1: ISO 22000:2018

The full requirements of ISO 22000:2018 apply (all clauses 4–10). This covers:
- Context of the organization (Clause 4)
- Leadership and food safety policy (Clause 5)
- Planning and risk/opportunity assessment (Clause 6)
- Support (resources, competence, communication, documented information) (Clause 7)
- Operation: PRPs, hazard analysis, HACCP plan, OPRPs (Clause 8)
- Performance evaluation (internal audits, management review) (Clause 9)
- Improvement and corrective action (Clause 10)

### Component 2: Sector-Specific PRP Standards (ISO/TS 22002-x)

FSSC 22000 requires sector-appropriate PRPs from the ISO/TS 22002 series:

| Sector | Applicable Standard |
|---|---|
| Food manufacturing | ISO/TS 22002-1:2009 |
| Catering | ISO/TS 22002-2:2013 |
| Farming (primary production) | ISO/TS 22002-3:2011 |
| Food packaging manufacturing | ISO/TS 22002-4:2013 |
| Feed and animal food production | ISO/TS 22002-6:2016 |
| Biochemical sector | ISO/TS 22002-6 (adapted) |

These PRP standards provide detailed, sector-specific requirements for infrastructure, utilities, waste management, equipment suitability, cleaning and sanitation, pest control, personal hygiene, and cross-contamination prevention.

### Component 3: FSSC Additional Requirements (v6)

These are requirements specific to FSSC 22000, beyond ISO 22000 and the PRPs:

1. **Food safety culture** — documented culture programme with action plans, measurement, and review; demonstrable senior management leadership
2. **Food fraud (VACCP)** — vulnerability assessment and critical control points for economically motivated adulteration; mitigation measures integrated into the FSMS
3. **Food defence** — threat assessment and critical control points (TACCP); site security measures to prevent malicious contamination
4. **Allergen management** — formal allergen risk assessment; segregation, cleaning validation, labelling controls; staff training
5. **Environmental monitoring** — programme for detecting environmental pathogens (e.g., Listeria, Salmonella) in relevant processing environments
6. **Management of services and purchased materials** — controls for outsourced services and materials that affect food safety
7. **Labelling and consumer/customer awareness** — labelling complies with applicable legislation; consumer communication on food safety
8. **Product design and development** — food safety requirements incorporated at the design stage for new and modified products
9. **Quality management (v6 enhancement)** — explicit quality management requirements integrated alongside food safety requirements
10. **Logo use** — FSSC 22000 logo and certification marks used in accordance with scheme rules

## FSSC 22000 vs. ISO 22000

| Feature | ISO 22000:2018 | FSSC 22000 v6 |
|---|---|---|
| **GFSI recognition** | No | Yes |
| **Sector-specific PRPs** | Not specified (organization selects) | Required (ISO/TS 22002-x series) |
| **Food fraud (VACCP)** | Not explicitly required | Required (Additional Requirement) |
| **Food defence (TACCP)** | Not explicitly required | Required (Additional Requirement) |
| **Food safety culture** | Mentioned (Clause 5) | Formal documented programme required |
| **Allergen management** | Via PRP / hazard analysis | Explicit standalone requirement |
| **Environmental monitoring** | Not explicitly required | Required (Additional Requirement) |
| **Retailer acceptance** | Limited (GFSI not recognized) | Accepted by most major global retailers |
| **Audit frequency** | Annual surveillance + 3-year recert | Annual + mid-term surveillance |
| **Certification body accreditation** | ISO 17021 | ISO 17021 + FSSC 22000 approval |

## FSSC 22000 Certification Process

### 1. Preparation and Gap Analysis
- Assess current FSMS against ISO 22000, relevant ISO/TS 22002 PRP standard, and FSSC Additional Requirements
- Identify and address gaps
- Establish food safety culture programme, VACCP, TACCP, allergen controls, and environmental monitoring

### 2. Selecting a Certification Body
- Must be an FSSC 22000-approved certification body (listed on FSSC 22000 website)
- Certification body must be accredited to ISO 17021 by an IAF-recognized accreditation body

### 3. Stage 1 Audit (Documentation Review)
- Auditor reviews FSMS documentation: food safety policy, HACCP plan, PRP procedures, VACCP, TACCP, allergen risk assessment, environmental monitoring programme
- Identifies gaps or areas requiring improvement before Stage 2

### 4. Stage 2 Audit (Certification Audit)
- Full on-site audit: inspection of premises, processes, records, staff interviews
- Verification that all FSSC 22000 requirements are implemented and effective
- Non-conformities raised and categorized

### 5. Non-Conformance Management

| Category | Description | Timeframe |
|---|---|---|
| **Critical** | Immediate food safety risk or complete system failure | Immediate; may suspend audit |
| **Major** | Significant failure to meet a requirement | 28 calendar days |
| **Minor** | Partial failure; isolated or low-risk issue | 3 months (or before surveillance) |

### 6. Certification Decision
- Certification body reviews audit report and corrective action evidence
- If satisfactory: **FSSC 22000 certificate issued**
- Certificate registered on the FSSC 22000 database (publicly searchable)

**Certificate validity:** 3 years (with annual surveillance audits)

### 7. Surveillance Audits
- **Annual surveillance audit** (unannounced, mandatory from v5 onwards for manufacturing)
- Focused on critical requirements: food safety culture, VACCP, TACCP, allergen management, environmental monitoring
- Unannounced audit announced within a 4-week window maximum

### 8. Recertification
- Full recertification audit before 3-year certificate expiry
- Covers all requirements in full

## FSSC 22000 Grading

FSSC 22000 does **not** use a graded certification system (unlike BRCGS AA/A/B/C). Sites either:
- **Pass** — certificate issued
- **Fail** — certificate not issued or suspended (due to unresolved majors or criticals)

Certificates may be **suspended** (if corrective actions not resolved within required timescales) or **withdrawn** (for repeated major failures or critical non-conformances).

## FSSC 22000 Additional Requirements — Version 6 Key Changes vs. Version 5.1

1. **Quality management** — new dedicated additional requirement for quality management system
2. **Food safety culture** — stronger requirements; must include measurement tools and senior management review outcomes
3. **Environmental monitoring** — more explicit scope and frequency requirements
4. **Allergen management** — standalone requirement reinforced; cleaning validation now explicitly included
5. **VACCP** — aligned with updated GFSI guidance; must address supply chain vulnerabilities
6. **Unannounced audits** — extended to broader range of sectors (was primarily manufacturing)

## Who Should Use FSSC 22000?

FSSC 22000 is ideal for:
- **Manufacturers seeking GFSI-recognized certification** who already have or want an ISO-framework management system
- **Suppliers to major global retailers** (Walmart, Tesco, Ahold, Metro) who require GFSI benchmarked certification
- **Organizations integrating food safety with ISO 9001 or ISO 14001** (compatible HLS structure)
- **Companies in multiple regions** wanting a globally accepted, single certification
- **Organizations seeking a structured approach** to food fraud, food defence, and allergen management within one FSMS
