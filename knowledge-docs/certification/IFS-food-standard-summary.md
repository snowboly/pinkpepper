# IFS Food Standard — Version 8 Summary

## Overview

**IFS Food (International Featured Standards Food)** is a GFSI-benchmarked food safety and quality certification scheme developed by German and French retail associations (HDE and FCD). It is widely used across Europe and increasingly recognised globally, particularly by European retailers.

**Current version:** IFS Food Version 8 (v8), published January 2023; mandatory from March 2024.

**Scheme owner:** IFS Management GmbH (Berlin, Germany)

**GFSI recognition:** Yes — benchmarked against GFSI Guidance Document Seventh Edition.

**Market focus:** Primarily Europe; accepted by major European retailers including Carrefour, Aldi, Lidl, Rewe, Edeka, and others.

**Scope:** Applicable to food manufacturers and processors (post-farm gate) who produce or process food products. Separate IFS standards exist for logistics, brokers, cash & carry, and wholesale.

---

## Key Features

- **Combined food safety and quality:** IFS Food addresses both food safety and product quality within the same standard, unlike BRCGS (primarily safety) or ISO 22000 (primarily safety)
- **Knock-Out (KO) requirements:** 10 critical clauses; a score of zero on any KO results in automatic audit failure
- **Numerical scoring system:** Each clause scored from A (full compliance) to D (not implemented), converted to a percentage score
- **Two certification levels:** Higher Level (full compliance) and Foundation Level (partial compliance)
- **GFSI recognized:** Accepted by major European retailers as proof of food safety management

---

## Structure of IFS Food v8

IFS Food is organized into **6 chapters** covering food safety and quality management:

### Chapter 1: Senior Management Responsibility

#### 1.1 Corporate Policy / Corporate Principles
- Senior management documents and communicates food safety and quality policy
- Objectives set, monitored, and reviewed at planned intervals
- Food safety culture actively promoted and measured

#### 1.2 Corporate Structure
- Organizational chart defined; responsibilities and authorities clearly documented
- Food safety and quality team appointed with competent leader
- Substitution arrangements documented for key roles

#### 1.3 Customer Focus
- Customer requirements identified and met
- Customer satisfaction monitored
- Complaint management system in place

### Chapter 2: Quality and Food Safety Management System

#### 2.1 Quality Management
- Quality management system documented and implemented (manual or equivalent)
- Document control and record retention procedures in place
- Change management process established

#### 2.2 Food Safety Management
- HACCP system implemented (based on Codex Alimentarius 7 principles)
- HACCP plan documented, verified, and reviewed at least annually
- Prerequisite programmes (PRPs) established and monitored
- Food fraud vulnerability assessment (VACCP) and mitigation measures
- Food defence threat assessment (TACCP) and security measures

#### 2.3 Specification Management
- Raw material specifications (ingredients, packaging, semi-processed and finished goods)
- Specifications agreed with customers where required
- Specifications updated and controlled

### Chapter 3: Resource Management

#### 3.1 Human Resources
- Competency requirements defined for all food safety/quality roles
- Recruitment and induction processes ensure competence
- Training programme covering food safety, hygiene, and allergens for all staff

#### 3.2 Personal Hygiene
- Personal hygiene policy documented and enforced (jewellery, clothing, illness reporting)
- Protective clothing appropriate to area (high-risk, high-care, standard)
- Medical screening and fitness-to-work procedures

#### 3.3 Facilities for Personnel
- Adequate changing rooms, canteen, and welfare facilities
- Hand washing facilities at all entry points to production areas

### Chapter 4: Planning and Production Process

#### 4.1 Prerequisite Programmes (PRPs)
PRPs must cover:
- Site layout and product flow (segregation of raw/RTE areas)
- Building fabric (floors, walls, ceilings, drains)
- Utilities (water, air, steam, energy)
- Cleaning and disinfection
- Pest management
- Waste disposal
- Equipment suitability and maintenance
- Personal hygiene and facilities
- Storage and transport conditions
- Cross-contamination prevention

#### 4.2 Allergen Management
**IFS Food treats allergen management as a critical area.**
- Allergen risk assessment covering all ingredients and cross-contact routes
- Allergen matrix for all products
- Segregation of allergenic raw materials
- Validated allergen cleaning procedures
- Allergen labelling verified (pre-packaged, PPDS, non-prepacked as applicable)
- Staff training on allergen awareness (documented)

#### 4.3 Product Development / Product Modification / Modification of Production Processes
- Food safety and quality incorporated in product/process design (HACCP extended to new products)
- Shelf-life validation documented
- Sensory and microbiological testing of new products

#### 4.4 Purchasing
- Supplier approval programme (risk-based: audit, certification, questionnaire)
- Approved supplier list maintained and reviewed
- Performance monitoring of critical suppliers

#### 4.5 Packaging
- Packaging materials suitable for food contact (compliance with applicable regulations)
- Packaging specifications maintained

#### 4.6 Product Location and Traceability
- Full traceability from raw material through to finished product and customer
- Mock recall/trace test conducted at least annually; results documented
- Batch coding system in place

#### 4.7 GMO / Organics
- Controls for products with GMO or organic claims

### Chapter 5: Measurements, Analyses, Improvements

#### 5.1 Internal Audits
- Internal audit programme covering all areas affecting food safety and quality
- High-risk and production areas: minimum annually (higher frequency recommended)
- Auditors competent and independent from audited area

#### 5.2 Inspections
- Regular site/process inspections (hygiene, GMP, pest, cold chain)
- Results documented and actions tracked

#### 5.3 Process and Environment Monitoring
- CCP monitoring records maintained
- Environmental monitoring programme (microbiological swabbing, particularly for Listeria in relevant environments)
- ATP testing or equivalent for cleaning validation

#### 5.4 Calibration and Checking of Measuring and Monitoring Devices
- All measuring equipment calibrated and verified at defined frequencies
- Calibration records maintained; out-of-calibration investigations documented

#### 5.5 Customer Complaints
- Customer complaint management system
- Trend analysis conducted; root cause identified; corrective actions implemented

#### 5.6 Incident Management, Product Withdrawal and Product Recall
- Incident management procedure (food safety incidents, contamination events)
- Product recall and withdrawal procedure tested (at least annually)
- Communication to competent authorities and customers when required

#### 5.7 Management of Non-Conforming Products
- Non-conforming product identified, segregated, and managed (rework, disposal, or concession)
- Corrective and preventive action (CAPA) process

#### 5.8 Corrective Actions
- Root cause analysis for non-conformances and customer complaints
- Corrective actions documented, implemented, and verified for effectiveness

### Chapter 6: Food Defence and External Inspections

#### 6.1 Food Defence
- Threat assessment (TACCP) for malicious contamination
- Site security measures (access control, CCTV, visitor management, tamper-evident packaging)
- Critical access points identified and controlled

#### 6.2 External Inspections and Audits
- Records of all external audits, regulatory inspections, and customer visits
- Non-conformances from external audits tracked and addressed

## IFS Food Scoring System

Each clause in IFS Food is scored on a **four-point scale:**

| Score | Meaning | Points Awarded |
|---|---|---|
| **A** | Full compliance | 20 points |
| **B** | Almost full compliance (minor deviation) | 15 points |
| **C** | Small part of the requirement implemented | 5 points |
| **D** | Requirement not implemented | 0 points |

**KO (Knock-Out) clauses:** Scored on a Yes/No basis:
- If a KO clause is scored **D** (not implemented), the audit is automatically failed regardless of overall score.
- If a KO clause is scored **C**, 50% is deducted from the total score.

---

## IFS Food Certification Levels

### Higher Level
- Overall score: **≥75%** of total achievable points
- Zero KO clauses scored D
- Zero "D" scores in any non-KO clause (all non-KO requirements at least partially met)
- **GFSI-recognized**
- Certificate valid: **12 months**
- Unannounced audit required in Year 2+

### Foundation Level
- Overall score: **≥50% and <75%**
- Zero KO clauses scored D
- **GFSI-recognized** (limited; some retailers may not accept Foundation Level)
- Certificate valid: **12 months**
- Considered an entry-level certification; sites are expected to progress to Higher Level

### Audit Failure
- Score **<50%**, OR
- Any KO clause scored **D** (not implemented)

## IFS Food Knock-Out (KO) Requirements

The 10 KO clauses are considered fundamental; failure = automatic audit failure:

| KO No. | Clause | Requirement |
|---|---|---|
| KO 1 | 1.2.4 | Senior management responsibility — food safety and quality management responsibility defined and implemented |
| KO 2 | 2.2.3 | HACCP system — documented and implemented in compliance with Codex Alimentarius |
| KO 3 | 3.2.1.2 | Staff hygiene rules — enforced for all personnel handling food |
| KO 4 | 4.2.1 | Allergen management — risk assessment for allergens conducted and controls implemented |
| KO 5 | 4.6.1 | Traceability — full traceability from raw material to finished product |
| KO 6 | 4.9.2 | Foreign body monitoring — detection equipment (metal detector, X-ray, sieve) in place and validated |
| KO 7 | 5.3.2 | CCP monitoring — critical limits defined and records maintained for all CCPs |
| KO 8 | 5.6.1 | Product recall — product recall and withdrawal procedure in place and tested |
| KO 9 | 5.7.1 | Non-conforming products — non-conforming products identified, segregated, and disposed of appropriately |
| KO 10 | 6.1.1 | Food defence — threat assessment conducted and security measures implemented |

## IFS Food Audit Process

### 1. Application
- Select IFS-approved certification body (listed on IFS Management GmbH website)
- Submit application; agree scope and audit date

### 2. Document Review (optional pre-audit)
- Certification body may review HACCP plan and key documentation before site audit

### 3. Certification Audit (Initial)
- Full on-site audit
- Auditor reviews documentation, inspects premises, interviews staff, assesses implementation
- All chapters covered; each clause scored A/B/C/D; KO clauses evaluated separately
- Audit duration depends on site size and complexity (typically 1–3 days)

### 4. Audit Report and Corrective Actions
- Detailed audit report issued
- Non-conformances raised; corrective actions submitted by site within **2 weeks** (KO/major) or **3 months** (minor)
- Corrective action plan reviewed by certification body

### 5. Certification Decision
- Final score calculated; certification level determined (Higher / Foundation / Fail)
- IFS certificate issued and registered in **IFS Database** (publicly accessible)

**Certificate validity:** 12 months

### 6. Surveillance and Unannounced Audits
- Annual recertification audit required
- **Unannounced audit** required in Year 2+ (mandatory for Higher Level)
- IFS also operates an **IFS Integrity Program**: unannounced spot checks by IFS on certified sites

### 7. Recertification
- Annual full recertification audit (IFS does not use a 3-year cycle like ISO 22000)

## IFS Food v8 Key Changes vs. v7

1. **Food safety culture** — new standalone requirement; culture programme must be documented and measured
2. **VACCP (food fraud)** — stronger requirement integrated into Chapter 2; must be part of the FSMS
3. **TACCP (food defence)** — enhanced requirements; threat assessment now more structured
4. **Environmental monitoring** — more detailed requirements for pathogen monitoring (especially Listeria)
5. **Allergen management** — cleaning validation explicitly required; labelling controls strengthened
6. **Sustainability** — guidance elements on environmental sustainability integrated
7. **Digital/remote auditing** — provisions for partial remote audit (document review) under defined conditions
8. **Integrity program** — IFS unannounced spot-check program formalized and strengthened

## IFS Food vs. Other GFSI Standards

| Feature | IFS Food v8 | BRCGS Food Safety Issue 9 | SQF Edition 9 | FSSC 22000 v6 |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Origin** | Germany/France (retailers) | UK (retailers) | USA (FMI) | Netherlands (ISO-based) |
| **Basis** | Proprietary standard | Proprietary standard | Proprietary standard | ISO 22000 + PRPs + Additional Req. |
| **GFSI recognition** | Yes | Yes | Yes (Level 2 & 3) | Yes |
| **Market focus** | Europe | Global (UK-strong) | North America | Global |
| **Grading system** | Higher / Foundation (% score) | AA / A / B / C / D / E / F | Excellent / Good / Complies | Pass / Fail |
| **Quality included** | Yes (integrated) | Optional modules | Optional (Level 3) | Yes (v6 Additional Req.) |
| **KO requirements** | Yes (10 KOs) | Yes (Fundamental requirements) | No formal KOs | No formal KOs |
| **Unannounced audits** | Required (Year 2+) | Required (Year 2+) | Optional or required | Required (annual) |
| **Certification cycle** | Annual | Annual | Annual | 3-year with annual surveillance |

## Who Should Use IFS Food?

IFS Food is ideal for:
- **Food manufacturers supplying European retailers** (especially German, French, Italian, Spanish markets)
- **Suppliers to Aldi, Lidl, Carrefour, Rewe, Edeka** and other European retail chains
- **Businesses seeking combined food safety and quality certification** from a European retailer perspective
- **Organizations already certified to BRCGS** who need IFS acceptance for additional European customers
- **Manufacturers of private-label products** for European supermarkets
