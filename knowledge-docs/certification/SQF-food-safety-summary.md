# SQF (Safe Quality Food) Program — Summary

## Overview

The **Safe Quality Food (SQF) Program** is a GFSI-recognized food safety and quality certification scheme owned and managed by the **Food Marketing Institute (FMI)** and operated by the **Safe Quality Food Institute (SQFI)**. SQF is widely used in North America and increasingly globally, particularly for exports to the USA and Canada.

**Current editions:**
- **SQF Food Safety Code (Edition 9.0)**
- **SQF Quality Code (Edition 9.0)**

**Scope:** Primary production through to retail distribution across all food sectors.

**Recognition:** GFSI-benchmarked; accepted by major global retailers.

---

## Key Features

- **Dual certification:** Offers separate **Food Safety** and **Quality** certifications (can be combined)
- **Modular structure:** Tailored to specific sectors (primary production, manufacturing, storage & distribution, etc.)
- **Risk-based approach:** Requirements scaled to product risk and business complexity
- **HACCP-based:** Built on HACCP principles and prerequisite programmes
- **GFSI recognition:** Accepted by GFSI member retailers worldwide

---

## SQF Certification Levels

SQF offers **three levels** of certification:

### Level 1: Fundamentals of Food Safety

- Entry-level certification
- Focus on: Good Agricultural Practices (GAP) or Good Manufacturing Practices (GMP)
- Prerequisite programmes established
- No formal HACCP plan required
- **Not GFSI-recognized**

### Level 2: Food Safety Fundamentals — HACCP-Based Food Safety Plan

- Comprehensive food safety certification
- Prerequisite programmes + HACCP plan required
- **GFSI-recognized**
- Most common certification level
- Meets major retailer requirements

### Level 3: Comprehensive Food Safety and Quality Management System

- Combines **Level 2 Food Safety** + **SQF Quality Code**
- Includes quality management requirements (product specifications, customer satisfaction, quality audits)
- **GFSI-recognized** for food safety component
- Preferred by customers seeking both safety and quality assurance

## SQF Food Safety Code Structure (Edition 9.0)

The SQF Food Safety Code is organized into **modules** tailored to different sectors:

### Module 1: Food Safety Fundamentals

Applies to all SQF certifications. Covers:

#### 1.1 Management Commitment
- Senior management responsibility for food safety
- Food safety policy documented
- Resources allocated

#### 1.2 Document Control and Records
- Documented procedures maintained
- Records retained as required
- Change control

#### 1.3 Specifications and Product Development
- Product specifications for all products
- New product food safety review

#### 1.4 Supplier and Raw Material Approval
- Supplier approval programme (risk-based)
- Raw material specifications
- Supplier performance monitoring

#### 1.5 Product Identification, Traceability, Withdrawal, and Recall
- Full traceability one step back and one step forward
- Batch coding system
- Recall procedure and mock recall annually

#### 1.6 Food Defence and Food Fraud
- Food defence plan (malicious contamination prevention)
- Food fraud vulnerability assessment
- Mitigation strategies implemented

#### 1.7 Corrective and Preventive Actions
- System for identifying and correcting non-conformances
- Root cause analysis and preventive actions

#### 1.8 Internal Audits, Inspections, and Verification
- Internal audits conducted (frequency based on risk)
- Pre-shipment inspections
- Verification of controls

#### 1.9 Training
- All staff trained in food safety
- HACCP team leader trained (Level 2 certified sites)
- Training records maintained

### Module 2: Good Manufacturing Practices (GMPs)

Prerequisite programmes covering:

#### 2.1 Site Location and Grounds
- Site located to minimize contamination risks
- Grounds maintained (waste areas, external environment)

#### 2.2 Construction and Layout of Premises
- Building design minimizes contamination risk
- Zoning and workflow (raw to ready-to-eat separation)
- Floors, walls, ceilings cleanable and maintained

#### 2.3 Utilities (Water, Ice, Air, Lighting)
- Potable water supply
- Ice produced from potable water
- Compressed air food-grade (if in contact with food)
- Lighting adequate and shatterproof where required

#### 2.4 Equipment
- Equipment designed for food use
- Maintained, cleaned, and calibrated
- Temperature and monitoring equipment calibrated

#### 2.5 Maintenance
- Planned maintenance programme
- Temporary repairs risk-assessed

#### 2.6 Personnel Practices and Facilities
- Handwashing facilities adequate
- Toilets and changing facilities provided
- Personal hygiene rules enforced (clothing, jewellery, illness exclusion)

#### 2.7 Sanitation and Hygiene
- Cleaning and sanitation schedule
- Chemicals approved for food use
- Cleaning effectiveness verified

#### 2.8 Waste and Refuse Disposal
- Waste stored in covered containers
- Waste areas cleaned regularly
- Waste removal frequent

#### 2.9 Pest Control
- Pest control programme in place (contracted or competent in-house)
- Bait stations and traps monitored
- Proofing maintained

#### 2.10 Receiving, Storage, and Transport
- Goods inward inspection
- Temperature-controlled storage monitored
- Stock rotation (FIFO)
- Transport vehicles clean and temperature-controlled

#### 2.11 Allergen Management
- Allergen risk assessment
- Allergen controls (segregation, cleaning, labelling)
- Staff trained on allergens

### Module 3: HACCP System (Level 2 and 3)

Level 2 and 3 sites must implement a full **HACCP plan** based on Codex principles:

1. **Assemble HACCP team** (multidisciplinary; leader trained)
2. **Describe product and intended use**
3. **Construct flow diagram** and verify on-site
4. **Conduct hazard analysis** (biological, chemical, physical)
5. **Determine Critical Control Points (CCPs)**
6. **Establish critical limits** for each CCP
7. **Establish monitoring system** for CCPs
8. **Establish corrective actions** for deviations
9. **Establish verification procedures**
10. **Establish documentation and record-keeping**

#### HACCP Plan Requirements

- HACCP plan documented and approved
- Reviewed annually or when changes occur
- CCP monitoring records maintained
- Deviations investigated and corrected
- Verification activities conducted (internal audits, testing, review of records)

## SQF Quality Code (Optional — Level 3)

Sites pursuing **Level 3 certification** must also comply with the **SQF Quality Code**, which includes:

- **Quality management system** (policies, objectives, responsibilities)
- **Product specifications** and quality standards
- **Supplier quality monitoring**
- **Product inspection and testing** (quality checks, sensory evaluation)
- **Customer complaints** and satisfaction monitoring
- **Quality audits** and continual improvement

---

## SQF Audit Process

### 1. Pre-Assessment (Optional)

- Conduct gap analysis against SQF Code
- Identify areas requiring improvement before certification audit

### 2. Application

- Select SQF-licensed certification body
- Submit application specifying scope and level of certification

### 3. Certification Audit

**Two-part audit:**

#### Part 1: System Review (Desktop Audit)
- Review of documented food safety plan, HACCP, procedures, and records
- May be conducted remotely

#### Part 2: Site Audit
- On-site inspection of facilities, processes, and implementation
- Interview staff
- Review records
- Identify non-conformances

**Audit options:**
- **Announced audit:** Date agreed in advance (standard option)
- **Unannounced audit:** No advance notice (required by some retailers; available after initial announced audit)

### 4. Nonconformance Management

**Nonconformance categories:**

| Category | Description | Timeframe for Correction |
|---|---|---|
| **Critical** | Immediate food safety risk or systemic failure | Immediate action required |
| **Major** | Significant breach of SQF requirements | 30 days |
| **Minor** | Isolated or low-impact issue | 90 days |

### 5. Certification Decision

- Certification body reviews audit findings and corrective actions
- If satisfactory: **SQF certificate issued**

**Certificate validity:** 12 months

### 6. Surveillance and Recertification

- **Annual recertification audit** required
- Mid-term surveillance audit may be required (depends on risk and nonconformance history)
- **Unannounced audits:** Some sites required to undergo unannounced audits (retailer-driven or voluntary upgrade)

---

## SQF Grading System

SQF sites receive a **rating** based on audit performance:

| Rating | Criteria |
|---|---|
| **Excellent (E)** | Full compliance; no critical, major, or minor non-conformances |
| **Good (G)** | No critical non-conformances; ≤3 major non-conformances |
| **Complies (C)** | No critical non-conformances; 4–20 major non-conformances |
| **Non-Compliant (NC)** | Critical non-conformance(s) identified; certificate not issued or suspended |

---

## SQF Practitioner Requirement

- Each SQF-certified site must have a designated **SQF Practitioner**
- SQF Practitioner responsible for overseeing food safety/quality programme implementation
- Must complete SQFI-recognized **SQF Practitioner Training Course**
- Certificate of completion required before certification audit

**Training providers:** SQFI-approved training organisations (online or in-person courses available)

## SQF vs. Other GFSI Schemes

| Feature | SQF | BRC | FSSC 22000 | IFS |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Origin** | USA (FMI) | UK (retailers) | Netherlands (ISO-based) | Germany/France (retailers) |
| **Market focus** | North America | Global (UK-strong) | Global | Europe |
| **Levels** | 3 levels (incl. non-GFSI entry level) | Single standard | Single standard | Single standard |
| **Quality option** | Yes (Level 3) | Yes (optional modules) | No (food safety only) | Yes (IFS Food includes quality) |
| **Unannounced audits** | Optional or required | Required (Year 2+) | Required (Year 2+) | Required (Year 2+) |
| **GFSI recognition** | Yes (Level 2 & 3) | Yes | Yes | Yes |

## Benefits of SQF Certification

- **North American market access:** Widely accepted by USA and Canadian retailers
- **GFSI recognition:** Meets global retailer requirements
- **Flexibility:** Modular structure tailored to sector
- **Quality differentiation:** Level 3 combines safety and quality (competitive advantage)
- **Retailer confidence:** Demonstrates robust food safety systems
- **Continual improvement:** Structured audit and improvement cycle

---

## Who Should Use SQF?

SQF is ideal for:
- **Exporters to North America** (USA, Canada, Mexico)
- **Primary producers** (farms, growers, packers) — SQF includes strong primary production modules
- **Food manufacturers and processors** seeking GFSI certification
- **Storage and distribution operators**
- **Businesses seeking combined food safety and quality certification** (Level 3)

---

## Getting Started with SQF

1. **Attend SQF Practitioner Training** (designate SQF Practitioner; complete training)
2. **Conduct gap analysis** against SQF Code
3. **Implement prerequisite programmes** (GMPs)
4. **Develop HACCP plan** (if seeking Level 2 or 3)
5. **Establish documentation and records**
6. **Conduct internal audits** and pre-assessment
7. **Select SQF-licensed certification body**
8. **Schedule and complete certification audit**
9. **Address non-conformances** and achieve certification

**Further information:** [https://www.sqfi.com](https://www.sqfi.com)

---

## Ongoing Compliance

- Maintain food safety and quality systems daily
- Monitor CCPs and PRPs
- Conduct internal audits (at least annually)
- Review and update HACCP plan annually
- Prepare for annual recertification audit 2–3 months in advance
- Respond promptly to audit non-conformances
