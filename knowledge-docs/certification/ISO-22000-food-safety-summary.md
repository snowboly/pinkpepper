# ISO 22000 Food Safety Management System — Summary

## Overview

ISO 22000 is an **international standard** for food safety management systems, published by the International Organization for Standardization (ISO). It specifies requirements for any organization in the food chain to demonstrate its ability to control food safety hazards and ensure food is safe at the point of consumption.

**Current version:** ISO 22000:2018

**Scope:** Applicable to all organizations in the food chain, regardless of size or complexity:
- Primary producers (farms)
- Food manufacturers
- Transport and storage operators
- Retail and foodservice
- Suppliers of equipment, packaging, cleaning agents, additives

**Recognition:** Globally recognized; accepted by retailers and certification bodies worldwide.

---

## Key Principles

ISO 22000 integrates:
1. **Interactive communication** throughout the food chain
2. **System management** (based on ISO management system structure)
3. **Prerequisite programmes (PRPs)**
4. **HACCP principles** (Codex Alimentarius)

---

## Structure of ISO 22000:2018

The standard follows the **High-Level Structure (HLS)** common to all ISO management system standards:

### Clause 1–3: Scope, Normative References, Terms and Definitions

Background and definitions; no requirements.

### Clause 4: Context of the Organization

- Understand the organization and its context (internal and external issues affecting food safety)
- Identify interested parties (customers, regulators, consumers) and their requirements
- Define the scope of the food safety management system (FSMS)
- Establish, implement, maintain, and continually improve the FSMS

### Clause 5: Leadership

- **Top management commitment:** Demonstrate leadership and commitment to the FSMS
- **Food safety policy:** Establish and communicate a food safety policy
- **Organizational roles and responsibilities:** Assign responsibilities and authorities
- **Food safety team leader:** Appoint a competent person to lead the food safety team

### Clause 6: Planning

- **Risk and opportunity assessment:** Identify risks and opportunities related to food safety and business objectives
- **Food safety objectives:** Set measurable food safety objectives
- **Planning changes:** Manage changes to the FSMS in a controlled manner

### Clause 7: Support

- **Resources:** Provide adequate resources (people, infrastructure, work environment)
- **Competence:** Ensure staff are competent (training, qualifications)
- **Awareness:** Ensure staff understand their role in food safety
- **Communication:** Establish internal and external communication channels
- **Documented information:** Maintain documented procedures and records as required by the standard

### Clause 8: Operation

#### 8.1 Operational Planning and Control

Plan, implement, and control processes needed to meet food safety requirements.

#### 8.2 Prerequisite Programmes (PRPs)

Establish, implement, and maintain **PRPs** appropriate to the organization:
- Infrastructure and maintenance
- Layout of premises and workspace (zoning, workflow)
- Utilities (water, air, energy)
- Waste disposal
- Equipment suitability, cleaning, and maintenance
- Management of purchased materials (ingredients, packaging)
- Measures to prevent cross-contamination
- Cleaning and sanitizing
- Pest control
- Personnel hygiene and facilities
- Rework
- Procedures for product recall
- Warehousing (storage conditions)
- Product information and consumer awareness

PRPs must be:
- Documented
- Verified for effectiveness
- Updated when changes occur

#### 8.3 Traceability System

- Establish a traceability system to identify product batches and their relation to raw materials and distribution
- Retain records to enable traceability

#### 8.4 Emergency Preparedness and Response

- Identify potential emergency situations (fire, flood, contamination, power failure, terrorism)
- Plan response procedures
- Test emergency response (drills, simulations)

#### 8.5 Hazard Control

##### 8.5.1 Preliminary Steps to Enable Hazard Analysis

- Gather information on raw materials, ingredients, packaging, product characteristics, intended use, flow diagrams, process descriptions
- Describe process steps and control measures in place

##### 8.5.2 Hazard Analysis

- Conduct systematic hazard analysis:
  - Identify and list all reasonably foreseeable food safety hazards (biological, chemical, physical)
  - Assess severity and likelihood of each hazard
  - Determine control measures required

##### 8.5.3 Validation of Control Measures

- Validate that selected control measures are capable of achieving the intended level of control

##### 8.5.4 HACCP Plan

- **Critical Control Points (CCPs):** Identify CCPs using a decision tree or logic diagram
- **Critical limits:** Establish measurable critical limits for each CCP (temperature, time, pH, etc.)
- **Monitoring system:** Define how and how often CCPs are monitored
- **Corrective actions:** Specify actions to take when a CCP deviates from critical limits
- **Verification:** Define procedures to verify the HACCP plan is working effectively

##### 8.5.5 Operational PRPs (OPRPs)

- Identify operational prerequisite programmes (control measures that are not CCPs but are essential for controlling hazards)
- Examples: specific cleaning procedures, supplier approval, allergen controls

#### 8.6 Updating Information Specifying PRPs and HACCP Plan

- Review and update PRPs and HACCP plan when changes occur (new products, processes, equipment, suppliers, legislation)

#### 8.7 Control of Monitoring and Measuring

- Ensure monitoring and measuring equipment is calibrated and verified
- Maintain calibration records

#### 8.8 Verification Related to PRPs and HACCP Plan

- Verify that PRPs, OPRPs, and the HACCP plan are implemented and effective
- Verification activities include:
  - Internal audits
  - Analysis of verification results
  - Review of monitoring records
  - Product testing

#### 8.9 Control of Product and Process Nonconformities

##### 8.9.1–8.9.2 Nonconformity Handling

- Identify and control nonconforming product (does not meet food safety requirements)
- Evaluate nonconforming product; decide on disposition (rework, release under concession, reject, destroy)

##### 8.9.3 Corrective Actions

- Take corrective actions to eliminate the cause of nonconformities
- Review effectiveness of corrective actions

##### 8.9.4 Handling of Potentially Unsafe Products

- If potentially unsafe product identified after release: notify relevant parties (customers, authorities, consumers)
- Initiate product withdrawal or recall if necessary
- Monitor and record withdrawal/recall effectiveness

### Clause 9: Performance Evaluation

#### 9.1 Monitoring, Measurement, Analysis, and Evaluation

- Monitor and measure FSMS performance
- Analyze and evaluate results (trends, effectiveness of controls)

#### 9.2 Internal Audit

- Conduct internal audits at planned intervals
- Verify the FSMS conforms to ISO 22000 and is effectively implemented
- Maintain audit records

#### 9.3 Management Review

- Top management reviews the FSMS at planned intervals
- Review inputs: audit results, nonconformities, customer feedback, hazard analysis updates, external changes
- Review outputs: decisions on improvements, resource needs, policy updates

### Clause 10: Improvement

#### 10.1 Nonconformity and Corrective Action

- When nonconformity occurs: take action to control and correct it; evaluate the need for action to eliminate causes; implement corrective actions; review effectiveness

#### 10.2 Continual Improvement

- Continually improve the FSMS through:
  - Communication (internal and external)
  - Management review
  - Internal audit
  - Analysis of results
  - Validation and verification activities
  - Corrective actions
  - System updates

#### 10.3 Updating the FSMS

- Evaluate and update the FSMS at planned intervals
- Update based on management review outputs, analysis of verification data, changes in context

## Certification Process

### 1. Preparation

- Establish FSMS compliant with ISO 22000
- Conduct gap analysis against the standard
- Implement required processes and documentation
- Conduct internal audits

### 2. Application

- Select accredited certification body (look for ISO 17021 accreditation)
- Submit application and scope of certification

### 3. Stage 1 Audit (Documentation Review)

- Auditor reviews FSMS documentation (food safety policy, procedures, HACCP plan, records)
- Identifies gaps or areas requiring improvement before Stage 2

### 4. Stage 2 Audit (Implementation Audit)

- On-site audit to verify FSMS is implemented and effective
- Auditor inspects premises, interviews staff, reviews records
- Non-conformities identified and categorized (Major / Minor)

### 5. Corrective Actions

- Organization addresses non-conformities
- Submits evidence of corrective actions within agreed timescale (typically 90 days)

### 6. Certification Decision

- Certification body reviews audit report and corrective action evidence
- If satisfactory: **ISO 22000 certificate issued**

**Certificate validity:** 3 years

### 7. Surveillance Audits

- Annual surveillance audits conducted to verify ongoing compliance
- Smaller in scope than initial certification audit

### 8. Recertification

- Full recertification audit conducted before certificate expiry (every 3 years)

## ISO 22000 vs. Other Standards

| Feature | ISO 22000 | BRC | FSSC 22000 |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Type** | International standard | Private certification scheme | ISO 22000 + additional requirements |
| **Scope** | Entire food chain | Primarily manufacturers | Manufacturers (GFSI-recognized) |
| **Flexibility** | High (adaptable to any organization) | Prescriptive | Prescriptive |
| **GFSI recognition** | No (unless certified to FSSC 22000) | Yes | Yes |
| **Audit frequency** | Annual surveillance + 3-year recert | Annual + mid-term | Annual + mid-term |

## Benefits of ISO 22000 Certification

- **Global recognition:** Accepted by customers and retailers worldwide
- **Structured framework:** Clear requirements for building robust FSMS
- **Integration:** Compatible with other ISO standards (ISO 9001, ISO 14001)
- **Supply chain assurance:** Demonstrates commitment to food safety to customers
- **Legal compliance:** Helps meet regulatory requirements
- **Continual improvement:** Drives ongoing enhancement of food safety systems
- **Risk management:** Systematic identification and control of food safety hazards

---

## Who Should Use ISO 22000?

ISO 22000 is suitable for:
- Organizations seeking internationally recognized food safety certification
- Food manufacturers, processors, and packagers
- Transport, storage, and distribution companies in the food chain
- Suppliers of ingredients, additives, packaging materials, cleaning agents
- Organizations wanting to integrate food safety management with other ISO standards

**Not GFSI-recognized:** If GFSI recognition is required by major retailers, consider **FSSC 22000** (Food Safety System Certification), which is based on ISO 22000 plus additional requirements and is GFSI-benchmarked.
