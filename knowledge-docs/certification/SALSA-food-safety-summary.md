# SALSA Food Safety Standard — Summary

## Overview

SALSA (Safe And Local Supplier Approval) is a food safety certification scheme designed specifically for **small and growing food and drink businesses** in the UK. Developed by Soil Association Certification and supported by major UK retailers, SALSA provides an accessible, affordable route to third-party food safety certification.

**Target businesses:** SME manufacturers, artisan producers, farm shops, delis, caterers supplying retail.

**Recognition:** Accepted by major UK retailers as equivalent to BRC or IFS for lower-risk products.

---

## Key Features

- **Risk-based approach:** Suitable for lower-complexity operations
- **Affordable:** Lower cost than BRC or ISO 22000
- **Accessible:** Simplified documentation requirements
- **Retail-recognized:** Meets supplier approval requirements for major UK supermarkets
- **Annual certification:** Valid for 12 months; renewed annually

**Certification bodies:** Soil Association Certification and other SALSA-approved bodies.

---

## Structure of SALSA

The standard is organized into **12 modules**:

1. **Management Commitment and Continual Improvement**
2. **HACCP Food Safety Plan**
3. **Food Safety Management System**
4. **Premises and Equipment**
5. **Control of Operations**
6. **Product Development and Labelling**
7. **Supplier and Raw Material Approval**
8. **Training and Personnel**
9. **Product Traceability and Recall**
10. **Product Testing and Monitoring**
11. **Allergen Management**
12. **Food Defence and Food Fraud**

---

## Module 1: Management Commitment

- Food safety policy documented and communicated
- Senior management responsible for food safety
- Regular management reviews
- Resources allocated to maintain food safety
- Continual improvement culture

---

## Module 2: HACCP Food Safety Plan

- HACCP plan developed by trained HACCP team leader (Level 3 or 4 qualification)
- HACCP principles applied:
  1. Conduct hazard analysis
  2. Identify Critical Control Points (CCPs)
  3. Set critical limits
  4. Establish monitoring procedures
  5. Define corrective actions
  6. Implement verification procedures
  7. Maintain documentation and records
- HACCP plan reviewed annually or when processes change

---

## Module 3: Food Safety Management System

- Internal audits conducted annually
- Corrective and preventive actions (CAPA) system
- Documented procedures for key processes
- Management of non-conforming product
- Incident and complaint management
- Record-keeping and document control

---

## Module 4: Premises and Equipment

- Site located and designed to minimize contamination risks
- Adequate space for operations
- Walls, floors, ceilings cleanable and in good repair
- Lighting adequate and shatterproof where required
- Ventilation sufficient; extraction provided where necessary
- Water supply potable and sufficient
- Drainage adequate; no risk of backflow
- Equipment suitable for purpose; maintained and calibrated
- Temperature monitoring equipment calibrated

---

## Module 5: Control of Operations

- Production processes controlled and monitored
- Temperature control throughout storage, preparation, cooking
- Cleaning and sanitation procedures in place
- Pest control programme (contracted service or in-house with competent person)
- Waste management controlled; bins lidded and cleaned
- Glass, brittle plastic, and metal controls (breakage procedure)

---

## Module 6: Product Development and Labelling

- New product development procedure includes food safety review
- Product specifications maintained
- Shelf-life validation conducted
- Labelling accurate and compliant with UK law (allergenic ingredients, date marks, storage instructions)

---

## Module 7: Supplier and Raw Material Approval

- All suppliers approved before use
- Risk-based supplier approval:
  - **High-risk suppliers:** Third-party certification required (BRC, SALSA, ISO 22000, etc.)
  - **Lower-risk suppliers:** Supplier questionnaire and performance monitoring
- Supplier performance monitored
- Goods-inward inspection conducted
- Supplier reviews conducted annually

---

## Module 8: Training and Personnel

- All food handlers trained in food safety (Level 2 minimum)
- HACCP team leader trained to Level 3 or 4
- Induction training provided before unsupervised work
- Refresher training every 3 years
- Training records maintained
- Personal hygiene standards enforced
- Staff illness reporting and exclusion procedure
- Protective clothing provided and laundered
- Jewellery and personal items restrictions

---

## Module 9: Product Traceability and Recall

- Full traceability one step back (suppliers) and one step forward (customers)
- Batch coding system in place
- Traceability test conducted annually (achieve traceability within 4 hours)
- Product recall procedure documented
- Recall team identified
- Mock recall exercise conducted annually
- Incident notification procedure (enforcement authority contact)

---

## Module 10: Product Testing and Monitoring

- Microbiological testing programme in place (product and environmental swabs)
- Testing frequency risk-based
- Accredited laboratory used
- Results reviewed; corrective action for failures
- Shelf-life validation supported by testing

---

## Module 11: Allergen Management

- Allergen risk assessment conducted
- Allergen controls in place:
  - Segregated storage
  - Dedicated equipment or cleaning validation
  - Allergen cleaning procedures
  - Staff training on allergen risks
- Product labelling includes full allergen declaration
- "May contain" statements used only when justified by risk assessment
- Allergen incidents investigated

---

## Module 12: Food Defence and Food Fraud

- Food defence risk assessment (sabotage, tampering, malicious contamination)
- Security measures in place (controlled access, visitor management, stock security)
- Food fraud vulnerability assessment (economically motivated adulteration)
- Supplier authenticity and integrity checks

---

## SALSA Audit Process

### 1. Application

- Apply to SALSA-approved certification body
- Pay certification fee (lower than BRC/IFS)
- Submit pre-audit questionnaire

### 2. Audit

- **Announced audit** (date agreed in advance)
- Duration: typically half-day to one day depending on site complexity
- Auditor reviews documentation and conducts site inspection
- Non-conformances identified and categorized:
  - **Critical:** Immediate food safety risk
  - **Major:** Significant breach of standard
  - **Minor:** Isolated or low-risk issue

### 3. Corrective Action

- Corrective actions submitted within agreed timescale (typically 28 days)
- Evidence provided (photos, updated procedures, records)
- Certification body verifies corrective actions

### 4. Certification Decision

**Grades:**
- **Pass:** All critical and major non-conformances resolved
- **Fail:** Critical non-conformances not resolved; re-audit required

**Certificate validity:** 12 months from audit date.

### 5. Surveillance

- No mid-term surveillance audit required (unlike BRC)
- Annual re-certification audit required to maintain certification

---

## Benefits of SALSA Certification

- **Retailer acceptance:** Recognized by major UK supermarkets as proof of supplier due diligence
- **Lower cost:** More affordable than BRC or ISO 22000 (suitable for small businesses)
- **Simplified documentation:** Less bureaucratic than larger schemes
- **Tailored to SMEs:** Requirements proportionate to business size and complexity
- **Market access:** Opens doors to supplying retail and foodservice customers requiring third-party certification
- **Continuous improvement:** Structured framework for improving food safety systems

---

## Who Should Use SALSA?

SALSA is ideal for:
- Small manufacturers (artisan bakeries, preserves, sauces, ready meals)
- Farm shops and delis producing own-label products
- Start-ups and growing businesses
- Businesses supplying regional retailers or independents
- Producers not yet ready for BRC complexity/cost

**Not suitable for:**
- Large-scale manufacturers (BRC or FSSC 22000 more appropriate)
- High-risk operations (raw meat/poultry, infant formula) — retailers may require BRC
- Export-focused businesses (FSSC 22000 or IFS may be preferred)

## SALSA vs. Other Standards

| Feature | SALSA | BRC | ISO 22000 |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Target** | SMEs | Large manufacturers | Any size |
| **Complexity** | Low-medium | High | Medium-high |
| **Cost** | £ | ££££ | £££ |
| **Audit frequency** | Annual | Annual + mid-term | Annual + surveillance |
| **Retailer recognition (UK)** | Yes (for lower-risk) | Yes (all risk levels) | Yes |
| **International recognition** | Limited | Global | Global |

## Getting Started with SALSA

1. **Assess readiness:** Ensure HACCP plan in place; staff trained; prerequisite programmes established
2. **Choose certification body:** Soil Association Certification or other SALSA-approved body
3. **Pre-audit preparation:** Review SALSA standard; conduct internal audit; identify gaps
4. **Apply for audit:** Submit application and fee
5. **Conduct audit:** Host auditor; demonstrate compliance
6. **Close out non-conformances:** Submit corrective actions
7. **Achieve certification:** Receive certificate; promote to customers

**Further information:** [https://www.salsafood.co.uk](https://www.salsafood.co.uk)

---

## Review and Maintenance

- Maintain food safety systems between audits
- Conduct internal audits quarterly or biannually
- Review HACCP plan annually
- Monitor supplier performance
- Update procedures when processes change
- Prepare for annual re-certification audit 2–3 months in advance
