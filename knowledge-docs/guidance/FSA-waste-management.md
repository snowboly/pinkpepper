# Food Waste Management — FSA Guidance and Legal Requirements

## Legal Basis

- **EC 852/2004, Annex II, Chapter VI** — primary hygiene obligations for food waste
- **Environmental Protection Act 1990** — duty of care for waste in the UK
- **Waste (England and Wales) Regulations 2011** — waste hierarchy
- **Regulation (EC) No 1069/2009** — animal by-products (retained in UK law)
- **Environmental Protection (Duty of Care) Regulations 1991** — transfer of waste

---

## EC 852/2004 — Core Waste Requirements

1. Food waste, non-edible by-products, and other refuse must be **removed from food rooms as quickly as possible**
2. Must be deposited in **closable containers**
3. Refuse stores must be **designed and managed** to remain clean and free from animals and pests
4. All waste must be **eliminated hygienically and in an environmentally friendly way** in accordance with applicable legislation

---

## Waste Hierarchy (Legal Requirement)

UK and EU law requires FBOs to follow the waste hierarchy in order of preference:

1. **Prevention** — reduce waste generation (over-ordering, over-production)
2. **Reuse** — reuse food before disposal where safe (redistribution to food banks)
3. **Recycling** — separate waste streams for composting, anaerobic digestion
4. **Other recovery** — energy from waste
5. **Disposal** — landfill (least preferred; avoid)

## Waste Segregation Requirements

### Colour-Coded Bin Systems (Recommended)

| Colour | Waste Type |
|---|---|
| Black/grey | General non-recyclable waste |
| Blue | Dry recyclables (paper, card, some plastics) |
| Brown/green | Food/organic waste for composting/AD |
| Yellow | Clinical or hazardous waste (not typical in food production) |
| Red | Animal by-products (if applicable) |

### Food Business Waste Streams

| Stream | Examples | Disposal Route |
|---|---|---|
| Food waste (non-ABP) | Fruit peel, vegetable trimmings, bread, cooked food — no raw meat/fish | Composting, anaerobic digestion, food waste processor |
| Used cooking oil / fat | Fryer oil, grease | Licensed waste carrier; often collected by biodiesel companies — **never pour down drain** |
| Animal by-products (Cat 3) | Raw meat trimmings, bones, fish offal, eggshell | Licensed ABP collector; separate designated bin |
| Packaging waste | Cardboard, plastic wrapping, glass | Recycling — segregated by type |
| General mixed | Non-recyclable waste | Licensed waste carrier |
| Hazardous | Cleaning chemicals, aerosols | Licensed hazardous waste contractor |

## Animal By-Products (ABP) — EC 1069/2009

### Categories

| Category | Examples | Disposal Route |
|---|---|---|
| **Category 1** (highest risk) | Specified risk material (SRM) in BSE-affected species, illegal food imports, pets | Incineration only |
| **Category 2** | Manure, digestive tract contents, catering waste from international transport | Incineration or composting/biogas with specific conditions |
| **Category 3** (lowest risk) | Raw meat/fish trimmings from slaughter of healthy animals, out-of-date food products, eggshell, used cooking oil | Composting, biogas, rendering, pet food |

### Key FBO Obligations for ABP
- Use a **registered/approved ABP collector** only
- Provide a **commercial document** (consignment note) accompanying each collection (retained 2 years)
- Store ABP in **clearly labelled, sealed, leak-proof containers** — separate from food waste
- Register premises with the Animal and Plant Health Agency (APHA) if generating ABP
- Maintain a **register of collections** (date, quantity, destination)

### Catering Waste Rules
- "Catering waste" is Category 2 ABP when it includes meat (even if cooked)
- Must be collected by a licensed ABP carrier
- Cannot be composted at home or on farm without specific approval
- Exception: waste from countries not affected by certain exotic diseases

## Storage of Food Waste

### Bin Requirements
- Closable lids (pedal-operated preferred to avoid hand contamination)
- Leak-proof construction; easy to clean
- Sufficient size — not overflowing between collections
- Located away from food preparation/storage areas where possible
- Identified by waste type

### External Storage Areas
- Hard, cleanable surface (concrete preferred)
- Pest-proof — no gaps where rodents can enter
- Adequate drainage
- Screened from public view where possible
- Not adjacent to food intake areas
- Covered from rain
- Sufficient ventilation to prevent odour build-up
- Cleaned and disinfected regularly (frequency documented in cleaning schedule)

### Collection Frequency
- Food waste should be collected at least **weekly** (more often in warm weather or high-volume operations)
- Used cooking oil: as required, but stored securely in sealed drums
- ABP: typically collected weekly or on agreed schedule with licensed carrier

## Used Cooking Oil (UCO) Management

1. Allow oil to cool before handling
2. Transfer to designated sealed containers (not down drains — **illegal**)
3. Store on impermeable surface with bunding to contain spills
4. Arrange collection with licensed waste carrier who provides receipts
5. Maintain records of volumes and collection dates
6. Consider oil filtration to extend oil life before disposal

## Duty of Care

UK FBOs have a legal duty of care for all business waste under the EPA 1990:
- Only transfer waste to **authorised persons** (licensed waste carriers registered with the Environment Agency / SEPA / NRW / NIEA)
- Obtain and retain **waste transfer notes** (minimum 2 years)
- Describe waste accurately on transfer notes
- Take reasonable steps to ensure waste is disposed of properly

**Failure to comply** can result in unlimited fines.

| Record | Retention Period |
|---|---|
| Waste transfer notes | Minimum 2 years |
| ABP commercial documents / consignment notes | Minimum 2 years |
| UCO collection receipts | Minimum 2 years |
| Waste carrier registration checks | Retain copy of licence |
| Cleaning records for refuse stores | Part of cleaning schedule records |

## Pest Prevention Through Good Waste Management

- Never leave refuse uncovered overnight
- Clean waste areas after every collection
- Check for pest activity around external bins weekly
- Report signs of pests immediately (gnaw marks, droppings, smear marks)
- Ensure external bins are positioned away from walls and vegetation where possible
- Eliminate food spillages in waste areas immediately
