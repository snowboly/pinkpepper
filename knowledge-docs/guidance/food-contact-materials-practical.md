# Food Contact Materials — Practical Compliance Guide for Food Businesses

## Overview
This guide helps food businesses comply with EC/UK Regulation 1935/2004 and Regulation EU/UK 10/2011 (plastics). It covers how to assess, purchase, use, and document food contact materials (FCMs) in day-to-day operations.

---

## What Counts as a Food Contact Material in Your Kitchen or Production Area?

| Item | FCM? |
|---|---|
| Cutting boards | Yes |
| Knives and utensils | Yes |
| Mixing bowls and containers | Yes |
| Cooking pots and pans | Yes |
| Food trays and baking tins | Yes |
| Packaging materials (wrapping film, boxes, bags) | Yes |
| Disposable gloves used during food handling | Yes |
| Conveyor belts in food production | Yes |
| Refrigerator shelving and liners | Yes |
| Oven liners and baking paper | Yes |
| Rubber seals on mixers, mincers, filling machines | Yes |
| Cleaning cloths used on food-contact surfaces | Borderline — treated as FCM for hygienic purposes |
| Non-stick coatings (PTFE) on pans | Yes |
| Food packaging received from suppliers | Yes (supplier's responsibility, but you should verify) |

---

## The Basic Rule: Will It Transfer?

Before using any material in contact with food, ask:
- Is it designed for food contact? (Look for the glass-and-fork symbol or "for food contact" marking)
- Is it suitable for the temperature it will be used at?
- Is it suitable for the type of food it will contact (acidic, fatty, aqueous)?
- Is it in good condition (no cracks, discolouration, strong odour)?

## Purchasing FCMs — What to Check

### On the Product or Label
- **Glass-and-fork symbol** or "for food contact" text
- Temperature range (e.g. "suitable for microwave", "freezer safe", "max 100°C")
- Intended food types or food simulants (e.g. "not suitable for acidic foods")
- Single-use or reusable designation

### From Your Supplier (Business-to-Business)
- Request a **Declaration of Compliance (DoC)** — this is mandatory for plastics under Regulation 10/2011 and best practice for all FCMs
- DoC should state: the regulation it complies with, the substances/materials covered, any migration limits met, test conditions
- Retain all DoCs on file — update when the supplier changes formulation

## Colour-Coded Equipment

A colour-coded system for cutting boards, knives, and utensils prevents cross-contamination between food types and allergens.

### Standard Colour Coding (UK/EU Best Practice)

| Colour | Food Type |
|---|---|
| **Red** | Raw meat |
| **Blue** | Raw fish and seafood |
| **Yellow** | Cooked meat and poultry |
| **Green** | Salad, fruit, and vegetables |
| **White** | Dairy, bakery, and general |
| **Brown** | Root vegetables and soiled produce |
| **Purple** (emerging) | Allergen-free / free-from preparations |

- Display colour chart at each prep station
- Train all staff on colour-code system on induction
- Replace boards when scoring becomes deep (bacteria and allergens harbour in grooves)
- Inspect boards daily; discard damaged ones immediately

## Cutting Boards in Detail

### Acceptable Materials
| Material | Suitability | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **High-density polyethylene (HDPE)** | Preferred | Dishwasher safe, non-porous, colour-coded available |
| **Polypropylene (PP)** | Acceptable | Similar to HDPE; check temperature rating |
| **Tempered glass** | Acceptable for some uses | Hard on knives; not ideal for heavy chopping |
| **Stainless steel** | Specific applications | Not for general chopping — damages knife edges |
| **Wood (hardwood, e.g. maple)** | Limited use only | Not permitted for raw meat/fish handling in most high-care environments; traditional use in bakery (wooden pastry boards) may be acceptable; must be smooth, free of cracks, regularly treated with food-grade oil |
| **Bamboo** | Limited — treat as wood | Harder than wood; drier but similar limitations |

### Maintenance
- Wash in dishwasher at ≥60°C after each use, or hand-wash with hot water and detergent then sanitise
- Inspect for deep scoring — replace when grooves form (cannot be adequately cleaned)
- Do not use the same board for ready-to-eat and raw food even after washing, unless fully validated cleaning procedure has been completed

## Stainless Steel Equipment

### Recommended Grades

| Grade | Composition | Best For |
|---|---|---|
| **304 (18/8)** | 18% chromium, 8% nickel | General food contact; most equipment, worktops, sinks, pots |
| **316 (18/10/2)** | 18% chromium, 10% nickel, 2% molybdenum | Acidic foods, brine/salt environments, vinegar, citrus, fermentation |

### Surface Finish
- **2B (mill finish)** — standard, smooth; acceptable for most food contact
- **No. 4 (brushed/satin)** — most common for food equipment; easy to clean
- **Electropolished** — smoothest; best for high-care/pharmaceutical; reduces bacterial adhesion
- **Avoid rough/pitted surfaces** — bacteria and allergens harbour in surface irregularities

### Avoid for Direct Food Contact
- Carbon steel (corrodes and contaminates)
- Galvanised steel (zinc contamination with acidic foods)
- Untreated aluminium with highly acidic foods (aluminium migration, discolouration)
- Chrome-plated items where plating is damaged

## Non-Stick Coatings (PTFE / Teflon)

- PTFE (polytetrafluoroethylene) coatings are food-safe at normal cooking temperatures (<260°C)
- Above 260°C, degradation begins; above 300°C, off-gassing of potentially toxic compounds
- Replace non-stick pans/trays if coating is scratched, flaking, or chipped — fragments in food present a physical hazard
- Do not use metal utensils on non-stick surfaces
- Wash by hand with soft cloth; not all are dishwasher-safe (check manufacturer)

## Single-Use Plastics

### UK Single-Use Plastics Restrictions (England, from October 2023)
The following single-use plastic items are banned in England:
- Single-use plastic plates
- Single-use plastic trays
- Single-use plastic bowls
- Single-use plastic cutlery (forks, knives, spoons, chopsticks)
- Single-use plastic balloon sticks
- Single-use plastic polystyrene food and beverage containers (for hot food/drink)

**Allowed** (for food contact): plastic packaging, plastic cups (except expanded polystyrene), plastic lids, plastic straws in some circumstances.

Scotland, Wales, and Northern Ireland have implemented similar or broader restrictions. Check current legislation for your jurisdiction.

### Alternatives to Single-Use Plastics
- Paper/cardboard containers (ensure food-grade; wax coating or plastic lining still present in some)
- Bagasse (sugarcane pulp) containers
- Cornstarch (PLA — polylactic acid) containers — compostable but not biodegradable in standard waste
- Reusable items (ceramic, metal, glass) — wash hygienically

---

## Reusing FCMs

- Only reuse FCMs that are **designed for reuse** and are in good condition
- Single-use containers (e.g. takeaway trays) must **not** be reused for food even if washed — they are not designed for repeated use and may migrate more on repeated contact
- Reusable containers: check manufacturer's maximum-use guidance
- Inspect before each use for cracks, discolouration, strong odour, or structural damage — discard if any present

---

## Inspecting and Replacing FCMs

### Daily Inspection
- All food-contact items should be visually inspected before use
- Reject and discard items with: cracks, chips, breaks, deep scoring, discolouration, rust, peeling coating, missing or illegible markings, strong unusual odour

### Scheduled Replacement
- Cutting boards: replace when scoring is deep or colour coding is unclear
- Rubber seals/gaskets on equipment: replace per manufacturer schedule or when showing signs of wear
- Non-stick pans: replace when coating is compromised
- Gloves: single-use disposable — never reuse; inspect for tears before putting on

## Record Keeping for FCMs

| Record | How | Retention |
|---|---|---|
| Declarations of Compliance from FCM suppliers | File by supplier/product | 5 years or product shelf life + 6 months |
| FCM inspection records | Daily / batch inspection log or visual check at point of use (minimal documentation required for routine items) | 2 years |
| Replacement records (damaged FCMs discarded) | Maintenance log | 2 years |
| Cleaning records for reusable FCMs | Cleaning schedule | 3 months to 1 year |
| Equipment service records (FCM-related parts) | Service file | 5 years |
