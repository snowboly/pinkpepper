# Pest Control in Food Premises — FSA Guidance

## Legal Basis

- **EC 852/2004, Annex II, Chapter IX, Para 4** — "Adequate procedures must be in place to control pests. Adequate procedures must also be in place to prevent domestic animals from having access to places where food is prepared, handled or stored."
- **EC 852/2004, Chapter I, Para 2(b)** — Premises design must protect against contact with toxic materials and pests
- **Food Safety Act 1990** (UK) — food must not be rendered injurious to health by pest contamination
- **Prevention of Damage by Pests Act 1949** (UK) — local authorities can require pest control action

---

## Integrated Pest Management (IPM)

IPM is the preferred approach to pest control in food premises. It combines multiple strategies in a hierarchy:

### IPM Hierarchy
1. **Prevention** (first line) — proofing, exclusion, good housekeeping
2. **Monitoring** — early detection via traps, sensors, regular inspection
3. **Physical control** — traps, EFKs, pheromone traps, ultrasonic devices
4. **Chemical control** (last resort) — rodenticides, insecticides used only where other methods insufficient; by licensed contractor only

## Common Pests and Risks

### Rodents (Rats and Mice)

| Pest | Key Indicators | Risks |
|---|---|---|
| **Brown rat** (*Rattus norvegicus*) | Droppings (larger, cylindrical, blunt ends), gnaw marks, burrows, smear marks, footprints, urine | Salmonella, Weil's disease (leptospirosis), structural damage, fire risk from gnawing cables |
| **House mouse** (*Mus musculus*) | Droppings (smaller, spindle-shaped), gnaw marks, nesting material, scratching sounds, urine pillars | Salmonella, Listeria, E. coli, gnawing contamination |
| **Ship rat** (*Rattus rattus*) | Droppings (slender, pointed), typically in roof spaces | Similar to brown rat |

### Flying Insects

| Pest | Key Indicators | Risks |
|---|---|---|
| **Blow flies / house flies** | Adult flies, puparia, maggots near drains/waste | Salmonella, E. coli, Campylobacter — mechanical vectors |
| **Fruit flies / vinegar flies** | Small flies around fruit, drains, fermented materials | Yeast contamination, cross-contamination |
| **Moths (stored product)** | Adults, larval webbing in dry goods | Product contamination; Indian meal moth common in flour, cereals |

### Stored Product Insects (SPIs)

| Pest | Affected Products | Indicators |
|---|---|---|
| **Grain weevil** | Flour, cereals, rice, grain | Adults in product, fine boring dust, heated grain |
| **Rust-red flour beetle** | Flour, cereals, spices, dried fruit | Adults in product, distinctive musty odour |
| **Tobacco beetle / larder beetle** | Cured meats, dried products, spices | Adults, larvae, frass in product |
| **Psocids (booklice)** | Damp grain, cereals, spices | Tiny insects in damp areas; indicates humidity problem |

### Cockroaches

| Species | Preferred Environment | Risk |
|---|---|---|
| **German cockroach** (*Blattella germanica*) | Warm, humid — kitchen equipment voids, dishwashers, behind fridges | Salmonella, E. coli — mechanical vector; nocturnal |
| **Oriental cockroach** (*Blatta orientalis*) | Cooler, damp — drains, basements, exterior | Similar pathogen risk |

Indicators: live/dead cockroaches (especially at night), egg cases (oothecae), droppings (cylindrical, dark), musty odour, smear marks around harbourage points.

### Birds

| Pest | Risks |
|---|---|
| Feral pigeons, gulls, sparrows | Salmonella, Campylobacter, Chlamydia psittaci (psittacosis); ectoparasites; nesting debris; droppings blocking drains/defacing food contact surfaces |

Birds are protected under the Wildlife and Countryside Act 1981 — only licensed methods of lethal control permitted.

## Proofing (Exclusion) — Prevention

Effective proofing is the most cost-effective pest control measure.

### Rodent Proofing
- All external openings ≥6 mm (for mice) or ≥12 mm (for rats) must be sealed
- Pipe penetrations through walls: seal with steel wool + expanding foam (or purpose-made collar)
- External doors: brush strips or rubber seals to reduce gap to <6 mm; automatic closers
- Drain covers: metal grilles or rodent-proof drain guards
- Roof vents: wire mesh ≥6 mm gauge stainless steel or galvanised
- Trees/vegetation pruned away from buildings by ≥1 metre

### Insect Proofing
- Window and vent screens (vent mesh aperture ≤1.5 mm for flies)
- Air curtains / fly screens on external doors
- External doors kept closed when not in use; self-closing mechanism fitted
- Strip curtains in high-traffic doorways (clean and maintained)
- LED lighting (UV-reduced) on external walls — less attractive to flying insects

### Bird Proofing
- Anti-perch spikes, wire, or gel on ledges, pipes, and structural beams
- Netting over roof spaces or loading bays
- Reflective tape and raptor kites as deterrents (limited effectiveness alone)

## Monitoring

### Bait Stations (Rodents)
- **Tamper-proof** bait stations only (with key lock where public access possible)
- Positioned on **rodent runs** — along walls, in corners, behind equipment (not in open areas)
- Inspected at intervals specified by the pest control contractor (typically weekly–monthly depending on risk)
- Record on site plan with numbering; log bait consumption, signs of activity, bait condition
- Bait stations must be outside food rooms where possible; if inside, non-toxic monitoring blocks used initially

### Electronic Fly Killers (EFKs)
- Installed where flying insects pose a risk; **not directly over food**
- UVA tubes replaced annually (before attractant effectiveness declines)
- Fitted with tray/board to catch dead insects — emptied and cleaned regularly (minimum weekly or when ≥30% full)
- Position: approximately 2 m from floor; away from natural light sources (which compete with UV)
- Log maintenance and catches on service record

### Pheromone Traps (Stored Product Insects)
- Placed in dry goods storage, ingredient stores
- Species-specific — identify correct trap for the target pest
- Check and replace at manufacturer-specified intervals
- High catch rates indicate active infestation — escalate to contractor

### Cockroach Monitoring
- Sticky/glue traps placed in harbourage areas (behind equipment, under sinks, in voids)
- Species identification from catches guides treatment
- Thermal imaging/endoscopes used by contractors for voids inspection

## Pest Control Contractor Requirements

For most food businesses, a professional pest control contractor is essential.

### What to Expect from a Contractor
- Qualified (BPCA/RSPH Certificate in Pest Control or equivalent)
- Full survey of premises before contract commencement
- **Site plan** showing monitoring device positions and numbers
- **Written service reports** after each visit — must identify: pests found, activity levels, bait consumption, recommendations
- Regular scheduled visits (frequency based on risk — quarterly minimum; monthly or more for high-risk)
- **Emergency call-out** availability
- Copies of contractor's public liability insurance and relevant licences retained on file
- COSHH data sheets for any pesticides used

### Rodenticide Use — Legal Requirements
- Only authorised rodenticides may be used (active ingredient listed on product label for that pest/situation)
- **Anticoagulant rodenticides** (brodifacoum, bromadiolone, difenacoum, etc.) must be used in accordance with the UK Campaign for Responsible Rodenticide Use (CRRU) code — bait must be in tamper-proof stations; bait removed when treatment is complete; dead rodents retrieved
- Rodenticide use inside food premises requires careful risk assessment — poisoned bait must not contact food

## Staff Reporting Procedure

All food handlers must be instructed to:
1. **Report immediately** any pest sightings to the supervisor/manager
2. **Do not handle** dead rodents without PPE
3. **Record** the sighting (date, time, location, type) in the pest sighting log
4. **Do not attempt** to set additional bait without contractor authorisation

A clearly posted internal pest sighting form or reporting mechanism must be in place.

## Action Levels

| Finding | Action Required |
|---|---|
| Live rodent in food production area | Immediate stop of production; notify contractor; deep clean; investigate entry point |
| Rodent droppings in food contact area | Immediate cleaning and disinfection; assess contamination risk; notify contractor |
| Live cockroach | Immediate isolation of area; notify contractor same day |
| EFK catch: sudden increase | Investigate source; increase frequency of cleaning; notify contractor |
| Live SPI in ingredients | Quarantine affected product; notify contractor; check adjacent stock |
| Bird activity near food intake | Proof access points; contact contractor; document |
