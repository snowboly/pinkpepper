# Cross-Contamination Prevention Guidance

## Introduction

Cross-contamination occurs when harmful bacteria, allergens, or foreign objects are transferred from one food to another. Preventing cross-contamination is a cornerstone of food safety. This guidance provides practical measures to minimize cross-contamination risks.

---

## Types of Cross-Contamination

### 1. Direct Cross-Contamination

Transfer from one food to another through direct contact:
- Raw meat touching ready-to-eat food
- Blood or juices dripping from raw food onto cooked food
- Unwashed vegetables contaminating salad

### 2. Indirect Cross-Contamination

Transfer via contaminated surfaces, equipment, or hands:
- Using same chopping board for raw chicken then cooked meat
- Using contaminated knife
- Touching raw food then ready-to-eat food without handwashing

### 3. Allergen Cross-Contamination

Transfer of allergenic ingredients to allergen-free food:
- Nut residue on shared equipment
- Flour dust settling on gluten-free products
- Shared utensils transferring allergenic ingredients

---

## Physical Separation

### Separate Areas (Best Practice)

**High-risk ready-to-eat areas** physically separated from raw food preparation:
- Separate rooms or clearly defined zones
- Physical barriers (walls, partitions, or marked floor lines)

**Workflow:**
- Design workflow to prevent raw and cooked food routes crossing
- Linear flow: Raw → Preparation → Cooking → Cooling → Ready-to-eat

### Zoning

If separate rooms not possible, use zoning:
- **Raw zone:** Dedicated area for raw meat, poultry, fish preparation
- **Ready-to-eat zone:** Separate area for cooked food, salads, ready-to-eat items
- Mark zones clearly (floor tape, signage)
- Staff do not cross zones without changing PPE and handwashing

---

## Equipment and Utensil Separation

### Colour-Coded System

Implement colour-coded equipment to prevent cross-contamination:

| Colour | Use |
|---|---|
| **Red** | Raw meat and poultry |
| **Blue** | Raw fish and seafood |
| **Yellow** | Cooked and ready-to-eat food |
| **Green** | Fruit, salad, vegetables (ready-to-eat) |
| **Brown** | Vegetables (to be cooked) |
| **White** | Bakery and dairy |

**Items to colour-code:**
- Chopping boards
- Knives
- Tongs and utensils
- Containers and trays
- Cleaning cloths and mops

### Storage of Equipment

- Store colour-coded equipment separately
- Label storage areas clearly
- Replace equipment when colour fades or item damaged

### Equipment Cleaning Between Tasks

- Wash, rinse, and sanitise all equipment after use
- Pay particular attention when switching from raw to ready-to-eat tasks
- Use separate dishcloths or disposable paper towels for cleaning

## Storage Controls

### Fridge Storage Hierarchy

Store food top-to-bottom in order of risk:

1. **Top shelf:** Ready-to-eat food, cooked food (fully covered)
2. **Middle shelves:** Dairy, prepared vegetables, eggs
3. **Bottom shelf:** Raw meat, poultry, fish (in sealed containers)

**Why?**
- Prevents drips from raw food contaminating ready-to-eat food below
- If raw meat leaks, it cannot contaminate other food

### Separate Storage

- Use separate fridges for raw and ready-to-eat food (if possible)
- If using same fridge: strictly enforce top-to-bottom rule; use sealed containers

### Covering and Packaging

- All food covered or in sealed containers
- Prevents cross-contamination from drips, dust, or contact
- Use food-grade cling film, lids, or lidded containers

### Dry Storage

- Store raw ingredients separately from ready-to-eat items
- High-risk allergens in sealed containers (nuts, sesame, flour)
- Cleaning chemicals in separate locked cupboard (never near food)

## Personal Hygiene

### Handwashing

**Critical for preventing cross-contamination.**

**Wash hands:**
- Before starting work
- After handling raw food
- After touching face, hair, or nose
- After using the toilet
- After handling waste or dirty items
- When switching between raw and ready-to-eat tasks
- After removing gloves

**20-second handwashing method:**
1. Wet hands; apply soap
2. Scrub palms, backs of hands, between fingers, thumbs, wrists (20 seconds)
3. Rinse thoroughly
4. Dry with single-use paper towel

### Gloves

- Gloves **not a substitute for handwashing**
- Change gloves between tasks (e.g., after handling raw food, before touching ready-to-eat food)
- Wash hands before putting gloves on and after removing gloves
- Discard gloves if torn or contaminated

### Protective Clothing

- Wear clean protective clothing (aprons, whites)
- Change apron when moving from raw to ready-to-eat tasks
- Remove apron before leaving food area (e.g., toilet, break)

## Cleaning and Sanitising

### Two-Stage Clean

1. **Clean:** Remove visible dirt and grease using detergent and water
2. **Sanitise:** Apply food-safe sanitiser to reduce bacteria

**Sanitisers only work on clean surfaces** — always clean first.

### Cleaning Frequency

- **Food contact surfaces:** Clean and sanitise after each use and between tasks
- **Floors:** Clean daily (or more frequently if visibly dirty)
- **Walls and high-level surfaces:** Clean weekly or monthly

### Colour-Coded Cleaning Equipment

- Use colour-coded cleaning cloths and mops matching food zones
- **Never use a cloth from a raw meat area in a ready-to-eat area**

### Sanitiser Contact Time

- Apply sanitiser and leave for required contact time (typically 30–60 seconds)
- Do not wipe off immediately
- Allow to air-dry

## Allergen Cross-Contamination Prevention

### Storage

- Store allergenic ingredients separately from allergen-free ingredients
- Use sealed containers
- Label clearly

### Equipment

- Use dedicated equipment for allergen-free production (e.g., gluten-free utensils)
- If shared equipment used: clean thoroughly and verify allergen removal

### Production Scheduling

- Produce allergen-free batches first (when equipment is clean)
- Produce allergenic batches last
- Clean and verify between allergen-containing and allergen-free batches

### Cleaning Validation

- Use allergen swab tests (if available) to verify allergen removal
- Rinse surfaces thoroughly after cleaning (remove allergenic residues)

## Pest Control

Pests are a source of contamination:
- Droppings contain harmful bacteria (Salmonella, E. coli)
- Body parts and hairs contaminate food
- Pests introduce allergens (e.g., cockroach allergen)

**Prevention:**
- Maintain pest control programme (monthly contractor visits)
- Proof building (seal gaps, fit door brushes, use insect screens)
- Good housekeeping (clean up spills, remove food waste promptly)

**See also:** `SOP-pest-control.md`

## Preventing Foreign Body Contamination

Foreign bodies (glass, metal, plastic, stones, etc.) can contaminate food.

### Controls

- **Glass and brittle plastic:** Minimize use in food areas; use shatterproof alternatives
- If breakage occurs: stop production; clear area; dispose of food nearby; clean thoroughly; inspect equipment

- **Metal:** Use metal-detectable plasters for cuts; use magnetic traps for metal fragments (if processing equipment used)

- **Packaging materials:** Remove outer packaging before entering food area (prevents cardboard, staples, plastic strapping contaminating food)

- **Personal items:** No pens, jewellery, buttons, or loose items in pockets in food areas

- **Maintenance:** Use food-grade lubricants; check equipment for loose parts before use

---

## Staff Training and Awareness

All staff must be trained on:
- Cross-contamination risks (direct, indirect, allergen)
- Colour-coded equipment system
- Handwashing technique and frequency
- Workflow and zone restrictions
- Cleaning and sanitising procedures

**Frequency:** Induction + annual refresher

---

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

1. **Using the same chopping board for raw and cooked food** (even if rinsed between)
2. **Not washing hands after handling raw food**
3. **Storing raw meat above ready-to-eat food** in fridge
4. **Using same utensils for raw and cooked without cleaning**
5. **Touching ready-to-eat food with bare hands** (use tongs, gloves, or utensils)
6. **Not changing apron between raw and ready-to-eat tasks**
7. **Leaving food uncovered** (allows dust, pests, or drips to contaminate)

---

## Key Takeaways

- **Separate raw and ready-to-eat food** physically and through workflow design
- **Use colour-coded equipment** and never cross-use
- **Store food in fridge top-to-bottom** (ready-to-eat at top, raw at bottom)
- **Wash hands frequently**, especially when switching tasks
- **Clean and sanitise surfaces and equipment** after handling raw food
- **Cover all food** to prevent contamination
- **Prevent allergen cross-contamination** through segregation, dedicated equipment, and cleaning validation
