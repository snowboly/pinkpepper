# UK General Food Law Guidance - FSA Update 2025

## Overview
The Food Standards Agency "General food law" guidance was updated on 29 October 2025 and explains how general food law operates in Great Britain and Northern Ireland.

- Jurisdiction: UK / GB / NI
- Source type: guidance
- Last updated: 29 October 2025
- Official source: https://www.food.gov.uk/business-guidance/general-food-law

## Core points
- In Great Britain, food law consists of domestic law plus assimilated law.
- In Northern Ireland, EU food law continues to apply alongside domestic law.
- The guidance highlights general food law duties on:
  - food safety
  - traceability
  - non-misleading presentation and food information
  - prompt withdrawal or recall of unsafe food

## Practical retrieval points
- Use this source for UK questions on the baseline duties created by assimilated Regulation 178/2002 in GB and EU Regulation (EC) No 178/2002 in NI.
- It is useful when the user asks about traceability, importer/exporter responsibilities, or when to withdraw or recall unsafe food.

## Compliance implications
- GB and NI should not be treated as identical for all food-law questions.
- Traceability and recall planning remain core legal duties even for small businesses.
- Where the bot discusses UK product withdrawal or recall, it should anchor the answer in general food law rather than only best practice.
