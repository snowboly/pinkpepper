# UK Food Incidents, Withdrawals and Recalls Guidance - FSA Update 2026

## Overview
The Food Standards Agency "Food incidents, product withdrawals and recalls" guidance was last updated on 24 March 2026. It explains current reporting, withdrawal, recall, and traceability expectations for UK food businesses.

- Jurisdiction: UK / GB / NI
- Source type: guidance
- Last updated: 24 March 2026
- Official source: https://www.food.gov.uk/business-guidance/food-incidents-product-withdrawals-and-recalls

## Core points
- A withdrawal removes unsafe food from the supply chain before it reaches consumers.
- A recall removes unsafe food from the supply chain and tells consumers what action to take.
- If a business believes food supplied is harmful, unfit, or non-compliant, it should immediately withdraw or recall the food and notify the competent authority.
- If unsafe food has already reached consumers, the business should tell the FSA incidents team.

## Traceability link
- The guidance ties incident response directly to traceability.
- Businesses need to be able to identify suppliers and food business customers quickly.
- The FSA guidance bundle covers:
  - traceability systems
  - decision-making for withdrawals and recalls
  - roles and responsibilities
  - how to inform consumers of a recall

## Practical retrieval points
- Use this source for UK recall procedure questions, especially where the user asks what to do first after discovering unsafe food.
- It is helpful for answers on notification duties, traceability evidence, and recall communications.

## Compliance implications
- Recall readiness is not only a crisis topic; it depends on everyday supplier and customer traceability records.
- Businesses should keep a documented escalation path for incidents, not improvise one after a product has reached consumers.
- Root cause analysis and corrective action should follow the incident response, not replace it.
