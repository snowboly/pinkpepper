# UK Managing Food Safety Guidance - FSA Update 2026

## Overview
The Food Standards Agency "Managing food safety" guidance was updated on 14 January 2026. It sets out current FSA expectations for food safety management, HACCP-based procedures, supplier checks, traceability, recall readiness, and training.

- Jurisdiction: UK / GB / NI
- Source type: guidance
- Last updated: 14 January 2026
- Official source: https://www.food.gov.uk/business-guidance/managing-food-safety

## Core points
- Food businesses must have food safety management procedures based on HACCP principles.
- Procedures should be kept current, documented, and reviewed when operations change.
- Supplier checks, traceability, withdrawal/recall readiness, and staff training are all part of managing food safety, not optional extras.

## 2026 Listeria point
- The FSA notes that Commission Regulation (EU) 2024/2895 will apply in the EU and Northern Ireland from 1 July 2026.
- The change affects Annex I, Chapter 1, criterion 1.2(b) of Regulation (EC) No 2073/2005.
- The updated requirement is that Listeria monocytogenes must not be detected in 25 g during shelf life for the relevant ready-to-eat products, unless the food business can rely on the alternative criterion that allows demonstration the product will not exceed 100 cfu/g through shelf life.

- Use this source for current FSA expectations on HACCP review, supplier controls, traceability records, and recall readiness.

## Compliance implications
- UK businesses should review shelf-life studies and validation evidence for ready-to-eat foods where Listeria is relevant.
- NI-facing businesses should treat the 1 July 2026 application date as operationally important.
- HACCP documentation should link microbiological control decisions to current shelf-life evidence, not old assumptions.
