# Chilling and Freezing Guidance for Food Businesses

## Introduction

Proper chilling and freezing are essential for controlling bacterial growth and maintaining food safety. This guidance covers storage temperatures, cooling procedures, and best practices for chilled and frozen food.

---

## Legal Temperature Requirements

### Chilled Food

**Legal maximum:** ≤8°C (The Food Hygiene (England) Regulations 2006)

**Industry best practice:** 0–5°C (required by most food safety certification schemes)

### Frozen Food

**Recommended:** ≤-18°C (FSA guidance and industry standard)

---

## Chilled Storage Best Practices

### Fridge Temperature Control

- Maintain fridge temperature at **0–5°C** (target)
- Monitor and record temperatures **twice daily** (minimum)
- Never exceed 8°C (legal maximum)

### Thermometer Placement

- Place thermometer in **warmest part of fridge** (usually top front or door area)
- Avoid placing near cooling vents (coldest spot; not representative)

### Loading and Airflow

- **Do not overload fridges** (restricts cold air circulation)
- Leave space between items for air to circulate
- Do not block air vents
- Store food in containers (not directly on shelves)

### Fridge Storage Hierarchy (Top to Bottom)

1. **Top:** Ready-to-eat food, cooked food (covered)
2. **Middle:** Dairy, prepared vegetables, eggs
3. **Bottom:** Raw meat, poultry, fish (in sealed containers to prevent drips)

### Covering Food

- All food covered or in sealed containers
- Prevents cross-contamination and drying out
- Use food-grade cling film, lids, or containers

---

## Frozen Storage Best Practices

### Freezer Temperature Control

- Maintain freezer temperature at **≤-18°C**
- Monitor and record temperatures **twice daily** (minimum)
- Food should be solid frozen (no soft areas or ice crystal loss)

### Freezer Loading

- Do not overload with fresh food (raises temperature)
- Freeze in small batches
- Allow space for air circulation

### Freezer Burn Prevention

- Wrap food tightly in freezer-safe packaging
- Remove excess air
- Use airtight containers
- Label with date and contents

## Cooling Hot Food

### Cooling Requirement

Cooked food must be cooled rapidly to prevent bacterial growth:

**Target:** Cool from **75°C to 8°C within 90 minutes** (best practice)

**Legal maximum:** Cool from **60°C to 21°C within 2 hours**, then to **8°C or below within a further 4 hours** (total 6 hours maximum)

### Danger Zone

**5°C–63°C is the "danger zone"** where bacteria multiply rapidly.

Food should spend as little time as possible in this zone.

### Rapid Cooling Methods

1. **Blast chiller** (preferred method)
   - Cools food rapidly using high-velocity cold air
   - Can achieve 90-minute cooling target

2. **Shallow containers**
   - Transfer hot food to shallow containers (depth ≤50mm)
   - Increases surface area for faster heat loss

3. **Ice bath**
   - Place container of hot food in larger container filled with ice and water
   - Stir food occasionally to distribute cold

4. **Portion and spread**
   - Divide large batches into smaller portions
   - Spread on clean trays (increases surface area)

5. **Stir frequently**
   - Stir liquids and semi-solid foods to distribute heat

### What NOT to Do

- **Do not place hot food directly in fridge or freezer** (raises internal temperature; endangers other food)
- **Do not cover tightly during initial cooling** (traps heat; slows cooling)
- **Do not stack containers during cooling** (insulates food; slows cooling)

### Cooling Monitoring

- Record cooling temperatures at intervals:
  - Start temperature (T0)
  - 30 minutes (T30)
  - 60 minutes (T60)
  - 90 minutes (T90) — Target: ≤8°C achieved

- If cooling too slowly: transfer to smaller containers; use blast chiller; use ice bath

- **Discard food if >4 hours elapsed and still >8°C** (bacteria may have multiplied to unsafe levels)

## Defrosting (Thawing) Frozen Food

### Safe Defrosting Methods

**1. In the fridge (safest method)**
- Place frozen food in fridge to defrost slowly
- Keep in sealed container or on tray (catch drips)
- Allow sufficient time (large items may take 24–48 hours)
- Maintain fridge temperature ≤5°C

**2. Microwave defrost setting**
- Use only if cooking immediately after defrosting
- Follow microwave instructions
- Cook straight away (do not re-chill partially defrosted food)

**3. Cold running water**
- Place food in sealed waterproof bag
- Submerge in cold running water
- Change water regularly (every 30 minutes)
- Cook immediately after thawing

**4. Cook from frozen (if product designed for this)**
- Some products can be cooked from frozen (check manufacturer instructions)
- Increase cooking time; verify core temperature reaches ≥75°C

### Unsafe Defrosting Methods

- **Do NOT defrost at room temperature** (bacteria multiply in outer layers while centre still frozen)
- **Do NOT defrost in hot water** (raises surface temperature into danger zone)
- **Do NOT leave defrosting food uncovered** (cross-contamination risk)

### After Defrosting

- Use defrosted food within **24 hours**
- **Do not refreeze thawed raw food** (unless cooked first)
- Cooked food that was frozen can be refrozen after reheating to ≥75°C (though quality may suffer)

## Stock Rotation

### FIFO (First In, First Out)

- Use older stock before newer stock
- Place new deliveries behind existing stock
- Check and rotate stock daily

### Date Labelling

- Label all food with:
  - **Use by / Best before date**
  - **Date opened** (if applicable)
  - **Batch code** (for traceability)

### Use-By Dates

- **Do not use food past its use-by date** (safety date)
- Discard expired food
- Monitor dates daily

---

## Fridge and Freezer Maintenance

### Daily Checks

- Check and record temperatures twice daily
- Visual check for frost build-up (freezer)
- Check door seals close properly
- Clean up spills immediately

### Weekly Checks

- Deep clean interior (empty, wash with detergent, sanitise)
- Check gaskets and seals for wear
- Defrost manual-defrost freezers if ice build-up >5mm

### Annual Service

- Refrigeration engineer service annually
- Gas pressure check, thermostat calibration, condenser cleaning
- Obtain service certificate

### Breakdown Procedure

If fridge or freezer breaks down:

1. **Check temperature immediately**
2. **Keep door closed** (retains cold)
3. **Call refrigeration engineer**
4. **Transfer stock to working unit if possible**
5. **Monitor food temperature:**
   - Fridge: if >8°C for >4 hours, discard high-risk ready-to-eat food
   - Freezer: if food thawed or >-12°C, discard or assess safety
6. **Record incident and action taken**

---

## Deliveries of Chilled and Frozen Food

### Temperature Checks on Delivery

- Check temperature of at least **3 random items** per delivery
- Use calibrated probe thermometer
- Record temperatures on goods inward log

**Acceptance criteria:**
- Chilled: ≤8°C (preferably ≤5°C)
- Frozen: ≤-12°C (solid frozen; no signs of thawing)

**Rejection criteria:**
- Chilled: >8°C
- Frozen: Signs of thawing (soft, ice crystals melted, packaging wet)

### Delivery Vehicle Checks

- Check vehicle is refrigerated and clean
- Check temperature display (if fitted)
- Report concerns to supplier

### Immediate Storage

- Transfer chilled and frozen food to cold storage **immediately** on delivery
- Do not leave in ambient conditions

## Freezing Fresh Food

### Foods Suitable for Freezing

- Raw meat, poultry, fish
- Cooked dishes (soups, stews, casseroles)
- Bread, pastry, baked goods
- Vegetables (blanch first)
- Fruit

### Foods Not Suitable for Freezing

- Salad leaves (high water content; go limp)
- Soft cheese (texture changes)
- Eggs in shell (expand and crack)
- Mayonnaise and cream-based sauces (separate)

### Freezing Procedure

1. Cool cooked food rapidly before freezing
2. Wrap tightly in freezer-safe packaging
3. Remove excess air
4. Label with product name, date frozen, use-by date
5. Freeze in small batches (avoid raising freezer temperature)

### Freezer Life

| Food | Recommended Freezer Life |
|------|--------------------------|
| Raw beef, lamb, pork | 6–12 months |
| Raw poultry | 9–12 months |
| Raw fish (oily) | 3 months |
| Raw fish (white) | 6 months |
| Cooked dishes | 2–3 months |
| Bread | 3 months |
| Fruit and vegetables | 8–12 months |

---

## Key Takeaways

- **Chill food at 0–5°C** (legal maximum 8°C)
- **Freeze food at ≤-18°C**
- **Cool hot food rapidly** (≤8°C within 90 minutes preferred)
- **Monitor and record temperatures twice daily**
- **Defrost in the fridge** (safest method)
- **Rotate stock (FIFO)**; check use-by dates daily
- **Do not refreeze thawed raw food** unless cooked first
