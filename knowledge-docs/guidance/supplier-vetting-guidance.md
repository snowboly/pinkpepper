# Supplier Vetting and Approval Guidance

## Introduction

Supplier approval is a legal requirement under EU and UK food law. Food businesses must ensure that raw materials and ingredients are sourced from approved suppliers who meet food safety standards. This guidance provides practical steps for vetting and approving suppliers.

---

## Legal Requirements

**Regulation (EC) No 852/2004, Article 5** requires food businesses to:
- Identify and control food safety hazards throughout the supply chain
- Ensure suppliers comply with food safety requirements
- Verify supplier compliance through approval and monitoring systems

**Failure to approve and monitor suppliers can result in:**
- Enforcement action (improvement notices, prosecutions)
- Product recalls due to supplier failures
- Damage to reputation

---

## Risk-Based Supplier Approval

Not all suppliers present the same level of risk. Tailor approval requirements to the risk level.

### Risk Classification

| Risk Level | Examples | Approval Requirements |
|---|---|---|
| **High Risk** | Raw meat, poultry, fish, dairy, ready-to-eat products, products for vulnerable groups | Full approval (audit or third-party certification required) |
| **Medium Risk** | Dry ingredients, canned goods, frozen vegetables, low-risk processed foods | Documentary approval + sampling/testing |
| **Low Risk** | Packaging materials, cleaning chemicals (non-food-contact), low-risk ancillary items | Basic approval (supplier questionnaire + company registration) |

## Supplier Approval Process

### Step 1: Initial Information Gathering

Request the following from the supplier:

**All Suppliers:**
- Company name, address, and contact details
- Company registration number
- Food business registration or approval number
- Public liability and product liability insurance (minimum £5 million cover recommended)
- Emergency contact details (24/7)

**Food Suppliers (High and Medium Risk):**
- Food safety certification (BRC, SALSA, ISO 22000, FSSC 22000, SQF, or equivalent)
- HACCP plan summary or food safety management system documentation
- Allergen management procedures
- Traceability procedures
- Product specifications (for each product supplied)

**Food Contact Material Suppliers:**
- Declaration of compliance with EC Regulation 1935/2004
- Migration test certificates (if applicable for direct food-contact materials)
- Material safety data sheets

### Step 2: Verify Third-Party Certification (High-Risk Suppliers)

**If supplier holds certification (BRC, SALSA, ISO 22000, etc.):**
1. Request copy of current certificate
2. Verify certificate details:
   - Scheme and standard version
   - Scope covers products being supplied
   - Expiry date (certificate in date)
   - Certification body is accredited (check accreditation mark)
3. Check certificate authenticity (contact certification body if in doubt)
4. Obtain copy of latest audit report (if supplier willing to share)

**If certificate valid and scope appropriate:** Proceed to Step 4 (trial order)

**If no third-party certification:** Proceed to Step 3 (supplier audit)

### Step 3: Supplier Audit (if no third-party certification)

**When to audit:**
- High-risk supplier with no third-party certification
- Supplier in country with weak regulatory oversight
- Previous supplier performance issues

**Audit checklist should cover:**
- Senior management commitment to food safety
- HACCP system and prerequisite programmes
- Premises and equipment (layout, hygiene, maintenance)
- Cleaning and pest control
- Personnel hygiene and training
- Supplier approval and raw material controls
- Traceability and recall capability
- Allergen management
- Corrective action and complaints handling

**Audit outcome:**
- **Pass:** Supplier approved subject to trial order
- **Conditional pass:** Corrective actions required; re-audit or evidence required before approval
- **Fail:** Supplier not approved; seek alternative supplier

**Auditor:**
- Trained internal auditor (Level 3 HACCP or equivalent)
- Independent third-party auditor or consultant (recommended for critical suppliers)

### Step 4: Product Sampling and Testing (Medium and High Risk)

Request product samples for evaluation:

**Sensory Evaluation:**
- Appearance, smell, texture, taste (if safe to taste)
- Packaging integrity
- Labelling accuracy

**Laboratory Testing:**
- **Microbiological testing:** Total Viable Count (TVC), Enterobacteriaceae, pathogens (Salmonella, Listeria, E. coli) as appropriate to product risk
- **Chemical testing:** Allergen testing (if allergen-free claims made), additive levels, contaminants (heavy metals, pesticides) if risk-assessed
- **Authenticity testing:** Species identification (meat, fish), origin verification if required

**Test results:**
- Pass: Results meet specifications; proceed to trial order
- Fail: Discuss with supplier; request corrective action or reject supplier

### Step 5: Trial Order

Place small trial order to assess:
- Delivery reliability (on-time delivery)
- Delivery vehicle condition and temperature control (chilled/frozen products)
- Product quality and consistency
- Packaging integrity
- Labelling accuracy (ingredients, allergens, date marks, batch codes)
- Temperature on arrival (probe check)

**Trial outcome:**
- Satisfactory: Approve supplier and add to Approved Supplier List
- Unsatisfactory: Reject supplier or request improvements and re-trial

### Step 6: Approval Decision and Documentation

**Manager or designated approver** reviews all documentation and trial results.

**Approval decision:**
- ☐ Approved: May be used for ordering
- ☐ Conditionally approved: Corrective actions required; re-review by [date]
- ☐ Rejected: Do not use; seek alternative supplier

**Document and record:**
- Supplier approval form completed and signed
- Supplier added to **Approved Supplier List** with:
  - Supplier name, address, contact details
  - Products supplied
  - Risk category
  - Approval method (certification / audit / documentary)
  - Approval date
  - Review due date (annual or risk-based)
  - Certification expiry date (if applicable)

---

## Approved Supplier List

### Maintaining the List

- Maintain an up-to-date **Approved Supplier List** (spreadsheet or database)
- Only suppliers on the list may be used for purchasing
- List accessible to purchasing staff
- Manager authorizes all additions, changes, or removals

### Review Frequency

- **High-risk suppliers:** Review annually (or when certificate expires, whichever is sooner)
- **Medium-risk suppliers:** Review annually
- **Low-risk suppliers:** Review every 2 years or when changes occur

---

## Ongoing Supplier Monitoring

Approval is not a one-time activity. Suppliers must be monitored continuously.

### Delivery Inspections

**Every delivery must be inspected on arrival:**
- Vehicle cleanliness and temperature (chilled/frozen deliveries)
- Product temperature (use calibrated probe thermometer)
- Packaging integrity (no damage, tears, leaks)
- Labelling accuracy (check batch codes, date marks, allergen information)
- Absence of pests or pest damage
- Sensory check (appearance, smell)

**Record:** Goods inward inspection log

**Rejection:** Non-conforming deliveries rejected; supplier notified; corrective action requested

### Performance Metrics

Track supplier performance:
- On-time delivery rate (target ≥95%)
- Product rejection rate (target ≤2%)
- Temperature compliance (chilled/frozen deliveries) (target 100%)
- Labelling accuracy (target 100%)
- Customer complaints linked to supplier (target 0)

**Action if targets not met:**
- Contact supplier; request corrective action
- Increase monitoring frequency (sample more deliveries)
- Request evidence of corrective actions
- Consider suspending supplier if performance does not improve

### Product Testing Programme

- Periodic sampling and testing of high-risk products
- **Frequency:** Quarterly or as risk-assessed
- Tests: Microbiological, chemical (allergens, additives), authenticity

## Annual Supplier Review

### Review Process

All approved suppliers reviewed at least annually:

**Review includes:**
- Supplier performance data (delivery, quality, rejections, complaints)
- Certification status (check certificates still valid; request updated certificates if expired)
- Any changes to supplier processes, products, or ownership
- Results of product testing (if conducted)
- Audit findings (if audit conducted during the year)

**Review outcome:**
- **Remain approved:** Continue supply
- **Conditional approval:** Corrective action required; re-review in 3–6 months
- **Suspend:** Stop ordering until issues resolved; request evidence or re-audit
- **Remove approval:** Cease supply; source alternative supplier

**Record:** Supplier review record (date, reviewer, outcome, actions)

## Supplier Non-Conformance Management

### When Supplier Fails to Meet Requirements

**Trigger events:**
- Delivery rejected (temperature, quality, labelling)
- Customer complaint linked to supplier
- Test failure (microbiological, allergen)
- Certificate expired
- Supplier process change not notified

**Action:**
1. **Raise supplier non-conformance report**
   - Date, product, issue description, evidence
2. **Notify supplier immediately** (phone + written confirmation)
3. **Request corrective action plan**
   - Root cause investigation
   - Corrective actions and timescales
   - Preventive measures
4. **Quarantine affected stock** (if applicable)
5. **Verify corrective action effectiveness** (check subsequent deliveries; request evidence)

### Escalation

**Minor non-conformance (isolated incident):**
- Supplier corrects issue
- Monitor next delivery
- Close non-conformance if resolved

**Major non-conformance or repeated issues:**
- Suspend supplier (stop ordering)
- Request urgent corrective action and evidence
- Conduct supplier audit or request third-party audit
- Source alternative supplier if corrective action inadequate

**Critical non-conformance (immediate food safety risk):**
- Reject all stock from supplier
- Suspend supplier immediately
- Quarantine or recall affected product (if distributed)
- Notify enforcement authority if required (Environmental Health Officer or FSA)

## Supplier Changes

Suppliers must notify the food business **in advance** of any changes to:
- Ingredients, formulation, or suppliers of raw materials
- Manufacturing process or location
- Allergen status
- Ownership or management

**Food business action:**
- Review change impact (food safety, allergen, quality risk)
- Request updated documentation (specification, certificate, test results)
- Re-approve supplier if significant change
- Update product risk assessment and HACCP plan
- Update labelling if required

**Unapproved changes:** Reject deliveries until approval obtained.

## Supplier Contracts and Specifications

### Written Agreements (High and Medium Risk)

Maintain written agreements with suppliers covering:
- Product specifications (ingredients, quality standards, shelf life)
- Allergen declarations
- Delivery requirements (temperature, packaging, lead time)
- Traceability and batch coding
- Notification of changes (advance notice required)
- Right to audit (food business may audit supplier premises)
- Product recall cooperation (supplier must participate in recalls)

### Product Specifications

Each product must have a documented specification including:
- Product name and description
- Ingredient list (with sub-ingredients if applicable)
- Allergen declaration (14 major allergens highlighted)
- Microbiological and chemical standards
- Pack size, packaging type, and materials
- Storage conditions and shelf life
- Country of origin (if relevant for labelling)

**Review specifications:** Annually or when changes occur

## Traceability from Suppliers

### Supplier Responsibilities

Suppliers must:
- Provide batch codes or lot numbers on all deliveries
- Maintain traceability one-step-back to their suppliers
- Provide traceability information within **4 hours** on request
- Participate in product recall or withdrawal if required

**Verification:**
- Conduct traceability exercise with suppliers annually
- Request batch information for random delivery
- Verify supplier can trace within 4 hours

**See also:** `SOP-traceability-recall.md`

## Emergency Supplier Approval

In exceptional circumstances (regular supplier fails; urgent production need):

**Temporary approval** may be granted for up to **3 months** subject to:
1. Minimum documentary checks (registration, insurance, allergen declaration)
2. Manager authorization
3. Enhanced goods-inward inspection (sample every delivery)
4. Full approval process completed within 3 months

**Record:** Justification for emergency approval; expiry date; full approval deadline

---

## Key Takeaways

- **All suppliers must be approved** before use (legal requirement)
- **Risk-based approach:** High-risk suppliers require third-party certification or audit
- **Verify certificates:** Check validity, scope, and expiry
- **Conduct trial orders** before full approval
- **Monitor supplier performance** continuously (delivery inspections, metrics, testing)
- **Review suppliers annually** (check certificates, performance, changes)
- **Manage non-conformances** promptly (suspend if necessary)
- **Maintain Approved Supplier List** and restrict purchasing to approved suppliers only
