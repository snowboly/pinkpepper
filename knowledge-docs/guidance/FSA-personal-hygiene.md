# Personal Hygiene in Food Premises — FSA Guidance

## Legal Basis

- **EC 852/2004, Annex II, Chapter VIII** — personal hygiene requirements for all food handlers
- **Food Safety Act 1990** — it is an offence to sell food unsafe due to contamination (including from humans)
- **Health and Safety at Work Act 1974** — duty on employers to protect workers and public; employee duty to co-operate

---

## Handwashing

Handwashing is the single most important personal hygiene control in any food business.

### When to Wash Hands

Food handlers must wash hands:
- **Before** starting work
- **After** handling raw food (meat, fish, eggs, vegetables)
- **After** touching the face, nose, mouth, or hair
- **After** coughing or sneezing
- **After** eating, drinking, or smoking
- **After** handling waste or refuse
- **After** using the toilet
- **After** handling money
- **After** handling cleaning materials or equipment
- **After** touching packaging, phones, keys, or other non-food items
- **After** handling allergen-containing ingredients (before moving to allergen-free tasks)
- **When moving** between raw and ready-to-eat food handling
- **On return** from breaks

### Correct Handwashing Procedure (20-second method)

1. Wet hands with clean, running water (warm preferred)
2. Apply sufficient liquid soap
3. Rub palms together, fingers interlaced, backs of hands, between fingers, around thumbs, tips of fingers and nails — minimum **20 seconds** of scrubbing
4. Rinse thoroughly under running water
5. Dry completely with **single-use paper towels** (not a shared cloth towel — reusable cloth towels are not permitted in food handling areas)
6. Use paper towel to turn off the tap if it is not elbow/sensor operated

### Handwash Station Requirements (EC 852/2004)
- Sufficient number of dedicated **handwashing sinks** — separate from sinks used for washing food or equipment
- **Hot and cold running water** (or blended warm)
- Liquid soap (antibacterial where risk warrants it)
- Single-use paper towels or hygienic dryer (not warm air dryers in high-risk environments)
- Located in or adjacent to food handling areas; also in toilet facilities
- Must not be used for food washing or utensil washing

### Hand Sanitiser
Hand sanitiser (e.g. 70% alcohol gel) is a **supplement to**, not a replacement for, handwashing. It is effective after thorough handwashing for additional decontamination before handling high-risk RTE foods. Sanitiser is not effective against:
- Clostridium difficile spores
- Norovirus (limited efficacy — soap and water preferred)
- Cryptosporidium

## Protective Clothing

### Requirements

| Item | Standard |
|---|---|
| **Overalls / whites / apron** | Clean, appropriate colour for work area, changed daily or when contaminated |
| **Hair covering** (cap, hairnet, beard snood) | Required in food production/open food handling; all hair must be covered |
| **Footwear** | Dedicated, clean; toe-capped in some environments; not worn outside the food area |
| **Gloves** | When handling RTE foods or where risk warrants; single-use food-grade gloves; changed between tasks and allergen types; handwashing still required even when gloves worn |

### Colour Coding (Best Practice)
Different coloured uniforms or aprons can identify food handler areas or allergen roles, e.g.:
- **White/light**: general food handling
- **Blue**: raw meat handling
- **Green**: salad/RTE area
- **Red**: cleaning duties

### Storing Protective Clothing
- Stored separately from outdoor/personal clothing in a designated changing area or locker
- Not worn outside the food premises (risk of contamination from external environment)
- Laundered at high temperature (≥60°C) or by commercial laundry

## Jewellery, Accessories and Personal Items

### Prohibited in Food Handling Areas
- All jewellery **except** a plain wedding band and plain sleeper earrings (where ears are fully covered by hairnet)
- Watches (including smart watches)
- Nail varnish and false/acrylic nails — harbour bacteria; can flake into food; disguise dirt
- Long natural nails — must be kept short and clean
- Piercings (including ear stretchers, tongue piercings, facial piercings)
- False eyelashes

### Personal Items
- Mobile phones: must not be taken into food handling areas; designated use areas only (e.g. break room); if essential for work (e.g. digital checks), dedicated device kept clean
- Food/drink: consumed only in designated break areas, not in food handling areas
- Smoking: in designated areas only, away from food; hands washed on return

## Fitness to Work — Illness and Exclusion

### When to Exclude Staff

Food handlers must **not work with open food** if they have or are suspected of having:

| Condition | Exclusion Requirement |
|---|---|
| **Diarrhoea** | Exclude immediately; **48 hours minimum** after last episode (symptoms completely resolved) |
| **Vomiting** | Exclude immediately; **48 hours minimum** after last episode |
| **Diarrhoea and vomiting combined** | 48 hours from resolution of both |
| **Salmonella, E. coli O157, Cryptosporidium, Typhoid/Paratyphoid (enteric fever)** | Exclude until two negative stool samples 24 hours apart (microbiologically cleared) — contact local authority Health Protection Team for guidance |
| **Norovirus (confirmed or suspected)** | 48 hours after last episode (norovirus is highly infectious) |
| **Infected cuts, sores, boils, skin infections** | Exclude from open food handling; may work if wound fully covered with a **blue waterproof plaster** (blue = visible if it falls into food; waterproof = seals wound from food) |
| **Jaundice (suspected hepatitis A)** | Exclude until medically cleared |
| **Respiratory infection with cough/sneezing** | Do not handle open food; consider full exclusion; face covering may be appropriate |

### Return-to-Work Procedure
- Staff must report illness before their shift, not on arrival
- Manager/supervisor confirms symptoms have resolved and exclusion period is met
- For notifiable conditions (Salmonella, E. coli O157, typhoid, etc.) — return only with medical clearance
- Return-to-work interview recommended and recorded

### Notifiable Diseases
Certain conditions are **notifiable** to the local authority / HPT under the Health Protection (Notification) Regulations 2010:
- Typhoid fever / Paratyphoid fever
- Food poisoning (confirmed and suspected)
- Cholera
- Cryptosporidiosis

Responsible food handlers have a legal duty to notify if they know or suspect they have a notifiable disease.

## Cuts, Wounds and Sores

- All cuts on hands must be covered with a **blue waterproof plaster** before working with food
- Blue colour makes plasters visible if they fall into food
- Plasters should be metal-detectable where metal detection is in place
- Plasters count-checked at start and end of each shift (plaster log)
- If a plaster is lost/falls off during food handling: stop work immediately, quarantine product handled since last plaster check, investigate and retrieve if possible

## Visitor Hygiene

All visitors to food production areas must:
- Be provided with suitable protective clothing (hairnet, overalls, footwear covers)
- Receive a hygiene briefing before entry
- Not be permitted to enter if they have GI symptoms
- Sign a visitor hygiene declaration (confirming no current illness)
- Be accompanied by a responsible member of staff

---

## Training Requirements

- All food handlers must receive **food hygiene training** commensurate with their role before working unsupervised
- Training must be documented (date, subject, trainer, duration, signature)
- Minimum recognised standard: **Level 2 Award in Food Safety** (Highfield, CIEH, RSPH, or equivalent)
- Supervisors and those responsible for HACCP: **Level 3 Award in Food Safety** recommended
- Allergen awareness training: all staff handling food must complete allergen awareness training
- Refresher training: every 3 years minimum, or when new hazards are introduced

---

## Staff Personal Hygiene Policy

Every food business should have a written personal hygiene policy covering:
- Handwashing requirements
- Exclusion and fitness-to-work rules
- Jewellery, nail, and accessory restrictions
- Uniform and protective clothing requirements
- Mobile phone and personal item policy
- Cuts and wound management
- Smoking, eating, and drinking rules
- Visitor hygiene procedure

All new staff should sign the policy on induction. Displayed reminders (handwashing posters) should be placed at all handwashing points.
