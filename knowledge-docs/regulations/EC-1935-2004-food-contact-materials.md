# REGULATION (EC) No 1935/2004 — Food Contact Materials (FCM)

## Overview
The framework regulation for all materials and articles intended to contact food ("food contact materials" or FCMs). Sets general principles applying to all FCMs, with specific measures for individual material types addressed in supplementary regulations (most importantly EU 10/2011 for plastics). UK retained as UK Regulation 1935/2004 post-Brexit.

---

## Scope — What Is a Food Contact Material?

FCMs include:

| Category | Examples |
|---|---|
| Plastics | PP, PE, PET, PS, PVC food trays, bottles, wrapping films, cutting boards |
| Rubber | Seals, gaskets, conveyor belts |
| Paper and board | Packaging boxes, baking paper, grease-proof paper |
| Metals | Stainless steel worktops, aluminium foil, tin cans |
| Glass | Bottles, jars |
| Ceramics | Plates, cups, cooking pots |
| Coatings | Non-stick coatings (PTFE), lacquers on can interiors |
| Silicone | Bakeware, spatulas, oven mats |
| Wood | Cutting boards, spoons, barrels |
| Ion exchange resins | Water softening/filtering |
| Active and intelligent materials | Oxygen absorbers, freshness indicators |

**Also in scope**: materials used during primary production, processing, storage, distribution, and catering.

**Not in scope**: antiques; covering/coating materials forming part of the food itself (e.g. cheese rind, meat casings intended to be consumed with food).

---

## Article 3 — General Requirements (Inertness Principle)

FCMs must be manufactured so that **under normal or foreseeable conditions of use** they do not:
1. **Transfer** their constituents to food in quantities that could endanger human health
2. **Bring about** an unacceptable change in the composition of the food
3. **Bring about** a deterioration in the organoleptic characteristics of the food (taste, smell, appearance, texture)

This is the fundamental "inertness" or "non-transfer" principle.

---

## Article 4 — Active and Intelligent Materials

Active FCMs are permitted — those designed to extend shelf life or maintain/improve food condition by:
- Absorbing oxygen, moisture, ethylene
- Releasing preservatives, antioxidants, antimicrobials, carbon dioxide

They must still meet Article 3 and must be labelled "active food contact material."

Intelligent FCMs monitor condition or history of the food (e.g. time-temperature indicators) — permitted but must not mislead consumers.

---

## Article 15 — Labelling of FCMs

FCMs must carry at point of sale (to the final consumer) either:
- The words **"for food contact"**, or
- The **glass-and-fork symbol** (stylised fork and wine glass), or
- Specific intended use (e.g. "for use as a coffee cup")

Additionally, at pre-retail stage (business-to-business), FCMs must be accompanied by:
- Name/address of the business placing on the market
- Identification number of the material/article
- Declaration of compliance (see below)
- Adequate labelling to allow traceability
- Where appropriate: instructions for use, storage conditions, intended use, maximum/minimum temperatures

---

## Article 16 — Declaration of Compliance (DoC)

FBOs using approved specific measures must provide a written **Declaration of Compliance** stating the material complies with applicable regulations. For plastics this is mandatory (EU 10/2011). For other materials it is recommended best practice.

### DoC Must Include:
- Identity of the material/article
- Date of the declaration
- Identity and address of the business supplying the FCM
- Identity of the substances or materials covered
- Reference to applicable regulation(s)
- Information on substances subject to restriction (migration limits, SMLs, OMTs)
- Test results supporting compliance (or reference to test conditions)

**FBOs receiving FCMs should obtain and retain DoCs from all suppliers.**

## EU Regulation 10/2011 — Plastics

The most detailed specific measure. Applies to plastic food contact materials and articles.

### Union List of Authorised Substances
Only substances on the positive list may be used in plastic FCMs (with some national derogations for the time being). Each substance has:
- A **FCM reference number**
- Permitted use conditions
- Specific Migration Limits (SMLs) where applicable

### Migration Limits

| Limit Type | Abbreviation | Value |
|---|---|---|
| **Overall Migration Limit** | OML | 10 mg/dm² or 60 mg/kg food equivalent |
| **Specific Migration Limit** | SML | Per substance (e.g. SML for formaldehyde = 15 mg/kg) |

Migration testing conditions specified in Regulation (EU) 10/2011 Annex V simulate worst-case contact conditions (simulant type, time, temperature).

### Food Simulants

| Simulant | Code | Simulates |
|---|---|---|
| Ethanol 10% (v/v) | A | Aqueous, low alcohol foods |
| Acetic acid 3% (w/v) | B | Acidic foods (pH <4.5) |
| Ethanol 20% (v/v) | C | Alcoholic beverages <20% ABV |
| Ethanol 50% (v/v) | D1 | High-alcohol beverages, oil-in-water emulsions |
| Vegetable oil | D2 | Fatty foods |
| Poly(2,6-diphenyl-p-phenylene oxide) | E | Dry foods |

### Functional Barrier Concept
A functional barrier of plastic in a multi-layer material can prevent migration from non-list substances behind the barrier, provided migration stays below 0.01 mg/kg in food and the substance is not genotoxic/carcinogenic.

## Common Plastic Types and Food Safety Notes

| Plastic | Code | Common Uses | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Polyethylene terephthalate | PET (1) | Drinks bottles, food trays | Generally considered safe; not for high-temperature use |
| High-density polyethylene | HDPE (2) | Milk jugs, cutting boards | Good chemical resistance |
| PVC (polyvinyl chloride) | PVC (3) | Cling film (older), pipes | Plasticisers (phthalates) of concern; avoid for fatty foods |
| Low-density polyethylene | LDPE (4) | Bread bags, squeeze bottles | Suitable for cold/ambient foods |
| Polypropylene | PP (5) | Microwavable containers, lids | Good heat resistance up to ~120°C |
| Polystyrene | PS (6) | Yoghurt pots, foam trays | Not for hot or fatty foods (styrene migration risk) |
| Other (including PC, ABS) | Other (7) | Multi-use items | PC contains BPA — EU restricted to <0.05 mg/kg migration |

## Stainless Steel for Food Contact

No specific EU positive list exists for metals; general principles of 1935/2004 apply. UK guidance recommends:
- Grade **304 (18/8)** for most food applications
- Grade **316 (18/10/2)** for acidic or saline environments (marine, vinegar, ketchup)
- Surface finish: electropolished or smooth ground (≤0.8 µm Ra) to prevent bacterial harbouring
- Avoid carbon steel and untreated aluminium in contact with acidic foods

---

## Traceability Under 1935/2004

FBOs must have traceability systems enabling them to identify:
- Where FCMs and substances used to produce FCMs came from
- Who FCMs were supplied to (up to retail level)

Traceability must be maintained for all stages of manufacture, processing, and distribution. Records retained for minimum 5 years or, where shorter, product shelf life + 6 months.

---

## Practical Compliance Checklist for Food Businesses

1. **Identify all FCMs** in use (food-preparation equipment, packaging, storage containers, wrapping)
2. **Obtain DoCs** from all FCM suppliers — keep on file, request updates when formulations change
3. **Verify the glass-and-fork symbol** or "for food contact" wording on purchased materials
4. **Check temperature suitability** — match the FCM to intended use (e.g. microwave-safe PP only)
5. **Avoid reuse** of single-use FCMs not designed for reuse (e.g. single-use plastic containers)
6. **Inspect for damage** — cracked, scratched, or discoloured FCMs may increase migration risk; replace promptly
7. **Colour-coded equipment** — use colour-coded cutting boards and utensils to prevent cross-contamination and allow condition tracking
8. **Wooden utensils**: acceptable in low-risk applications; not recommended for raw meat or fish due to porosity and difficulty cleaning; must be smooth, crack-free, regularly replaced
9. **Include FCM compliance** in supplier approval procedures and purchasing specifications

---

## UK Retained Law Notes

Post-Brexit, UK 1935/2004 retained. EU 10/2011 retained for plastics. The UK is conducting its own review of FCM regulation; any new EU specific measures will not automatically apply in GB. FBOs exporting to EU must ensure products comply with current EU FCM rules.
