# REGULATION (EC) No 852/2004 — Hygiene of Foodstuffs

## Overview
Lays down general rules for food business operators (FBOs) on the hygiene of foodstuffs. Applies to all stages of production, processing and distribution. Primary production has additional requirements under Annex I.

## Article 5 — HACCP

FBOs must implement and maintain a permanent HACCP-based procedure covering the seven principles:

1. **Hazard identification** — biological, chemical, physical hazards at each step
2. **CCP identification** — steps where control is essential to prevent/eliminate/reduce hazards to acceptable levels
3. **Critical limits** — measurable boundaries (temperature, time, pH, aw) separating acceptable from unacceptable
4. **Monitoring procedures** — scheduled measurements at each CCP with defined frequency and method
5. **Corrective actions** — documented responses when a CCP is out of control
6. **Verification** — periodic review confirming HACCP is effective (audits, micro testing, calibration)
7. **Documentation** — records proportionate to business size and nature

HACCP must be reviewed on any modification to product, process or step.

Flexibility is permitted for small and/or traditional businesses: national guides to good practice may be used as an alternative to a full written HACCP plan.

## Annex II — General Hygiene Requirements

### Chapter I — General Requirements for Food Premises

- Premises must be kept clean and in good repair
- Layout must permit adequate cleaning/disinfection and prevent cross-contamination
- Adequate flush lavatories connected to drainage; toilets must not open directly into food rooms
- Sufficient washbasins with hot/cold water, soap, hygienic drying facilities
- Adequate natural or mechanical ventilation
- Adequate lighting (natural or artificial)
- Adequate drainage facilities
- Changing facilities for personnel where necessary

### Chapter II — Rooms Where Food is Prepared, Treated or Processed

- **Floors**: impervious, non-absorbent, washable, non-toxic; properly drained
- **Walls**: impervious, non-absorbent, washable, smooth up to appropriate height
- **Ceilings/overheads**: designed to prevent dirt accumulation, condensation, mould growth
- **Windows**: fitted with insect-proof screens (removable for cleaning) where openable to outside
- **Doors**: smooth, non-absorbent surfaces
- **Food-contact surfaces**: sound condition, easy to clean and disinfect
- **Cleaning facilities**: corrosion-resistant, easy to clean, supplied with hot and cold water
- **Food-washing sinks**: hot/cold potable water, kept clean and disinfected

### Chapter III — Movable/Temporary Premises

Sited, designed and maintained to avoid contamination, particularly from animals and pests.

### Chapter IV — Transport

- Conveyances/containers must be clean and in good repair
- Effective separation when transporting food alongside non-food items or different foodstuffs
- Bulk liquid/granulate/powder food must use dedicated containers
- Effective cleaning between loads where different products have been carried
- Adequate temperature maintenance and monitoring where required

### Chapter V — Equipment

- All food-contact articles must be effectively cleaned and disinfected where necessary
- Constructed and maintained to minimise contamination risk
- Non-returnable containers excepted from re-use requirements
- Chemical anti-corrosion additives used only in accordance with good practice

### Chapter VI — Food Waste

- Removed from food rooms as quickly as possible
- Deposited in closable containers
- Refuse stores designed to remain clean and free from animals and pests
- All waste eliminated hygienically and in accordance with applicable legislation

### Chapter VII — Water Supply

- Adequate supply of **potable water** required
- Clean seawater permitted for live bivalve molluscs, echinoderms, tunicates and marine gastropods
- Non-potable water (e.g. fire control, refrigeration) must circulate in separate, identified systems with no cross-connection or reflux into potable systems
- Recycled water must meet potable water standard or competent authority satisfaction
- Ice in contact with food: made from potable water, handled/stored hygienically
- Steam in direct contact with food: must not contain hazardous substances
- Post-heat-treatment cooling water must not contaminate product

### Chapter VIII — Personal Hygiene

- Food handlers must maintain high personal cleanliness; wear clean, suitable protective clothing
- No person suffering from, or carrying, a food-transmissible disease; infected wounds; skin infections; sores; or diarrhoea may handle food or enter food-handling areas
- Affected employees must immediately report illness/symptoms to the FBO

### Chapter IX — Provisions Applicable to Foodstuffs

- Raw materials must not be accepted if known/reasonably expected to be contaminated such that finished product would be unfit
- Raw materials and ingredients stored to prevent deterioration and contamination
- Food protected against contamination at all stages
- Adequate pest control procedures; domestic animals excluded from food areas
- Temperature control: foods that support pathogen growth must be kept at temperatures that prevent health risk — cold chain must not be broken (limited interruptions permitted for handling practicalities)
- Cooling: after heat treatment, cool as quickly as possible to safe temperature
- Thawing: undertaken to minimise pathogen growth; run-off liquid drained where it presents risk; thawed food handled to minimise further pathogen growth
- Hazardous/inedible substances (including animal feed) labelled and stored in separate, secure containers

### Chapter X — Wrapping and Packaging

- Wrapping/packaging materials must not be a source of contamination
- Stored to avoid contamination risk
- Packaging operations carried out so as to avoid product contamination
- Integrity of hermetically sealed containers (cans, glass jars) must be assured
- Re-used materials must be easy to clean and disinfect

### Chapter XI — Heat Treatment (Hermetically Sealed Containers)

- Every part of product must reach target temperature for required time
- Process must prevent post-treatment contamination
- Main parameters (temperature, pressure, sealing, microbiology) regularly checked; automatic devices used
- Process must conform to internationally recognised standard (pasteurisation, UHT, sterilisation)

### Chapter XII — Training

- Food handlers supervised, instructed and/or trained in food hygiene commensurate with their work
- Those developing/maintaining HACCP or operating guides must have adequate HACCP training
- Compliance with national training requirements for specific food sectors

## Enforcement and Penalties

EC 852/2004 is enforced by national competent authorities (e.g. local authority Environmental Health Officers in the UK and Ireland). Non-compliance can result in:

- Improvement notices
- Prohibition orders (closing premises or stopping processes)
- Prosecution and fines
- Voluntary/compelled recall

## Key Definitions

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| Food business | Any undertaking carrying out activities related to production, processing, storage, transport or distribution of food |
| Food business operator (FBO) | Natural or legal person responsible for ensuring food law compliance within the food business under their control |
| Primary production | Production, rearing, or growing of primary products including harvesting, milking and farmed animal production prior to slaughter |
| Hazard | A biological, chemical or physical agent in, or condition of, food with the potential to cause an adverse health effect |
| HACCP | Hazard Analysis and Critical Control Points — systematic preventive approach to food safety |
