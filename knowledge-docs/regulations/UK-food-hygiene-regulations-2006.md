# UK Food Hygiene Regulations 2006 (and Post-Brexit Retained Law)

## Overview
The Food Hygiene (England) Regulations 2006 (and equivalent SI for Wales, Scotland, and Northern Ireland) implement EC 852/2004, EC 853/2004, and EC 854/2004 into UK domestic law. Post-Brexit, these regulations continue to apply with the EU regulations retained as assimilated domestic law. They set out enforcement powers, penalties, and the registration/approval framework.

---

## Registration vs Approval

| Requirement | Who | How |
|---|---|---|
| **Registration** | All food businesses (before operations commence) | Free; notify local authority Environmental Health department; online via FSA Food Business Operator portal |
| **Approval** | Businesses handling products of animal origin (slaughterhouses, cutting plants, dairy processing, fish processing, egg processing, coldstores for POAO) | Application to competent authority; official veterinarian/inspector assessment; premises receive approval number |

**Failure to register** is a criminal offence.

**Change of ownership** — new owner must re-register. Change of food type/activity requires updated registration.

---

## Enforcement Authority

| Body | Remit |
|---|---|
| **Local Authority Environmental Health Officers (EHOs)** | Premises inspection, registration, enforcement of EC 852/2004 general hygiene requirements, FHRS scoring |
| **Food Standards Agency (FSA)** | Official controls in approved establishments (slaughterhouses, cutting plants); national policy |
| **Food Standards Scotland (FSS)** | Scotland equivalent |
| **DAERA** | Northern Ireland |
| **Animal and Plant Health Agency (APHA)** | Import/export controls for animal products |
| **Local Authority Trading Standards** | Labelling, composition, weight, product standards |

---

## Inspection Powers

EHOs have the right to:
- Enter food premises at any reasonable time (without prior notice)
- Inspect food, records, processes, and equipment
- Take samples and photographs
- Require food to be held pending examination
- Serve improvement notices, prohibition notices, or emergency prohibition notices
- Apply for an Emergency Prohibition Order (EPO) from a magistrates' court

---

## Enforcement Notices

### Improvement Notice
- Served when EHO is satisfied a legal requirement is being contravened
- Sets out the nature of the contravention, the measures required to remedy it, and a time limit (minimum 14 days)
- Failure to comply: criminal offence

### Prohibition Notice / Prohibition Order
- Used when an activity, process, or use of equipment or premises poses an imminent risk of injury to health
- Can prohibit use of specific equipment, process, or the premises themselves
- Operator must apply to have the notice lifted once remedied

### Emergency Prohibition Notice (EPN)
- Served by EHO immediately where an imminent risk of injury to health exists
- Supported within 3 days by application to magistrates for an Emergency Prohibition Order (EPO)
- EPO can close premises or stop operations immediately

---

## Penalties

| Offence | Penalty |
|---|---|
| Failing to register / operating unapproved premises | Unlimited fine; up to 2 years' imprisonment |
| Contravening food safety requirements | Unlimited fine; up to 2 years' imprisonment |
| Failing to comply with improvement notice | Unlimited fine; up to 2 years' imprisonment |
| Placing unsafe food on the market | Unlimited fine; up to 2 years' imprisonment |
| Obstruction of enforcement officer | Up to 3 months' imprisonment; level 5 fine |

## Food Hygiene Rating Scheme (FHRS)

Operated by the FSA in partnership with local authorities in England, Wales, and Northern Ireland.

### How It Works
- EHO inspects food business against food hygiene standards
- Score awarded from 0–5:

| Rating | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **5** | Hygiene standards are very good |
| **4** | Hygiene standards are good |
| **3** | Hygiene standards are generally satisfactory |
| **2** | Some improvement necessary |
| **1** | Major improvement necessary |
| **0** | Urgent improvement required |

- Three elements assessed:
  1. **Hygienic food handling** (food preparation, cooking, cooling, storage)
  2. **Cleanliness and condition of facilities and building** (structure, layout, lighting, ventilation)
  3. **Management of food safety** (HACCP, training records, procedures)

- Scores published on the FHRS website (ratings.food.gov.uk)
- **Display is mandatory** in Wales and Northern Ireland; voluntary in England
- Scotland operates the **Food Hygiene Information Scheme (FHIS)** with Pass/Improvement Required ratings

### Requesting a Re-inspection
- Businesses can request a re-inspection (fee may apply) after remedying issues
- Right of appeal against rating

## Guides to Good Practice (GGPs)

National industry guides to good practice can be used to help FBOs comply with EC 852/2004 requirements. Guides developed by trade associations are vetted by the FSA. Following a recognised GGP is prima facie evidence of compliance.

Examples:
- Catering Guide (Highfield/CIEH)
- British Retail Consortium (BRC) Technical Standards
- Chilled Food Association guidelines
- SALSA (Safe and Local Supplier Approval)

---

## Guides to Flexible HACCP

The FSA publishes "Safer Food Better Business" (SFBB) packs for small businesses — a simpler alternative to a full written HACCP plan, applicable under the flexibility provisions of EC 852/2004.

Available for:
- Catering
- Retailers
- Childminders
- Indian/Chinese/Thai/etc. catering variants

SFBB packs cover: hazards, safe methods, safe management, and a diary for checks.

## COVID-19 and Post-Brexit Changes

Post-Brexit divergence points:
- UK no longer participates in EU RASFF — uses FSA/FSS food/feed alert system
- Health marks on POAO use "UK" country code for GB establishments
- Northern Ireland retains EU market access under the Windsor Framework; NI businesses can use EU or UK health marks
- Imports of POAO from EU to GB now require health certificates and border checks at Border Control Posts (BCPs)
- UK Global Tariff applies to food imports from non-UK countries

---

## Scotland — Food (Scotland) Act 2015

Provides additional powers in Scotland including:
- **Improvement Requirement** — lower threshold than improvement notice; for minor non-compliance
- **Compliance Notice** — for continued non-compliance after improvement requirement
- Extended powers for FSS

Scotland also has the **Food (Scotland) Act 2015** powers for labelling, composition, and food crime investigation.
