# Commission Implementing Regulation (EU) 2026/194 - High-Risk Import Controls

## Overview
Commission Implementing Regulation (EU) 2026/194 of 28 January 2026 further amends Implementing Regulation (EU) 2019/1793 on temporary increases of official controls and emergency measures governing entry into the Union of certain goods from certain third countries.

- Jurisdiction: EU
- Source type: regulation
- Published: 29 January 2026
- CELEX: 32026R0194
- Official source: https://eur-lex.europa.eu/eli/reg_impl/2026/194/oj

## Why it matters
- This is a live 2026 update to the EU import-control list for higher-risk foods and feeds.
- It matters for businesses importing affected categories into the EU or Northern Ireland.

## Practical retrieval points
- Use this source when the user asks what changed in 2026 for official controls on imported food.
- It should be read as another amendment layer on top of 2019/1793, not as a standalone replacement code.
- Questions on border checks, increased control frequency, and emergency conditions should be answered against the latest amended position.

## Compliance implications
- Importers need to confirm the current annex listing for product and origin combinations, not rely on older lists.
- HACCP or supplier-approval procedures for imported raw materials may need updating where heightened controls apply.
- NI-facing businesses should remember that EU rules remain relevant there for applicable movements.
