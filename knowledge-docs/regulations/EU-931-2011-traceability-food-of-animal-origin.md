# Commission Implementing Regulation (EU) No 931/2011 — Traceability for Food of Animal Origin

## Overview
Commission Implementing Regulation (EU) No 931/2011 adds specific traceability requirements for food of animal origin under the broader traceability framework in Article 18 of Regulation (EC) No 178/2002.

- **CELEX:** 32011R0931
- **Official source:** https://eur-lex.europa.eu/legal-content/EN/TXT/?uri=CELEX%3A32011R0931
- **OJ:** L 242, 20.9.2011, pp. 2-3
- **Application date:** 1 July 2012

---

## Why It Matters
General food law already requires one-step-back and one-step-forward traceability. This regulation makes the expectation more specific for food of animal origin, which is often higher risk and more difficult to trace quickly during incidents.

## Scope
The regulation applies to food defined as unprocessed and processed products under the hygiene framework for food of animal origin.

It is especially relevant where operators need to trace consignments quickly through the chain.

It does **not** serve as a general traceability regulation for every type of food; that broader role remains with Article 18 of EC 178/2002.

---

## Specific Traceability Requirements
Food business operators handling food of animal origin should have systems that can make the following information available:

- an accurate description of the food
- the volume or quantity
- the name and address of the food business operator from whom the food was dispatched
- the name and address of the consignee to whom the food was dispatched
- a reference identifying the lot, batch, or consignment, as appropriate
- the date of dispatch

The information must be available to the competent authority on request and retrievable without undue delay.

---

## Operational Implications

### 1. More Granular Records
For food of animal origin, vague “supplier/customer” records are not enough if they do not link the actual consignment, lot, or batch.

### 2. Faster Incident Response
The regulation is designed to support quicker withdrawals and recalls by making consignments identifiable in a more precise way.

### 3. Format Flexibility
The operator may choose the practical format of the records, but the information must be clear, retrievable, and available promptly when asked for by the authority.

---

## Interaction with Other Food-Safety Laws

- **EC 178/2002, Article 18:** base one-step-back / one-step-forward traceability rule
- **EC 852/2004 and EC 853/2004:** hygiene and sector-specific animal-origin requirements
- **Recall / withdrawal procedures:** this regulation supports the evidence trail needed for effective product isolation and recall scope decisions
