# Food Safety and Hygiene (England) Regulations 2013

## Overview
The Food Safety and Hygiene (England) Regulations 2013 are a key domestic enforcement instrument for food hygiene and safety rules in England. They sit alongside retained or assimilated food-hygiene law and provide enforcement mechanics, offences, penalties, powers, and procedural detail.

- **Official source:** https://www.legislation.gov.uk/uksi/2013/2996/contents/made
- **PDF:** https://www.legislation.gov.uk/uksi/2013/2996/pdfs/uksi_20132996_en.pdf

---

## Why It Matters
This regulation matters for:
- how hygiene rules are enforced in England
- offences and penalties
- powers of authorised officers
- detention, seizure, notices, and prosecutions
- the domestic legal layer beneath food-hygiene inspections

For PinkPepper, this is a more precise England-level enforcement source than treating EU hygiene law alone as the full answer.

---

## Core Practical Themes

### 1. Enforcement of Hygiene Law
The regulation provides the domestic machinery for enforcing food hygiene and related safety rules.

### 2. Officer Powers and Notices
Authorised officers may inspect, require action, and use formal notice routes where non-compliance is identified.

### 3. Offences and Penalties
Failure to comply with relevant hygiene obligations can lead to criminal enforcement outcomes.

### 4. Court and Procedure Interface
The regulation supports the procedural framework for prohibition, detention, appeals, and related enforcement actions.
