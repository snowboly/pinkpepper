# REGULATION (EC) No 2073/2005 — Microbiological Criteria for Foodstuffs

## Overview
Establishes microbiological criteria for certain foodstuffs and rules to be followed by FBOs when implementing general and specific hygiene measures. Two categories of criteria:

1. **Food safety criteria** — define acceptability of a product placed on the market
2. **Process hygiene criteria** — indicate acceptable functioning of the production process; non-binding limits that trigger corrective actions rather than product rejection

UK has retained this as UK Regulation 2073/2005.

---

## Food Safety Criteria

These apply throughout the shelf life of the product. If a food safety criterion is not met, the product must be withdrawn or recalled.

### Key Food Safety Criteria

#### Listeria monocytogenes

| Food | Limit | Stage | Analytical Reference Method |
|---|---|---|---|
| Ready-to-eat (RTE) foods for vulnerable populations (hospitals, care homes) | **Absent in 25 g** (n=5, c=0) | Throughout shelf life | EN/ISO 11290-1 |
| RTE foods that support L. mono growth, shelf life >5 days | **100 cfu/g** (n=5, c=0) | Throughout shelf life | EN/ISO 11290-2 |
| RTE foods that do not support L. mono growth (pH ≤4.4 or aw ≤0.92 or pH ≤5.0 and aw ≤0.94), or shelf life ≤5 days | **100 cfu/g** at end of shelf life | End of shelf life | EN/ISO 11290-2 |

**Vulnerable populations**: immunocompromised individuals, pregnant women (Listeria can cause listeriosis, foetal harm, miscarriage), elderly, infants.

#### Salmonella

| Food | Limit | Stage |
|---|---|---|
| Minced meat and meat preparations for raw consumption (beef tartare) | Absent in 25 g (n=5, c=0) | At point of manufacture |
| Minced meat, meat preparations, and MSM for cooking | Absent in 10 g (n=5, c=0) | At point of manufacture |
| Poultry meat (broilers, turkeys) | Absent in 25 g (n=5, c=0) | At point of manufacture |
| Poultry meat products for raw consumption | Absent in 25 g (n=5, c=0) | At point of manufacture |
| RTE foods except for infants and vulnerable populations | Absent in 25 g (n=5, c=0) | Throughout shelf life |
| RTE foods for infants and for special medical purposes | Absent in 25 g (n=10, c=0) | Throughout shelf life |
| Cheeses, butter, cream from raw/heat-treated milk | Absent in 25 g (n=5, c=0) | Throughout shelf life |
| Dried infant formulae and dietary foods for infants | Absent in 25 g (n=30, c=0) | Throughout shelf life |
| Sprouts (RTE) | Absent in 25 g (n=5, c=0) | Throughout shelf life |

#### E. coli (Verotoxigenic / STEC)

| Food | Limit |
|---|---|
| Sprouts (RTE) | Absent in 25 g for VTEC O157, O26, O111, O103, O145, O104:H4 |
| Unpasteurised fruit and vegetable juices (RTE) | Absent in 25 g for VTEC O157 |

#### Enterobacter sakazakii (Cronobacter spp.)

| Food | Limit |
|---|---|
| Dried infant formula (0-6 months) | Absent in 10 g (n=30, c=0) |
| Dried dietary foods for special medical purposes (infants) | Absent in 10 g (n=30, c=0) |

## Process Hygiene Criteria

These apply during and at end of the production process. Exceeding a process hygiene criterion triggers investigation of hygiene controls and corrective action — it does **not** automatically mean the product must be recalled.

### Key Process Hygiene Criteria

#### Aerobic Colony Count (TVC/ACC)

| Food | Limit | Stage |
|---|---|---|
| Fresh poultry carcasses | m=3.5 log cfu/cm², M=5 log cfu/cm² | After chilling |
| Fresh red meat carcasses (cattle, sheep, goat, horse) | m=3.5 log cfu/cm², M=5 log cfu/cm² | After chilling |
| Fresh pig carcasses | m=4 log cfu/cm², M=5 log cfu/cm² | After chilling |
| Minced meat | m=5×10⁵ cfu/g (beef), m=5×10⁶ cfu/g (other species) | End of manufacture |
| Pasteurised milk | m=5×10⁴ cfu/ml | At end of heat treatment process |

#### E. coli (Hygiene Indicator on Carcasses)

| Food | Limit | Stage |
|---|---|---|
| Fresh beef, veal, pork, sheep, goat, horse carcasses | m=0 cfu/cm², M=1 cfu/cm² (5-point sampling) | After chilling |
| Fresh poultry carcasses | m=0 cfu/cm², M=1 cfu/cm² | After chilling |

Where **m** = acceptable level, **M** = limit value (unsatisfactory if exceeded).

#### Enterobacteriaceae (Cheese Process Hygiene)

| Food | Limit | Stage |
|---|---|---|
| Soft and semi-soft cheese from pasteurised milk | m=10 cfu/g, M=100 cfu/g | When the manufacturing process ends |
| Enzyme-rennet cheese from raw/thermised milk | m=100 cfu/g, M=1000 cfu/g | When the manufacturing process ends |

#### Staphylococcal Enterotoxins

| Food | Limit | Stage |
|---|---|---|
| Cheese, milk powder, whey powder | Not detectable in 25 g (n=5, c=0) | When the manufacturing process ends / throughout shelf life |

## Sampling Plans

The notation used: **n/c/m/M**

| Symbol | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **n** | Number of sample units in the lot |
| **c** | Number of sample units giving values between m and M |
| **m** | Microbiological limit — satisfactory if all units ≤m |
| **M** | Upper limit — result is unsatisfactory if any unit exceeds M |

Example: n=5, c=2, m=100 cfu/g, M=1000 cfu/g — acceptable if ≤2 of the 5 samples are between 100 and 1000 cfu/g, and none exceed 1000 cfu/g.

## FBO Obligations

1. **Sample testing** — FBOs must test their products in accordance with the regulation's criteria
2. **Frequency** — Based on HACCP, type of food, likely microbiological risks, history of results
3. **Corrective actions** — If a criterion is exceeded:
   - Investigate cause
   - Review HACCP and prerequisite programmes
   - Take action to prevent recurrence
   - For food safety criteria: withdraw/recall as appropriate
4. **Records** — Retain test results and corrective actions records; available to competent authority
5. **Trend analysis** — Use results to identify trends before limits are breached

## Pathogens of Key Concern

| Pathogen | Key Foods | Risk Level | Infective Dose |
|---|---|---|---|
| Listeria monocytogenes | RTE, smoked fish, soft cheese, deli meats | High (vulnerable groups) | Low (100–1000 cells) |
| Salmonella spp. | Poultry, eggs, raw meat, sprouts | High | Medium (10³–10⁶ cells) |
| E. coli O157:H7 (STEC) | Beef, raw milk, leafy greens, sprouts | Very high (HUS risk) | Very low (<100 cells) |
| Campylobacter | Poultry (not covered by 2073 food safety criteria — process hygiene only) | Very high (most common bacterial FBO in EU) | Very low |
| Clostridium botulinum | Canned foods, MAP products, smoked fish | Critical | Toxin-based |
| Bacillus cereus | Cooked rice, cereals, starchy foods | Medium | Toxin-based |
| Staphylococcus aureus | High-protein RTE foods, dairy, confectionery | Medium | Toxin-based (>10⁵ cells) |
| Norovirus | Shellfish, salads, RTE foods (not covered by 2073) | High | Very low (18 particles) |

## UK Retained Law

UK Regulation 2073/2005 retained with no substantive changes. Any future updates to EU microbiological criteria will not automatically apply in GB; the FSA will issue equivalent UK guidance.
