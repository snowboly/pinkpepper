# UK Food Safety Act 1990

## Overview
The Food Safety Act 1990 is one of the main UK domestic legal foundations for food safety enforcement. It creates core offences around unsafe food, unfit food, falsely described food, and misleading presentation, and it provides an important due-diligence defence framework.

- **Official source hub:** https://www.food.gov.uk/about-us/key-regulations
- **Legislation.gov.uk source:** https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/1990/16/contents

## Core Practical Themes

### 1. Unsafe or Unfit Food
Food businesses must not render food injurious to health, sell food that is not of the nature or quality demanded, or place clearly unfit food into sale channels.

### 2. False or Misleading Description
Food must not be falsely described, presented, or labelled in a misleading way.

### 3. Due Diligence Defence
The Act supports an important due-diligence concept. A business may defend itself more effectively where it can show robust controls, training, supplier checks, and documented systems.

### 4. Enforcement Framework
The Act supports inspection, seizure, detention, notices, and prosecution routes used by authorities.
