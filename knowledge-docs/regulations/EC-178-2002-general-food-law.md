# REGULATION (EC) No 178/2002 — General Food Law

## Overview
Establishes the general principles and requirements of food law, creates the European Food Safety Authority (EFSA), and lays down procedures in food safety matters. The foundational regulation underpinning all EU food law. Retained in UK law post-Brexit with EFSA functions transferred to UK bodies.

---

## Key Principles

### Risk Analysis Framework (Articles 6–9)
Food law must be based on risk analysis comprising three components:

1. **Risk Assessment** — Scientific, independent evaluation of hazards; carried out by EFSA (EU) or FSA/FSS scientific committees (UK)
2. **Risk Management** — Policy decisions, proportionate measures; carried out by European Commission / UK government
3. **Risk Communication** — Interactive exchange of information between risk assessors, managers, consumers, and other stakeholders

### Precautionary Principle (Article 7)
Where scientific uncertainty exists and there are reasonable grounds to suspect that a food poses a risk to health, provisional risk management measures may be adopted **without waiting for full scientific evidence** to complete risk assessment. These measures must be:
- Proportionate
- No more trade-restrictive than necessary
- Based on review within a reasonable time
- Subject to full risk assessment at the earliest opportunity

---

## Article 14 — Food Safety Requirements

Food must not be placed on the market if it is:
- **Injurious to health** (immediate, short-term, or long-term effects on the consumer or their descendants; cumulative toxic effects; sensitivities of specific categories of consumer)
- **Unfit for human consumption** (contamination by extraneous matter; putrefaction; deterioration; or otherwise unacceptable)

If any food within a batch/lot is unsafe, the entire batch/lot is presumed unsafe unless evidence shows the rest is safe.

---

## Article 17 — Responsibilities

- **FBOs** are primarily responsible for ensuring food law compliance within the business under their control
- **Member States** shall enforce food law and monitor/verify compliance by FBOs
- **European Commission** maintains an overall monitoring role for harmonised application

---

## Article 18 — Traceability

**"One step back, one step forward"** principle:

FBOs must be able to identify:
1. Any person who has **supplied** the food, feed, food-producing animal, or substance incorporated into that food ("one step back")
2. Any **establishment** to which they have supplied their products ("one step forward")

### Practical Requirements
- Traceability records must be available to competent authorities on demand
- Must cover: product name, supplier, date of receipt, quantity, lot/batch number (where applicable)
- No specific retention period specified in 178/2002, but:
  - UK best practice: minimum 3 years for most products
  - Products with short shelf life: at least 6 months beyond shelf life
  - High-risk products (meat, dairy): often 5+ years

### Lot/Batch Identification
Traceability is supported by lot marking under Directive 2011/91/EU (retained in UK). The lot mark ("L" code or best-before/use-by date equivalent) links product to production records.

---

## Article 19 — Obligations in Respect of Food: FBOs

If an FBO considers that food they have imported, produced, processed, manufactured, or distributed:
- Does not comply with food safety requirements, **or**
- May be injurious to health

They **must**:
1. Immediately initiate procedures to **withdraw** the food from the market
2. **Inform** the competent authority
3. If the food may have reached consumers, effectively and accurately **inform consumers** (consider a public recall)
4. **Collaborate** with the competent authority on action taken

### Withdrawal vs Recall

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| **Withdrawal** | Removing product from the supply chain (before it reaches consumers) |
| **Recall** | Retrieving product already supplied to/purchased by consumers |

A recall requires consumer communication (e.g. point-of-sale notice, media alert, social media, FSA/FSS food alert).

---

## Article 20 — Obligations in Respect of Feed

Equivalent obligations apply to feed business operators where feed does not comply with feed safety requirements.

---

## RASFF — Rapid Alert System for Food and Feed

The EU operates a rapid alert network for notifying serious direct or indirect risks from food or feed:
- Member State competent authorities must notify RASFF of any measure taken to restrict marketing or recall a product
- UK equivalent post-Brexit: **Food and Feed Safety Alerts** system (managed by FSA/FSS/DAERA)

---

## Key Definitions

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| **Food** | Any substance or product (processed, partially processed, or unprocessed) intended to be, or expected to be ingested by humans. Excludes feed, live animals, plants prior to harvesting, medicinal products, cosmetics, tobacco, narcotics, residues/contaminants |
| **Feed** | Any substance or product including additives for oral feeding to animals |
| **Food law** | Laws, regulations and administrative provisions governing food generally; covers production, processing and distribution of feed for food-producing animals |
| **Food business** | Any undertaking whether for profit or not carrying out any activities related to the production, processing, storage, transport or distribution of food |
| **Placing on the market** | The holding of food or feed for the purpose of sale; includes offering for sale, supply without charge, etc. |
| **Traceability** | The ability to trace and follow a food, feed, food-producing animal or substance through all stages of production, processing and distribution |
| **Risk** | A function of the probability of an adverse health effect and the severity of that effect, consequential to a hazard |
| **Hazard** | A biological, chemical or physical agent in, or condition of, food or feed with the potential to cause an adverse health effect |

---

## UK Retained Law

After Brexit, GB retained 178/2002 with EFSA functions transferred to:
- **Food Standards Agency (FSA)** — England, Wales, Northern Ireland
- **Food Standards Scotland (FSS)** — Scotland

RASFF participation ceased; FSA operates the UK food/feed alert system independently.
