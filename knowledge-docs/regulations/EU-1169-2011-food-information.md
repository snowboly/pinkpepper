# REGULATION (EU) No 1169/2011 — Food Information to Consumers (FIC)

## Overview
Establishes the general principles, requirements and responsibilities governing food information provided to consumers. Covers both pre-packed and non-pre-packed foods sold in the EU/UK (retained in UK law post-Brexit as the Food Information Regulations 2014).

---

## Article 9 — Mandatory Particulars for Pre-Packed Food

The following must appear on labelling:

1. Name of the food
2. List of ingredients (in descending order of weight)
3. **Allergens** (emphasis required — see below)
4. Quantity of certain ingredients (QUID — Quantitative Ingredient Declaration)
5. Net quantity
6. Date of minimum durability ("best before") or use-by date
7. Special storage conditions and/or conditions of use
8. Name and address of FBO
9. Country of origin or place of provenance (where required)
10. Instructions for use (where omission would make appropriate use difficult)
11. Alcoholic strength (for beverages >1.2% ABV)
12. Nutrition declaration

---

## Article 21 — Allergen Labelling

Allergens must be:
- Listed in the ingredients list **and** emphasised by typeset (bold, italic, underlined, contrasting background colour)
- If no ingredients list: "Contains [allergen name]" statement required

### Annex II — The 14 Major Allergens

| # | Allergen | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Cereals containing gluten** | Wheat (incl. spelt, khorasan), rye, barley, oats and hybrids |
| 2 | **Crustaceans** | Crab, lobster, prawns, shrimp, scampi, crayfish |
| 3 | **Eggs** | All bird eggs; includes egg white, yolk, lecithin (E322), albumin |
| 4 | **Fish** | Exceptions: gelatine used as vitamin carrier or fining agent |
| 5 | **Peanuts** | Legume, not tree nut; high cross-contamination risk with nuts |
| 6 | **Soybeans** | Exceptions: fully refined soybean oil, certain tocopherols |
| 7 | **Milk** | Includes butter, cheese, cream, yoghurt, casein, whey, lactose |
| 8 | **Nuts** | Almonds, hazelnuts, walnuts, cashews, pecans, Brazil nuts, pistachios, macadamia |
| 9 | **Celery** | Includes celeriac, celery salt, celery seeds |
| 10 | **Mustard** | Includes seeds, powder, oil, prepared mustard |
| 11 | **Sesame seeds** | Includes sesame oil, tahini, halva |
| 12 | **Sulphur dioxide and sulphites** | Only if >10 mg/kg or 10 mg/litre total SO₂; E220–E228 |
| 13 | **Lupin** | Lupin flour/seeds; found in bread, pastries, pasta |
| 14 | **Molluscs** | Mussels, clams, oysters, scallops, squid, octopus, snails |

## Non-Pre-Packed Food (Loose/Open Food)

Includes food sold loose, food pre-packed for direct sale, and food served in catering.

**Requirements:**
- Allergen information must be provided to the consumer on request
- Must be indicated in writing (on a menu, chalkboard, label, or written notice to staff)
- Oral provision is permitted but must be backed by written documentation verifiable at point of sale
- Staff must be able to direct customers to the allergen information

**Natasha's Law (UK — effective October 2021):**
All food pre-packed for direct sale (PPDS) must carry a full ingredients list with allergens emphasised, and the name of the food — even if packed on-site and sold from the same premises.

---

## QUID (Quantitative Ingredient Declaration)

A declared ingredient or category of ingredient must show its percentage if it:
- Appears in the name of the food (e.g. "chicken pie" — % chicken must be shown)
- Is normally associated with that food name by consumers
- Is emphasised on labelling (text or image)

Percentage calculated at time of incorporation into recipe.

---

## Nutrition Declaration

Must include (per 100 g or 100 ml):

| Nutrient | Unit |
|---|---|
| Energy | kJ and kcal |
| Fat | g |
| of which saturates | g |
| Carbohydrate | g |
| of which sugars | g |
| Protein | g |
| Salt | g |

Optional additions: mono-unsaturates, polyunsaturates, polyols, starch, fibre, vitamins and minerals.

Can also be expressed per portion if portion size is clearly stated.

---

## Distance Selling (Online/Telephone Orders)

- Mandatory food information must be available **before** purchase is completed
- Must be provided on delivery at minimum
- All mandatory information (including allergens) must be accessible on the website/catalogue

---

## Date Marking

| Type | When Used | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| **"Best before"** | Foods where quality rather than safety deteriorates | Safe to eat after date but quality may decline |
| **"Use by"** | Foods that are microbiologically perishable | Unsafe to consume after date; FBO must not sell past this date |
| **"Display until"** / **"Sell by"** | Retailer stock rotation only; not a legal requirement | Not required by law; for retailer use only |

---

## Precautionary Allergen Labelling (PAL)

"May contain [allergen]" statements are **not regulated by FIC** but are a voluntary business decision. They should only be used when:
- A genuine risk assessment has identified an uncontrollable cross-contact risk
- All reasonable steps to prevent cross-contamination have been taken
- Used as a last resort, not a substitute for proper allergen management

Overuse of PAL restricts consumer choice for allergic individuals and is discouraged by enforcement authorities.

---

## UK Post-Brexit Differences

Since 1 January 2021, GB (England, Scotland, Wales) operates under the UK FIC (retained EU law). Key differences:
- **Natasha's Law** introduced PPDS labelling requirements (October 2021) — EU has proposed similar but not yet equivalent legislation
- UK future allergen review may add sesame as a 15th allergen (under consultation)
- Nutrition reference values remain aligned with EU for now

---

## Enforcement

Local Authority Trading Standards Officers enforce labelling law. Penalties include:
- Improvement notices
- Recall/withdrawal requirements
- Prosecution (unlimited fines in UK)
- Reputational damage; coroner's courts in fatality cases
