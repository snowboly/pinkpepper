# Commission Regulation (EU) 2024/2895 — Microbiological Criteria: Listeria monocytogenes

## Overview
Commission Regulation (EU) 2024/2895 of 20 November 2024 amends Regulation (EC) No 2073/2005 on microbiological criteria for foodstuffs. It introduces stricter Listeria monocytogenes criteria for ready-to-eat (RTE) foods, expands the categories of foods subject to testing, and aligns the EU framework with updated EFSA risk assessments on listeriosis.

- **CELEX:** 32024R2895
- **Amends:** Regulation (EC) No 2073/2005
- **In force:** From 1 July 2025 (phased implementation)
- **Official source:** https://eur-lex.europa.eu/eli/reg/2024/2895/oj

---

## Why It Matters
Listeriosis causes approximately 2,500 confirmed cases and 250 deaths per year in the EU. Unlike most foodborne pathogens, Listeria monocytogenes can grow at refrigeration temperatures (0–4°C), making it a persistent hazard in chilled RTE products. This regulation responds to evidence that existing limits were insufficient for certain high-risk product categories and vulnerable consumer groups (elderly, pregnant women, immunocompromised individuals).

---

## Key Changes from EC 2073/2005

### Stricter Food Safety Criteria (Category: Listeria in RTE)

**Existing criterion before amendment:**
- Absence (0 CFU/g) throughout shelf life for RTE foods **that support Listeria growth** — tested at point of placing on market
- 100 CFU/g limit for RTE foods **not supporting Listeria growth** — tested throughout shelf life

**Post-2024/2895 changes:**
1. **10 CFU/g limit** replaces 100 CFU/g for high-risk RTE categories that do not support growth but are consumed by vulnerable populations — including certain dairy products (soft/semi-soft cheeses), smoked/marinated fish products, and deli-style processed meats
2. **Expanded food categories** brought under the absence criterion (cannot contain detectable Listeria throughout shelf life):
   - Heat-treated RTE products in hermetically sealed packaging
   - Sprouted seeds intended for ready-to-eat consumption
   - Pre-cut fresh salads and mixed vegetable products
3. **New sampling requirements** for manufacturers: minimum 5-sample plans at critical control points for listed RTE categories with extended shelf life (>5 days at ≤6°C)

### Process Hygiene Criteria
- Extended to cold-smoked fish and fishery products (previously covered only heat-treated fish)
- New criterion for fermented sausages (pH-reduction alone insufficient; applies during fermentation and post-processing)

## Foods Most Affected

| Food Category | Previous Limit | New Limit | Applies From |
|---|---|---|---|
| Soft/semi-soft ripened cheeses (mould-ripened) | 100 CFU/g | 10 CFU/g | 1 July 2025 |
| Cold-smoked fish (salmon, trout, etc.) | 100 CFU/g | 10 CFU/g | 1 July 2025 |
| Sliced/portioned deli meats (≥5-day shelf life) | 100 CFU/g | 10 CFU/g | 1 July 2025 |
| Pre-cut fresh salads, sprouted seeds | 100 CFU/g | Absence (0/25g) | 1 July 2025 |
| Heat-treated RTE in sealed packs | 100 CFU/g | Absence (0/25g) | 1 July 2025 |

---

## Operator Obligations

### For Food Business Operators (FBOs)
1. **Review and update HACCP plans** to incorporate the tighter criteria as critical limits
2. **Validate shelf life** for any RTE product now subject to the 10 CFU/g limit — challenge testing or modelling may be required to demonstrate the limit is not exceeded at end of shelf life
3. **Increase sampling frequency** for high-risk RTE categories, particularly products with >5-day shelf life distributed chilled
4. **Review cold chain management**: temperature excursions that previously appeared acceptable may now lead to non-compliance
5. **Environmental monitoring programmes (EMP)** should be updated to increase L. monocytogenes swabbing in processing environments

### For Importers
Products imported into the EU from third countries must comply with these criteria from 1 July 2025. Border inspection posts will apply the 10 CFU/g limit when sampling affected categories.

---

## Sampling Plans
The regulation maintains the n=5, c=0 (for absence) and n=5, c=2 (for presence with limit) plans from EC 2073/2005 but extends them to new categories. The analytical reference method remains ISO 11290-1 (detection) and ISO 11290-2 (enumeration).

---

## HACCP Implications
- For products now under the absence criterion, HACCP must demonstrate **zero tolerance** throughout shelf life — a more demanding validation requirement than a numerical limit
- Corrective action procedures must be updated to reflect the lower trigger thresholds
- Supplier verification for raw materials is more critical, particularly for cold-smoked fish and dairy ingredients

---

## Interaction with Other Regulations
- **EC 2073/2005** (as amended): base regulation — must be read together
- **EC 852/2004**: general HACCP requirements, including EMP
- **EU 2023/915**: separate regulation for chemical contaminants — does not affect microbiological criteria
- **EU 2017/625**: official control inspections will use these updated criteria from July 2025

## Practical Retrieval Points
- Use for questions about Listeria limits in RTE foods, cold-smoked salmon, soft cheese, deli meats, pre-cut vegetables
- Use for questions about shelf-life validation for chilled RTE products
- Use for questions about HACCP critical limits for Listeria after July 2025
- The 10 CFU/g vs 100 CFU/g distinction is the most common compliance question
- This regulation does NOT apply to raw/unprocessed foods or products to be cooked before eating
