# REGULATION (EC) No 853/2004 — Specific Hygiene Rules for Food of Animal Origin

## Overview
Lays down specific hygiene rules for FBOs handling food of animal origin. Applies alongside EC 852/2004. Requires approval (not just registration) for most establishments handling products of animal origin (POAO). UK has retained this as UK Regulation 853/2004.

---

## Scope

Applies to unprocessed and processed products of animal origin including:
- Meat (red meat, poultry, game)
- Minced meat, meat preparations, and meat products
- Live bivalve molluscs
- Fishery products
- Raw milk and dairy products
- Eggs and egg products
- Frogs' legs and snails
- Rendered animal fats and greaves
- Treated stomachs, bladders and intestines
- Gelatine and collagen

**Retail operations** are generally exempt except where supplying other establishments.

---

## Establishment Approval

- FBOs must only operate from establishments approved by the competent authority
- Each establishment receives an approval number
- Registered establishments (lower risk) do not require approval
- Approval is product/species specific

---

## Health Mark and Identification Mark

| Mark | Used On | When Applied |
|---|---|---|
| **Health mark** (oval with country code + establishment number) | Fresh meat from approved slaughterhouses | Applied by official veterinarian |
| **Identification mark** (oval) | Processed POAO, dairy, eggs etc. | Applied by FBO after production |

---

## Meat — Slaughter Hygiene

- Ante-mortem inspection required before slaughter
- Post-mortem inspection required for every carcass
- Official Veterinarian (OV) or official auxiliary must be present during slaughter
- Carcasses must be free from faecal contamination; if present, must be trimmed or washed
- Slaughter line must be designed to prevent cross-contamination

### Temperature Requirements — Meat

| Product | Maximum Temperature |
|---|---|
| Fresh meat (red) | ≤7°C (carcasses) or ≤3°C (offal) within specified time post-slaughter |
| Fresh poultry | ≤4°C within specified time |
| Minced meat / meat preparations | ≤2°C (fresh) or frozen |
| Meat products | Specific to product type |

---

## Dairy — Raw Milk

### Raw Milk Collection Requirements
- Milking animals must not show signs of udder disease affecting milk
- Milk must be obtained hygienically
- Collected into clean containers, cooled immediately to ≤8°C (collection every day) or ≤6°C (not collected daily)
- Food-grade milk tankers only

### Pasteurisation and Heat Treatment
- Raw milk for liquid consumption must undergo minimum heat treatment
- Pasteurisation: typically 72°C for 15 seconds (HTST) or 63°C for 30 minutes (batch)
- UHT: ≥135°C for sufficient time to ensure commercial sterility
- All heat treatment must be validated and verified

### Temperature — Dairy Products
- Processed milk: ≤6°C after pasteurisation
- Hard cheeses: temperature depends on ripening requirements
- Soft cheeses: ≤4°C

---

## Fishery Products

### At Sea / Landing
- Fish must be kept chilled with ice or RSW (refrigerated sea water) immediately after catching
- RSW temperature: seawater ≤3°C
- Headed and gutted fish: ≤3°C; whole fish: approaching 0°C in ice/meltwater

### Processing
- Thawed fish not re-frozen unless incorporated into a product
- Parasites — visual inspection required; must be free from visible parasites
- Histamine: testing required for species susceptible (Scombridae, Clupeidae, Engraulidae, Coryphaenidae, Pomatomidae, Scombresocidae)

### Temperature Limits — Fishery Products

| Product | Temperature |
|---|---|
| Chilled fresh fishery products | Approaching 0°C (melting ice) |
| Cooked crustaceans and molluscs | ≤3°C if chilled; ≤-18°C if frozen |
| Frozen products | ≤-18°C (all parts) |

---

## Eggs and Egg Products

- Eggs must come from flocks inspected by competent authority
- Cracked eggs may only be used for egg products if collected promptly and hygienically
- Egg products: pasteurised; temperature of finished products maintained at ≤4°C (liquid) or frozen

---

## Live Bivalve Molluscs

- Must come from classified production areas (Class A, B, or C)
  - **Class A**: can be sold directly for human consumption
  - **Class B**: must undergo purification, relaying or heat treatment
  - **Class C**: must undergo extended relaying only
- Must carry health mark and batch identification
- Temperature during storage and transport: ≤6°C (refrigerated) or similar protective conditions

---

## HACCP Under 853/2004

All establishments must implement HACCP as per EC 852/2004. Species/product-specific hazards include:

| Product | Key Hazards |
|---|---|
| Red meat | E. coli O157:H7, Salmonella, Campylobacter, Listeria (post-process) |
| Poultry | Campylobacter, Salmonella |
| Dairy | Listeria monocytogenes, Salmonella, Brucella (raw milk) |
| Fishery | Histamine, Anisakis, Vibrio, Listeria (smoked) |
| Eggs | Salmonella Enteritidis |
| Bivalves | Norovirus, HAV, Vibrio, biotoxins |

---

## Documentation and Traceability

- Food chain information (FCI) must accompany animals to slaughter
- Batch identification must be maintained throughout production
- Records of temperatures, inspections, and test results retained per EC 178/2002 traceability requirements

---

## UK Retained Law Notes

Post-Brexit, GB has retained 853/2004 with amendments. Key differences:
- Health marking now uses UK country code (UK) rather than EU format
- Export of POAO to EU requires compliance with EU requirements and border checks
- Specific UK rules apply for Northern Ireland under the Windsor Framework
