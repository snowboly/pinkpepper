# Commission Regulation (EU) 2024/1756 — Contaminants Correction and Amendment

## Overview
Commission Regulation (EU) 2024/1756 of 25 June 2024 amends and corrects Regulation (EU) 2023/915 on maximum levels for certain contaminants in food. It addresses technical errors and omissions in the original text of EU 2023/915, updates specific maximum levels based on new EFSA opinions, and introduces or adjusts limits for several contaminants not adequately covered in the 2023 consolidation.

- **CELEX:** 32024R1756
- **Amends/corrects:** Regulation (EU) 2023/915
- **Published:** 25 June 2024
- **Applies from:** 15 July 2024 (with transitional periods for some categories)
- **Official source:** https://eur-lex.europa.eu/eli/reg/2024/1756/oj

---

## Why It Matters
Regulation (EU) 2023/915, which consolidated and replaced EC 1881/2006, contained a number of transposition errors and gaps identified following its entry into force. Additionally, EFSA published several contaminant opinions during 2023–2024 that required rapid regulatory follow-up. This regulation is the primary correction vehicle for those issues and represents the authoritative 2024 update to EU contaminant limits.

---

## Key Amendments and Corrections

### 1. Lead (Pb) — Updated Limits
Following EFSA's 2023 opinion on dietary exposure to lead, which found the former PTWI was no longer appropriate (no safe threshold identified), the Commission revised lead limits for certain categories:
- **Leafy vegetables** (spinach, lettuce, herbs): limits adjusted to reflect high consumer exposure modelling
- **Baby foods and infant formula**: stricter limits applied following re-evaluation of infant dietary patterns
- **Wild mushrooms**: limit corrected from an error in the 2023/915 text

### 2. Cadmium (Cd) — Corrections
- **Cocoa products**: corrected limits that were inadvertently transposed with incorrect values from the predecessor regulation 1881/2006
- **Oilseeds (sunflower, pumpkin)**: new maximum levels set following EFSA review; these were not covered in 2023/915

### 3. Nitrates — Expanded Scope
- **Baby foods containing spinach or similar nitrate-rich vegetables**: stricter limits aligned with infant dietary exposure modelling
- **Dried herbs and spices used in large quantities**: new limits introduced based on culinary use patterns identified in EFSA consumption data

### 4. Acrylamide — Clarifications
- Clarification that the limits in Annex I to EU 2023/915 apply to acrylamide in the **final product as sold**, not at intermediate processing stages
- Corrected table entries for cereal-based snacks and crisps that contained formatting errors in the 2023/915 text

### 5. Polycyclic Aromatic Hydrocarbons (PAH)
- **Smoked fish and smoked fishery products**: correction to bring limits in line with the text of the 2021 amendment (EU 2021/1323) which had been incorrectly consolidated
- **Dried herbs**: new limits for benzo[a]pyrene and PAH4 sum based on new occurrence data

### 6. Mycotoxins (Other than DON — see EU 2024/1022)
- **Ochratoxin A (OTA)**: corrected threshold for dried fruits (raisins, sultanas) that contained a typographic error
- **Fumonisins (B1+B2)**: updated limits for maize-based products; limit for popcorn corrected

### 7. Dioxins and PCBs
- Minor corrections to Annex entries for certain fish species; ensures consistency with Directive 2002/32/EC on undesirable substances in animal feed (where limits apply to feed ingredients derived from these species)

---

## Corrections to EU 2023/915 (Non-Substantive)
The following were purely textual corrections:
- Row numbering errors in several Annex I tables
- Footnote references that pointed to wrong footnote numbers
- Unit errors (mg/kg written where μg/kg was intended) for two minor entries
- Typographic errors in species names (Latin nomenclature corrected in two entries)

---

## Transitional Arrangements
Products already placed on the market before 15 July 2024 that comply with the pre-amendment limits of EU 2023/915 may remain in circulation until their normal date of minimum durability or use-by date. FBOs should not need to recall products already in distribution that were compliant under the pre-amendment text.

---

## Operator Implications

### Priority Actions
1. **Update internal specifications**: the corrected maximum levels now supersede EU 2023/915 — ensure purchasing specs, CoA (certificate of analysis) acceptance criteria, and internal lab method limits are updated
2. **Cross-check ongoing supplier approvals**: where specifications were set directly from EU 2023/915, verify the 2024/1756 corrections do not create a compliance gap
3. **Acrylamide monitoring plans**: note the clarification that limits apply to final product as sold — if interim process controls were calibrated to limits, this may change interpretation

### For Infant Food Manufacturers
The lead and cadmium corrections affecting baby foods are particularly significant. Review current raw material specifications and finished product testing frequencies.

---

## Reading EU 2023/915 Correctly Post-Amendment
When referring to EU 2023/915 for specific maximum levels, you **must** read it together with:
1. **EU 2024/1022** (DON limits — in force July 2024)
2. **EU 2024/1756** (corrections — in force July 2024)
3. Any further 2025 amendments (check EUR-Lex consolidated version: CELEX 32023R0915)

The consolidated version on EUR-Lex incorporates all amendments and is the authoritative reading text.
