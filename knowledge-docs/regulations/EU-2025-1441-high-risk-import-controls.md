# Commission Implementing Regulation (EU) 2025/1441 - High-Risk Import Controls

## Overview
Commission Implementing Regulation (EU) 2025/1441 of 18 July 2025 amends Implementing Regulation (EU) 2019/1793 on increased official controls and emergency measures for certain goods entering the Union from certain third countries.

- Jurisdiction: EU
- Source type: regulation
- Published: 23 July 2025
- CELEX: 32025R1441
- Official source: https://eur-lex.europa.eu/eli/reg_impl/2025/1441/oj

## Why it matters
- It changes the current EU control regime for certain imported food and feed from higher-risk origins.
- It is relevant for border checks, sampling frequencies, and emergency conditions on listed products.

## Practical retrieval points
- Use this source for questions about whether a product or origin is subject to enhanced import controls.
- It updates Annex-driven control requirements under Regulation (EU) 2019/1793 rather than replacing the framework entirely.
- It should be considered alongside Regulation (EC) No 178/2002 and Regulation (EU) 2017/625 when discussing import compliance.

## Compliance implications
- Importers should check whether products or countries of origin moved into, out of, or between the relevant annexes.
- Businesses relying on previous control frequencies should not assume the 2024 position still applies after July 2025.
- Border documentation, pre-notification, and testing expectations may differ after each amendment cycle.
