# Commission Regulation (EU) 2024/1022 — Deoxynivalenol (DON) Maximum Levels

## Overview
Commission Regulation (EU) 2024/1022 of 8 April 2024 amends Regulation (EU) 2023/915 as regards maximum levels for deoxynivalenol (DON) and its acetylated derivatives in cereals and cereal-based products. It implements EFSA's 2017 re-evaluation of DON toxicity, which established a significantly lower group tolerable daily intake (TDI) than the previous PMTDI.

- **CELEX:** 32024R1022
- **Amends:** Regulation (EU) 2023/915
- **Published:** 9 April 2024
- **Applies from:** 1 July 2024 (transitional arrangements until 31 December 2024 for products already in the supply chain)
- **Official source:** https://eur-lex.europa.eu/eli/reg/2024/1022/oj

---

## Why It Matters
Deoxynivalenol (DON, also known as vomitoxin) is a Fusarium mycotoxin produced on cereal crops — primarily wheat, barley, maize, and oats — under wet harvest conditions. It is among the most common mycotoxin contaminants in EU cereal supply chains. At dietary exposure levels, DON causes immunotoxicity, reduced weight gain, and gastrointestinal effects. Children are particularly vulnerable due to higher cereal consumption per body weight.

EFSA's 2017 opinion reduced the group TDI from 1 μg/kg bw/day to 0.8 μg/kg bw/day for DON and its acetylated forms (3-Ac-DON and 15-Ac-DON combined). Dietary exposure modelling showed that existing limits were insufficient to protect high consumers, especially children aged 3–10 years.

## New Maximum Levels for DON

### Unprocessed Cereals
| Commodity | Previous ML (μg/kg) | New ML (μg/kg) |
|---|---|---|
| Wheat (unprocessed, excl. durum) | 1,250 | 1,000 |
| Durum wheat (unprocessed) | 1,750 | 1,250 |
| Barley (unprocessed) | 1,250 | 1,000 |
| Maize (unprocessed) | 1,750 | 1,500 |
| Oats (unprocessed) | 1,750 | 1,250 |

### Milled Cereals and Flour
| Commodity | Previous ML (μg/kg) | New ML (μg/kg) |
|---|---|---|
| Wheat flour (milled cereal products) | 750 | 600 |
| Durum wheat semolina | 750 | 600 |
| Barley flour | 750 | 600 |
| Maize flour | 750 | 750 (unchanged) |
| Oat flakes / rolled oats | 1,000 | 750 |

### Foods for Infants and Young Children (most significant change)
| Commodity | Previous ML (μg/kg) | New ML (μg/kg) |
|---|---|---|
| Processed cereal-based foods for infants | 200 | 100 |
| Baby foods (cereal-containing) | 200 | 100 |
| Cereal-based foods for young children | 200 | 100 |
| Dietetic foods intended for medical purposes (cereal-based) | 200 | 100 |

### Bread, Pastries, Pasta, Breakfast Cereals
| Commodity | Previous ML (μg/kg) | New ML (μg/kg) |
|---|---|---|
| Bread, pastries, biscuits (wheat/barley based) | 500 | 400 |
| Dry pasta | 750 | 600 |
| Breakfast cereals (processed) | 750 | 500 |
| Breakfast cereals for children | 500 | 300 |

## Acetylated Forms (3-Ac-DON and 15-Ac-DON)
The regulation clarifies that maximum levels apply to the **sum of DON, 3-Ac-DON, and 15-Ac-DON** (expressed as DON equivalents), not to DON alone. Laboratories must use multi-mycotoxin methods capable of detecting acetylated derivatives.

---

## Operator Obligations

### For Grain Traders, Millers, and Cereal Processors
1. **Incoming goods testing**: Review existing mycotoxin testing programmes — if testing only for DON, ensure the method now covers acetylated forms
2. **Supplier contracts**: Update maximum residue level specifications to reflect the lower limits; suppliers in high-DON-risk regions (northern/central Europe in wet harvest years) may need additional safeguards
3. **Blending practices**: Blending of non-compliant with compliant grain to achieve compliance (dilution) is prohibited under EU law
4. **Segregation**: Identify compliant and non-compliant grain at intake; maintain traceability records

### For Manufacturers of Infant/Baby Foods
- Halving the DON limit from 200 to 100 μg/kg is the most operationally significant change
- Raw material selection must prioritise low-DON cereals; consider varietal selection and geographic sourcing
- Existing validated mycotoxin testing methods may need sensitivity upgrades to reliably detect at the lower limit

### For Importers
Products entering the EU for categories affected by the new limits must comply from 1 July 2024. Border sampling at POEs uses the new limits.

---

## Monitoring and Enforcement
- Member States must incorporate the updated limits into their national multi-annual control plans
- EFSA continues mycotoxin dietary exposure modelling; further amendments to other Fusarium mycotoxins (zearalenone, fumonisins) expected in subsequent regulations
- Sampling and analysis must follow Commission Regulation (EC) No 401/2006 (sampling methods for mycotoxins)

---

## Interaction with Other Regulations
- **EU 2023/915**: parent contaminants regulation — this amendment must be read alongside it
- **EC 401/2006**: sampling methods for mycotoxins in cereals
- **EU 2024/1756**: separate 2024 amendment that corrected other provisions in EU 2023/915
