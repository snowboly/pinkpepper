# Food Information (Amendment) (England) Regulations 2019 - Natasha's Law

## Overview
The Food Information (Amendment) (England) Regulations 2019 introduced the England-level legal change widely known as Natasha's Law. The change strengthens allergen labelling requirements for prepacked for direct sale (PPDS) food.

- **Official source:** https://www.legislation.gov.uk/uksi/2019/1218/contents/made
- **PDF:** https://www.legislation.gov.uk/uksi/2019/1218/pdfs/uksi_20191218_en.pdf

---

## Why It Matters
This regulation is central for questions about:
- PPDS food
- allergen labelling in England
- named allergen declarations
- distinction between PPDS and non-PPDS food

For PinkPepper, it should exist as a dedicated UK domestic source rather than only being inferred through wider EU food-information law.

---

## Core Practical Themes

### 1. PPDS Allergen Labelling
Where food is prepacked for direct sale, allergen information must be given on the package in line with the strengthened legal approach.

### 2. Product Classification Matters
Operators need to distinguish:
- PPDS
- non-prepacked food
- food prepacked on other premises

Misclassification can lead to the wrong compliance approach.

### 3. Operational Controls
This regulation links directly to recipe control, ingredient records, label generation, change management, and staff training.
